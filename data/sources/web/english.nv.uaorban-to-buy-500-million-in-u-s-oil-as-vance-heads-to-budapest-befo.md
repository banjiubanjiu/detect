# Orbán to buy $500 million in U.S. oil as Vance heads to Budapest before Hungary vote

April 7, 2026, 01:14 PM

Hungary will sign a deal to buy U.S. oil during Vice President JD Vance’s visit to Budapest on April 7, just days before the country’s parliamentary election, [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-07/hungary-to-agree-to-buy-oil-from-us-at-orban-vance-meeting) reported on April 7, citing sources.

The deal will be announced during a joint news conference with Prime Minister Viktor Orbán, as Vance moves to boost the Hungarian leader ahead of the vote.

Trump has also repeatedly voiced support for Orbán.

According to Bloomberg’s sources, Hungarian energy company Mol Nyrt. will agree to buy 500,000 tons of U.S. oil worth about $500 million. The company has also increased its purchases of Russian oil after securing sanctions waivers from both the United States and the European Union.

“Orbán, who doubled down on Russian energy after Moscow’s invasion of Ukraine in 2022, has also sought to build ties with the Trump administration through energy deals,” Bloomberg wrote. “At a White House meeting in November, Orbán agreed to buy 400 million cubic meters of liquefied natural gas over five years and sign a $114 million contract with Westinghouse Electric Co. to purchase nuclear fuel for Hungary’s nuclear power plant.”

In return, the Trump administration allowed Hungary to keep buying Russian oil.

The two sides also signed a memorandum of understanding for Hungary to buy as many as 10 small modular reactors under a deal that could be worth as much as $20 billion.

Before leaving for Hungary, Vance told reporters he was “looking forward to meeting my good friend,” Prime Minister Viktor Orbán.

On March 25, Trump declared his “full support” for Orbán and urged Hungarians to vote for him in the parliamentary election.

On April 1, Reuters, citing data from two polls, reported that the opposition Tisza party had widened its lead over Orbán’s ruling Fidesz party ahead of the vote.
