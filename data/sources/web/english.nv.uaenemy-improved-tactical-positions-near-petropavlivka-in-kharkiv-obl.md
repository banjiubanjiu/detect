# Russian forces push forward in Kharkiv and Donetsk oblasts

Russian forces have advanced in Ukraine’s Kharkiv and Donetsk oblasts (Photo: Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty/Serhii Nuzhnenko via REUTERS)

Russian forces have advanced in Ukraine’s Kharkiv and Donetsk oblasts, the DeepState monitoring group [reported](https://t.me/DeepStateUA/23382) on Telegram late on April 8.

According to updated frontline maps, the enemy has improved its tactical positions near Petropavlivka in Kharkiv Oblast and Stupochky in Donetsk Oblast.

![Image 7: DeepState map](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/MediaPhoto/images/000/583/062/original/2ff9f38ebf884f4a6e08ff9e5d25812b.png?q=85&stamp=20260409082031&f=webp)

 Photo: DeepState map 

![Image 8: DeepState map](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/MediaPhoto/images/000/583/063/original/68f37282d6fab07b8c5d36a4776edb24.png?q=85&stamp=20260409082031&f=webp)

 Photo: DeepState map 

On April 8, Pavlo Palisa, deputy head of the Ukrainian presidential office, said that [Russian forces plan to create a “buffer zone” extending into Ukraine’s Vinnytsia Oblast](https://english.nv.ua/nation/russia-plots-deeper-incursion-targeting-buffer-zone-beyond-frontlines-50598577.html) from Transdniestria, an unrecognized separatist enclave in Moldova.
