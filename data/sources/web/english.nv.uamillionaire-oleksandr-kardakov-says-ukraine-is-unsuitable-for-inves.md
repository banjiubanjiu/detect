# Ukrainian IT millionaire calls country 'unfit for investment'

April 6, 2026, 06:10 PM

![Oleksandr Kardakov, founder of Octava Capital and IT millionaire (Photo: NV/Oleksandr Medvedev)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/269/845/original/0d54c8a391bd791d4f8d8fa3660409f9.png?q=85&stamp=20260406170130&w=900&f=webp)

Oleksandr Kardakov, founder of Octava Capital and IT millionaire (Photo: NV/Oleksandr Medvedev)

One of the main obstacles for Ukraine’s MilTech sector is the chronic shortage of working capital and the state’s inability to purchase all domestically produced equipment, Ukrainian IT millionaire and Octava Capital founder Oleksandr Kardakov told NV in an [interview](https://biz.nv.ua/ukr/bizinterview/opk-ukrajini-kardakov-pro-galmuvannya-eksportu-zbroji-chomu-kitay-maye-dostup-do-ukrajinskih-kamer-50597507.html) on April 6.

Speaking about the staffing situation, the founder of Octava Capital stated: “I always manage to find specialists in any field.” He acknowledged that there are staffing issues, but that they can be resolved.

“They exist, but they can be solved. The people we need are coming,” said the entrepreneur.

“The funny thing is that it’s hard to find designers who prepare factory documentation. Creative people don’t want to come here. Most people are used to working with a drafting board and drawing paper. Few people work with new tools and are willing to do monotonous work. That’s the biggest problem. And those designers—specifically mechanical designers—who can develop something new need to be backed by a design bureau that documents everything for production and technology. It’s very hard to find people who will do that—the ones who’ll handle the more tedious work.”

Speaking about MilTech’s working capital shortage, Kardakov noted: “Technically, this is a complicated matter. Who’s going to give money for something that someone is selling to someone else—and we don’t even know who that is?”

He acknowledged that, in effect, this is currently “an equation with three unknowns.”

“Banks are playing it safe right now,” Kardakov stated.

“We recently had a case where a bank returned the money to the buyer because it didn’t like the payment. And this wasn’t the first payment. They simply didn’t like our contract, which has been in effect for a long time. The next payment sent to us was returned to the buyers. We’re going to switch banks.”

Speaking about investments in Ukraine, he noted: “There won’t be any investments in Ukraine for a long time. The country is unsuitable for investment.”

When asked what, aside from the war, makes Ukraine unsuitable for investment, he noted: “Everything. In none of the recent scandals has anyone been punished. I’m even too lazy to talk about it. I don’t even discuss it with myself anymore. Some of the money that has come into the country recently has been lost. In particular, when the Trypilska Thermal Power Plant was restored, funds passed through shell companies.”

Kadakov also noted that in 2025, Ukrainian defense startups raised over $105 million, which is “a pittance.”

“Do we currently have any weapons technologies that are unknown in the West? When it comes to drones, we’ve practically lost our lead,” Kardakov said.

“As for interceptors, there won’t be any leadership there either. Take Quantum-Systems (Germany), for example. Good for them! They developed a reconnaissance drone, then bought Ukrainian companies, and are now quietly doing everything there. Technologies spread very quickly. They don’t stay secret for long. There’s practically nothing left to protect today. The companies around the world that are involved in this have access to everything. These $105 million are piecemeal purchases of what they want to acquire to accelerate their projects.”
