April 7, 2026 Updated 8:57 a.m. ET

After 16 years in power, Prime Minister Viktor Orban of Hungary is struggling to maintain his footing as his governing Fidesz party trails badly in most polls. Now, Vice President JD Vance is attempting a last-ditch rescue effort to revive the flagging prospects of Europe’s nationalist standard-bearer.

Declaring that Hungary under Mr. Orban’s leadership and the United States under President Trump are bound by “moral cooperation” in the “defense of Western civilization,” Mr. Vance told a news conference in Budapest, the Hungarian capital:“Viktor Orban is, of course, going to win.”

Mr. Orban, whose Fidesz party is trailing badly in the polls, interjected approvingly: “That is a fact.”

Mr. Vance arrived in Budapest on Tuesday morning, just five days before Sunday’s election, widely seen as the most consequential in Hungary since the collapse of communism in 1989.

He heaped praise on Mr. Orban as “wise and smart” and abuse on European Union “bureaucrats” who he said “tried to destroy the Hungarian economy” to sway Sunday’s vote “because they hate this guy.”

At a time of war in the Middle East and a global energy crisis, Mr.Vance’s trip underscored just how important an election in a country with fewer than 10 million people and a tiny economy is to powerful nations far beyond its borders.
