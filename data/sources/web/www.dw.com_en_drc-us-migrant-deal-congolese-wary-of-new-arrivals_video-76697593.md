Congo's government says [migrants](https://www.dw.com/en/migration/t-41577210) deported from the United States under a "third‑country" arrangement could arrive in the capital of the [Democratic Republic of the Congo](https://www.dw.com/en/democratic-republic-of-congo-drc/t-38232616), Kinshasa, this month. Authorities insist the move involves temporary stays, not permanent resettlement, and that the US will finance and manage all costs and logistics. However, officials have not disclosed how many deportees would be sent, their nationalities or the duration of their stay. Civil society groups and critics warn the agreement lacks transparency and democratic oversight, calling for public debate and parliamentary approval. They argue the plan risks worsening Congo's existing humanitarian, social, and security challenges amid widespread uncertainty and public concern.
