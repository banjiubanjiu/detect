## Israel threatens entire Iran train network
 **The Israeli military has just warned the people of Iran not to use trains, saying that doing so “endangers your life”**.
The military’s Farsi-language [channel on X](https://x.com/IDFFarsi/status/2041385577287663833) issued what it called (translated here) an “urgent warning to users and train passengers in the country of Iran”:
> double quotation markDear Citizens, for the sake of your security, we kindly request that from this moment until 21:00 Iran time, you refrain from using and travelling by train throughout Iran.
> Your presence on trains and near railway lines endangers your life.

## IDF says it struck key petrochemical compound in Iran
 **Israeli Defence Forces said on Tuesday that Israeli forces struck a key petrochemical compound in Shiraz in southern Iran.** The Guardian was not able to independently verify this claim.
According to the IDF, this facility[ was one of the last remaining facilities](https://x.com/IDF/status/2041413222469603581?s=20) that produced critical chemical components for explosives and materials for ballistic missiles.
The IDF said it also struck a large ballistic missile array site in northwestern [Iran](https://www.theguardian.com/world/iran). The Guardian also was not able to independently verify this claim.

[2h ago02.47 EDT](https://www.theguardian.com/?page=with%3Ablock-69d4a71a8f080da25b48c4a7#block-69d4a71a8f080da25b48c4a7)
 **Nearly 3,600 people have been killed in the US-Israeli strikes on Iran since attacks began, including at least 1,665 civilians** , the Human Rights Activists news agency (HRANA) [said](https://www.en-hrana.org/day-38-of-u-s-and-israeli-attacks-on-iran-highest-rate-of-strikes-in-the-past-ten-days/).
Of those numbers, at least 248 of those killed were children, the US-based NGO said.
At least 49 civilians were killed and 58 others were injured on Monday, according to HRANA, which recorded 573 attacks across 215 incidents in 20 provinces over that 24-hour period – the highest rate of attacks seen in the last ten days.

Earlier, Saudi air defences intercepted and destroyed seven ballistic missiles targeting the eastern region of the country, with debris falling in the area of some energy facilities, the defence ministry [said](https://x.com/modgovksa/status/2041287030705959341?s=20). Crews are still assessing damages.
It is unclear who is responsible for either the drones or the missiles.

## Interim summary
Here’s a recap of the latest news from the [US-Israel war on Iran](https://www.theguardian.com/world/us-israel-war-on-iran) to bring you up to speed. It’s 9.30am in Tehran, 9am in Tel Aviv and Beirut and 2am in Washington DC.
  * **Donald Trump said he was “not at all” concerned about committing possible war crimes as he again threatened to destroy Iran’s bridges and power plants** if Tehran did not meet his deadline of Tuesday 8pm ET (midnight Tuesday GMT) to reopen the strait of Hormuz. “You know what’s a war crime? Having a nuclear weapon,” he said.
  * **Iran rejected a 45-day ceasefire proposal** and said it wanted a permanent end to the conflict.
  * **The Israeli military warned the people of Iran via X on Tuesday morning not to use trains and be near railway lines** “from this moment until 21:00 Iran time”, saying that doing so “endangers your life”.
  * **The UN security council is expected to vote on Tuesday on a resolution to protect commercial shipping in the strait of Hormuz but in significantly watered-down form** after veto-wielding China opposed authorising force, Reuters cited diplomats as saying.
