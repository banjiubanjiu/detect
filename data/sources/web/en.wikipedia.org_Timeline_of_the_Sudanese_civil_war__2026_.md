The following is a **timeline of the [Sudanese civil war (2023–present)](/wiki/Sudanese_civil_war_(2023%E2%80%93present) "Sudanese civil war (2023–present)") in 2026**.

This timeline is a dynamic and fluid list, and as such may never satisfy criteria of completeness. Moreover, some events may only be fully understood and/or discovered in retrospect.

January
-------

### 1 January

Hamid Ali Abubakar, the commander-in-chief of the [RSF](/wiki/Rapid_Support_Forces "Rapid Support Forces")-aligned faction of the [Sudanese Awakening Revolutionary Council](/wiki/Sudanese_Awakening_Revolutionary_Council "Sudanese Awakening Revolutionary Council") (RAC) and concurrent security adviser to [Hemedti](/wiki/Hemedti "Hemedti"), was killed in a drone strike on a meeting of RSF and allied commanders in Damrat al-Firdous, [Central Darfur](/wiki/Central_Darfur "Central Darfur").[[1]](#cite_note-1)

### 3 January

The RSF carried out drone strikes on [El-Obeid](/wiki/El-Obeid "El-Obeid") and [White Nile State](/wiki/White_Nile_State "White Nile State").[[2]](#cite_note-2) An airstrike against a power station in El-Obeid left the city without electricity.[[3]](#cite_note-3)[[4]](#cite_note-4)

Up to 64 civilians were killed in [SAF](/wiki/Sudanese_Armed_Forces "Sudanese Armed Forces") airstrikes on Hemedti's hometown of [Al Zorg](/wiki/Al_Zorg "Al Zorg") that destroyed the town's hospital, and also killed two of Hemedti's relatives and local tribal leader Bashir Barma Baraka Allah.[[5]](#cite_note-5)

The RSF and allied groups claimed to have taken El Bardab, north of [Kadugli](/wiki/Kadugli "Kadugli").[[6]](#cite_note-6)

### 4 January

The [Sudan Liberation Movement-Abdel Wahid](/wiki/Sudan_Liberation_Movement/Army "Sudan Liberation Movement/Army") (SLM-AW), a neutral rebel group, ordered the replacement of Sudanese state symbols with its own in the [Liberated Areas](/wiki/Liberated_Areas_(Sudan) "Liberated Areas (Sudan)") in Central and [North Darfur](/wiki/North_Darfur "North Darfur").[[7]](#cite_note-7)

### 5 January

The SAF claimed to have intercepted a drone attack by the RSF on [Merowe](/wiki/Merowe,_Sudan "Merowe, Sudan").[[8]](#cite_note-8)

Thirteen people, including eight children, were killed in an RSF drone strike on a house in El-Obeid, according to the Sudan Doctors' Network.[[9]](#cite_note-9)

### 7 January

One soldier was killed in a suspected RSF drone strike on a [Sudan Shield Forces](/wiki/Sudan_Shield_Forces "Sudan Shield Forces") camp in the [Butana](/wiki/Butana "Butana") plains in [Gezira State](/wiki/Gezira_State "Gezira State").[[10]](#cite_note-10)

### 9 January

The RSF and its allies, the [Sudan Liberation Forces Gathering](/wiki/Sudan_Liberation_Movement/Army#Sudan_Liberation_Forces_Alliance "Sudan Liberation Movement/Army") and the [Justice and Equality Movement](/wiki/Justice_and_Equality_Movement "Justice and Equality Movement"), attacked positions of the [Darfur Joint Protection Force](/wiki/Darfur_Joint_Protection_Force "Darfur Joint Protection Force") (JDF) at the [Chad–Sudan border](/wiki/Chad%E2%80%93Sudan_border "Chad–Sudan border").[[11]](#cite_note-11)

The SAF carried out airstrikes on RSF positions in [Darfur](/wiki/Darfur "Darfur") and [Kordofan](/wiki/Kordofan "Kordofan"). The SAF claimed 240 military vehicles were wrecked and hundreds of RSF members were killed. The SAF also claimed a Turkish [Baykar Bayraktar Akıncı](/wiki/Baykar_Bayraktar_Ak%C4%B1nc%C4%B1 "Baykar Bayraktar Akıncı") drone operated by the RSF was shot down over [Nyala Airport](/wiki/Nyala_Airport "Nyala Airport"). In [South Kordofan](/wiki/South_Kordofan "South Kordofan"), the [SPLM-N (al-Hilu)](/wiki/Sudan_People%27s_Liberation_Movement%E2%80%93North "Sudan People's Liberation Movement–North") said 13 civilians, including children, were killed in an airstrike on Um Jamina in Al-Sunut County.[[12]](#cite_note-12)

### 10 January

The JDF accused the RSF of killing and kidnapping 19 people in Jirjira, North Darfur.[[13]](#cite_note-13)

### 11 January

The SAF-aligned government announced its complete return to [Khartoum](/wiki/Khartoum "Khartoum"), having operated in [Port Sudan](/wiki/Port_Sudan "Port Sudan") as the temporary capital since 2023.[[14]](#cite_note-14)

The SAF conducted strikes against a supply convoy in [Yabus](/wiki/Yabus "Yabus"), [Blue Nile State](/wiki/Blue_Nile_State "Blue Nile State"), near the [Ethiopia–Sudan border](/wiki/Ethiopia%E2%80%93Sudan_border "Ethiopia–Sudan border").[[15]](#cite_note-15)

Five people were killed and 20 injured in an RSF drone strike on a market in Kartala, [South Kordofan](/wiki/South_Kordofan "South Kordofan").[[16]](#cite_note-16)

### 12 January

At least 27 people were killed and 73 others were wounded by an RSF drone strike on an army base in [Singa](/wiki/Singa,_Sudan "Singa, Sudan"). The attack reportedly targeted a meeting between government and SAF officials at the base. The governors of [White Nile](/wiki/White_Nile_State "White Nile State"), [Sennar](/wiki/Sennar_State "Sennar State"), and Blue Nile States were confirmed to have been in attendance, but survived the attack.[[17]](#cite_note-17)[[18]](#cite_note-18)

### 13 January

The SAF and allied groups claimed to have retaken Jirjira from the RSF.[[19]](#cite_note-19)

### 14 January

The SAF repelled an attack by the RSF and SPLM-N (al-Hilu) near Al-Kuwaik, close to Kadugli.[[20]](#cite_note-20)

The RSF was accused of abducting 97 young men from a gold mine in Eyal Bakhit, [West Kordofan](/wiki/West_Kordofan "West Kordofan").[[21]](#cite_note-21)

### 15 January

The RSF carried out a cross-border attack into Chad while pursuing a JDF unit that fled into the country, killing seven Chadian soldiers at the Birak garrison near [Tiné Djagaraba](/wiki/Tin%C3%A9_Djagaraba "Tiné Djagaraba").[[22]](#cite_note-22)[[23]](#cite_note-23)

Twelve people were killed in an RSF drone strike on a market in [Dalang, South Kordofan](/wiki/Dalang,_South_Kordofan "Dalang, South Kordofan").[[24]](#cite_note-24)

The SPLM-N (al-Hilu) and the RSF claimed to have repelled an SAF attack on [Habila](/wiki/Habila "Habila"), 30 km (19 mi) from Dalang.[[25]](#cite_note-25)

### 18 January

The SAF repelled a large scale RSF attack on Wadi al-Hout in [North Kordofan](/wiki/North_Kordofan "North Kordofan").[[26]](#cite_note-26)

### 20 January

The [Central Bank of Sudan](/wiki/Central_Bank_of_Sudan "Central Bank of Sudan") announced the resumption of its operations in Khartoum for the first time since the civil war began.[[27]](#cite_note-27)

### 21 January

The RSF detained 17 [Zaghawas](/wiki/Zaghawa_people "Zaghawa people") from Mahajeriya in [East Darfur](/wiki/East_Darfur "East Darfur"), including the head of the rural court.[[28]](#cite_note-28)

### 23 January

Five people were killed in an SAF drone strike on a market in Abu Zaima, North Kordofan.[[29]](#cite_note-zaima-29)

### 24 January

Two people were killed in an RSF drone strike on Dalang.[[30]](#cite_note-30) The RSF claimed to have shot down an SAF [Bayraktar](/wiki/Baykar_Bayraktar_family "Baykar Bayraktar family") drone over the city.[[29]](#cite_note-zaima-29)

### 25 January

The SAF repelled a large-scale attack by the RSF and SPLM-N (al-Hilu) from South Sudan on Milkan and Al-Silak in [Blue Nile State](/wiki/Blue_Nile_State "Blue Nile State").[[31]](#cite_note-31)

### 26 January

The SAF retook Habila and broke the [siege](/wiki/Battle_of_Dilling "Battle of Dilling") of [Dalang](/wiki/Dalang,_South_Kordofan "Dalang, South Kordofan").[[32]](#cite_note-32)

### 27 January

Five people were killed in a drone strike on Dalang.[[33]](#cite_note-33)

### 28 January

Dozens of people were killed in an RSF drone strike on Dalang.[[34]](#cite_note-34)

### 30 January

The European Union imposed sanctions on seven individuals for their role in escalating the conflict in Sudan, including Hemedti's brother Major Algoney Hamdan Dagalo Musa, and Al-Misbah Abu Zaid Talha, commander of the SAF-allied [Al-Bara' ibn Malik Battalion](/wiki/Al-Bara%27_ibn_Malik_Battalion "Al-Bara' ibn Malik Battalion").[[35]](#cite_note-35)

The RSF carried out a drone attack on El-Obeid.[[36]](#cite_note-36)

February
--------

### 1 February

A joint SAF-JDF force retook Al-Dashol, on the highway between Dalang and Kadugli.[[37]](#cite_note-37)

At least five people, including a senior RSF commander and a tribal leader, were killed in clashes with rival RSF factions in Khazan Jadid, East Darfur.[[38]](#cite_note-38)

### 3 February

The SAF said it had broken the RSF siege of Kadugli.[[39]](#cite_note-39) Later that day, RSF drone strikes on the city killed 15 people.[[40]](#cite_note-40)

The RSF and SPLM-N (al-Hilu) seized the towns of [Deim Mansour](/wiki/Deim_Mansour "Deim Mansour"), Bashir Nuqu and Khor al-Budi near [Kurmuk](/wiki/Kurmuk "Kurmuk") in Blue Nile state.[[41]](#cite_note-41)

### 4 February

One person was killed in an RSF drone strike on a military hospital in Kadugli.[[42]](#cite_note-42)

### 5 February

The United Kingdom imposed sanctions on six individuals over their role in sustaining the conflict, including three Colombians accused of recruiting mercenaries for the RSF, RSF field commander [Hussein Barsham](/wiki/Hussein_Barsham "Hussein Barsham"), and [Sudan Shield Forces](/wiki/Sudan_Shield_Forces "Sudan Shield Forces") commander [Abu Aqla Kikal](/wiki/Abu_Aqla_Kikal "Abu Aqla Kikal").[[43]](#cite_note-43)

Twenty-two people were killed in an RSF drone strike on a military hospital in Kouik, South Kordofan.[[44]](#cite_note-44) An SAF drone strike on a market in West Kordofan injured 11 people.[[45]](#cite_note-45)

### 6 February

At least one person was killed in a series of RSF drone strikes on truck convoys in North Kordofan.[[46]](#cite_note-46)

### 7 February

A RSF drone attack on a vehicle carrying displaced families near [Er Rahad](/wiki/Er_Rahad "Er Rahad") killed at least 24 people including eight children.[[47]](#cite_note-47)

The SAF repelled an RSF attack on Al-Silak.[[48]](#cite_note-48)

### 8 February

Three people were killed in an RSF drone strike on a vehicle carrying food supplies near [Umm Ruwaba](/wiki/Umm_Ruwaba "Umm Ruwaba") in North Kordofan.[[49]](#cite_note-49)

### 10 February

The SAF said it destroyed a [FK-2000](/wiki/FK-2000 "FK-2000") air defense system and drones used by the RSF in [Ed Dubeibat](/wiki/Ed_Dubeibat "Ed Dubeibat").[[50]](#cite_note-50)

### 11 February

Two people were killed in an RSF drone strike on a mosque in Er Rahad.[[51]](#cite_note-51)

### 13 February

Two people were killed in RSF drone strikes on al-Kurgal, South Kordofan.[[52]](#cite_note-52)

The SAF shelled the RSF and SPLM-N (Toka)-held towns of Malkon and [Yabus](/wiki/Yabus "Yabus"), near the Ethiopian border in Blue Nile state.[[53]](#cite_note-53)

### 14 February

An unspecified number of civilian casualties occurred following drone strikes by the RSF and SPLM-N (Toka) in [Qaysan](/wiki/Qays%C4%81n,_Abdullah_Qeissan "Qaysān, Abdullah Qeissan"), Blue Nile state.[[54]](#cite_note-54)

### 15 February

Three people were killed in an RSF drone strike on Al-Mazmoum Hospital in [Sennar State](/wiki/Sennar_State "Sennar State").[[55]](#cite_note-55)

At least 28 people were killed in a drone strike on a market outside Sodari in North Kordofan.[[56]](#cite_note-56)

The SAF claimed to have destroyed an RSF air defence system in [Abu Zabad](/wiki/Abu_Zabad "Abu Zabad"), West Kordofan.[[57]](#cite_note-57)

### 16 February

At least 26 people were killed in a suspected SAF drone strike on a displacement shelter in El Sunut, West Kordofan.[[58]](#cite_note-58)

### 18 February

The [World Food Programme](/wiki/World_Food_Programme "World Food Programme") delivered humanitarian aid to Dalang and Kadugli for the first time since 2024.[[59]](#cite_note-59)

One person was killed in an RSF drone strike on Kurmuk.[[60]](#cite_note-60)

### 19 February

The United States imposed sanctions on RSF commanders [Elfateh Abdullah Idris Adam](/wiki/Abu_Lulu "Abu Lulu"), [Gedo Hamdan Ahmed](/wiki/Gedo_Hamdan_Ahmed "Gedo Hamdan Ahmed") and [Tijani Ibrahim Moussa Mohamed](/wiki/Tijani_Ibrahim_Moussa_Mohamed "Tijani Ibrahim Moussa Mohamed") for their [actions at El Fasher](/wiki/El_Fasher_massacre "El Fasher massacre"). The three were added to the [Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List](/wiki/Specially_Designated_Nationals_and_Blocked_Persons_List "Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List").[[61]](#cite_note-61)

Three aid workers were killed in an RSF drone attack on a humanitarian aid convoy in Katala as it was travelling to Dalang and Kadugli.[[62]](#cite_note-62)

### 21 February

The RSF claimed to have taken El Tina, along the border with Chad and North Darfur. The JDF denied the claim.[[63]](#cite_note-63)

### 22 February

RAC leader [Musa Hilal](/wiki/Musa_Hilal "Musa Hilal"), who was in conflict with the RSF, survived an RSF drone attack on his guest house in Mustariha, North Darfur. A separate attack on the town killed several RAC leaders and injured Hilal's son.[[64]](#cite_note-64)

### 23 February

The RSF attacked Mustariha, leaving 28 civilians dead.[[65]](#cite_note-65)

Chad imposed an indefinite closure of [its border](/wiki/Chad%E2%80%93Sudan_border "Chad–Sudan border") with Sudan after RSF fighters entered the country while fleeing from SAF forces in El Tina.[[66]](#cite_note-66)

### 24 February

The [United Nations Security Council](/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council "United Nations Security Council") imposed sanctions on four RSF commanders, including Hemedti's brother [Abdul Rahim Dagalo](/wiki/Abdul_Rahim_Dagalo "Abdul Rahim Dagalo"), Gedo Hamdan Ahmed, [Elfateh Abdullah Idris Adam](/wiki/Abu_Lulu "Abu Lulu") and Tijani Ibrahim Moussa Mohamed, for atrocities committed in El Fasher.[[67]](#cite_note-67)

### 25 February

The RAC announced Musa Hilal's arrival in SAF-controlled territory outside Darfur following the RSF attack on Mustariha.[[68]](#cite_note-68)

### 26 February

The RSF and SPLM-N carried out drone strikes on [Geissan](/wiki/Geissan_District "Geissan District") in Blue Nile state.[[69]](#cite_note-69)

### 28 February

Nine people were killed in RSF drone strikes on El-Obeid.[[70]](#cite_note-70)

March
-----

### 1 March

The RSF and SPLM-N claimed to have taken the SAF garrison at al-Taqma, 7 km (4.3 mi) east of Dalang, and cut the road linking Dalang and [Habila](/wiki/Habila "Habila").[[71]](#cite_note-71)

### 2 March

Twelve people were injured in an RSF drone strike on the British Hospital in El-Obeid.[[72]](#cite_note-72)

### 4 March

Five people were killed by RSF and SPLM-N (al-Hilu) shelling in Dalang.[[73]](#cite_note-73)

Eighteen people were killed in an SAF airstrike on [Muglad](/wiki/Muglad "Muglad").[[74]](#cite_note-africanews-74)

### 5 March

The SAF claimed to have retaken [Bara](/wiki/Barah,_Sudan "Barah, Sudan") in North Kordofan from the RSF.[[74]](#cite_note-africanews-74) Shortly afterwards, the RSF looted and burned the nearby village of Umm Kuraidim,[[75]](#cite_note-75) killing seven people.[[76]](#cite_note-76)

The RSF claimed to have taken El Bardab, north of Kadugli.[[77]](#cite_note-77)

Twenty-eight people were killed by RSF shelling in Dalang.[[74]](#cite_note-africanews-74)

### 7 March

At least 34 people were killed in SAF drone strikes on [Abu Zabad](/wiki/Abu_Zabad "Abu Zabad").[[78]](#cite_note-78)

### 8 March

Six people were killed in an SAF drone strike on a fuel market in [Ed Daein](/wiki/Ed_Daein "Ed Daein"),[[79]](#cite_note-79) while 10 others were killed in a similar attack in [Nyala](/wiki/Nyala,_Sudan "Nyala, Sudan").[[80]](#cite_note-80) The SPLM-N (al-Hilu) said 17 people were killed in a separate SAF attack on a market in Jald, South Kordofan.[[81]](#cite_note-81)

### 9 March

The United States included the [Islamic Movement in Sudan](/wiki/Islamism_in_Sudan "Islamism in Sudan") and its armed wing, the [Al-Bara' ibn Malik Battalion](/wiki/Al-Bara%27_ibn_Malik_Battalion "Al-Bara' ibn Malik Battalion"), to its list of Specially Designated Global Terrorists.[[82]](#cite_note-82)

### 10 March

At least 52 people were killed in a suspected SAF drone strike on a civilian convoy near [Al-Fulah](/wiki/Al-Fulah "Al-Fulah"), West Kordofan.[[83]](#cite_note-83)

Seven people were killed by RSF and SPLM-N (al-Hilu) shelling in Dalang.[[84]](#cite_note-84)

### 11 March

At least 17 people were killed in an RSF drone strike on a school in [Shukeiri](/w/index.php?title=Shukeiri&action=edit&redlink=1 "Shukeiri (page does not exist)"), White Nile State.[[85]](#cite_note-85)

At least 20 people were killed in an SAF drone strike on Abu Zabad.[[86]](#cite_note-86)

### 12 March

At least 11 people were killed drone strike on Adikong market in West Darfur, near the Chadian town of [Adré](/wiki/Adr%C3%A9 "Adré"). [Médecins Sans Frontières](/wiki/M%C3%A9decins_Sans_Fronti%C3%A8res "Médecins Sans Frontières") attributed the attack to the SAF.[[87]](#cite_note-87)[[88]](#cite_note-88)

The SAF claimed to have retaken Jort East and the Ballamo camp in Blue Nile state from the RSF and SPLM-N (Touka).[[89]](#cite_note-89)

### 13 March

Thirteen RSF militants, including a major general, were killed in an SAF drone strike on their convoy in [Foro Baranga](/wiki/Foro_Baranga "Foro Baranga"), West Darfur.[[90]](#cite_note-90)

### 15 March

Enagi Abdullah, a commander of the Islamist and pro-SAF paramilitary group Popular Resistance, was arrested after making remarks in support of Iran amid the [2026 Iran war](/wiki/2026_Iran_war "2026 Iran war") that had been disavowed by the SAF-led government.[[91]](#cite_note-91)

### 16 March

The RSF retook Bara as well as Karnoi in North Darfur, near the border with Chad.[[92]](#cite_note-92)

The RSF claimed to have taken El Tina, which the JDF denied, adding that it had killed an RSF field commander during the attack.[[93]](#cite_note-93)[[94]](#cite_note-94) At least 17 people were reported killed in the fighting.[[95]](#cite_note-95)

### 17 March

The RSF burned a truck convoy transporting staple crops near [En Nahud](/wiki/En_Nahud "En Nahud") on its way to El-Obeid.[[96]](#cite_note-96)

### 18 March

The RSF was accused of killing 12 people in Shuraim Mima, north of Bara.[[97]](#cite_note-97)

A drone that originated from Sudan hit the Chadian border town of [Tina](/wiki/Tina_(Sudan) "Tina (Sudan)"), killing 17 people.[[98]](#cite_note-98)

### 19 March

The [Chadian National Army](/wiki/Chadian_National_Army "Chadian National Army") carried out a security operation in Tine following the drone attack the previous day, resulting in the seizure of weapons and the arrest of several Sudanese refugees accused being JDF members.[[99]](#cite_note-99)[[100]](#cite_note-100) Chad also closed its border with Sudan.[[101]](#cite_note-101)

### 20 March

Seventy people were killed and 146 injured in an SAF airstrike on the [Ed Daein](/wiki/Ed_Daein "Ed Daein") Hospital. The attack left over two million people without proper medical coverage according to the [World Health Organization](/wiki/World_Health_Organization "World Health Organization").[[102]](#cite_note-102)[[103]](#cite_note-103)

An RSF drone strike on [Al Dabbah](/wiki/Al_Dabbah,_Sudan "Al Dabbah, Sudan") injured three people and hit a power station, causing blackouts.[[104]](#cite_note-104)

### 24 March

The RSF and SPLM-N (al-Hilu) took [Kurmuk](/wiki/Kurmuk "Kurmuk"), near the Ethiopian border in [Blue Nile State](/wiki/Blue_Nile_State "Blue Nile State"). The offensive displaced over 73,000 people.[[105]](#cite_note-105)[[106]](#cite_note-106)

### 25 March

The RSF massacred 16 civilians in El Fasher.[[107]](#cite_note-107)

A drone strike in RSF-controlled [Saraf Omra](/wiki/Saraf_Omra "Saraf Omra"), [North Darfur](/wiki/North_Darfur "North Darfur") killed 22 people and injured 17. Six people were killed and 10 injured in a suspected RSF strike on a civilian truck in [North Kordofan](/wiki/North_Kordofan "North Kordofan").[[108]](#cite_note-108)

### 27 March

SAF drones struck RSF positions amassed near Tina in [North Darfur](/wiki/North_Darfur "North Darfur").[[109]](#cite_note-109)

### 28 March

The SAF said it repelled an advance by the RSF and SPLM-N (al-Hilu) into the western and northern outskirts of [Dalang](/wiki/Dilling,_South_Kordofan "Dilling, South Kordofan").[[110]](#cite_note-110)

### 29 March

The SAF claimed to have repelled an RSF and SPLM-N attack on Al-Kaili in [Blue Nile State](/wiki/Blue_Nile_State "Blue Nile State"), killing 94 fighters, capturing several others and destroying four combat vehicles.[[111]](#cite_note-111)

### 30 March

A pediatrician in Nyala was killed in an assault on his home by an RSF officer who was accompanied by several personnel.[[112]](#cite_note-112)
