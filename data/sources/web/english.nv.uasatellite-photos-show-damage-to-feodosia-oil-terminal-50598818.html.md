# Satellite imagery shows damage to Feodosia oil terminal

April 8, 2026, 07:09 PM

![Image 6: REUTERS/Stringer](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/271/424/original/f7481cbd2d05c216190c059c98e3e7d9.png?q=85&stamp=20260408215843&w=900&f=webp)

REUTERS/Stringer

A large fire burned at the [Feodosia Sea Oil Terminal](https://english.nv.ua/nation/ukraine-hits-oil-terminal-in-occupied-feodosia-50598676.html), the primary Russian oil depot in occupied Crimea, after a Ukrainian strike left at least two storage tanks ablaze, RFE/RL reported on April 8.

RFE/RL published Planet Labs satellite images showing smoke plumes and active fires at the terminal. Journalists who reviewed images from preceding days concluded that at least two tanks, survivors of earlier strikes by Ukrainian forces in October 2025, were burning. Satellite data indicated the blaze was still active as of 12 p.m. April 8.

Earlier the same day, Ukraine’s General Staff confirmed that Crimean oil depots in Feodosia and Hvardiiske were targeted. The latter supplies fuel to a nearby Russian airbase.
