# Taiwan opposition leader makes rare visit to China

Asia / Pacific  

Taiwanese opposition leader Cheng Li-wun arrived in China on Tuesday for a six-day visit aimed at building cross-strait "peace", as the government warns Beijing will seek to stop US arms sales to the democratic island.

Kuomintang (KMT) chairwoman Cheng Li-wun, pictured here on November 1, 2025, will become the party's first leader to visit China in a decade.© I-Hwa Cheng, AFP/File

[Taiwan](https://www.france24.com/en/asia-pacific/20260328-what-taiwan-imprisonment-opposition-figure-ko-wen-je-means-political-landscape)'s main opposition leader landed in China on Tuesday for a rare visit aimed at building cross-strait "peace", as the government warns Beijing will seek to stop US arms sales to the democratic island.

The KMT supports closer relations with [China](https://www.france24.com/en/tag/china/), which claims Taiwan is part of its territory and has threatened to use force to seize it.

But Cheng, whose unexpected rise to the top of the KMT drew a congratulatory message from Xi in October, has been accused by critics, including inside the party, of being too pro-China.
