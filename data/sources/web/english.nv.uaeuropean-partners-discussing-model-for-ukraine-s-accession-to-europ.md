# Ukraine’s EU accession model could emerge in 2027, deputy PM says

April 8, 2026, 04:24 PM

![Image 6: Taras Kachka commented on the prospects for Ukraine's rapid accession to the EU (Photo: NV)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/271/327/original/5d8407abfc74521a6f5c549cdb9500d4.png?q=85&stamp=20260408181123&w=900&f=webp)

Taras Kachka commented on the prospects for Ukraine's rapid accession to the EU (Photo: NV)

A model for Ukraine’s accession to the European Union could emerge in 2027 amid a very active discussion among European partners, Deputy Prime Minister for European and Euro-Atlantic Integration Taras Kachka said during a meeting with journalists on April 8.

“Right now, the political process in the EU is unfolding as follows: no one is waiting for the formal opening of the clusters (the negotiation clusters on EU accession, the opening of which was previously blocked by Hungary—ed.), everyone is waiting for us to make progress on meeting the benchmarks, and this is actually the decisive factor in determining how quickly we can join the European Union,” Kachka stated.

This discussion has begun very actively even though there is currently no clear model for Ukraine’s EU accession, he added.

“This is not a problem. On the contrary, it is a super success because the active discussion is underway. Member states are reacting very actively to it,” the official said.

He also noted that the Nordic-Baltic Eight (NB8)—a regional cooperation framework comprising five Nordic countries (Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, and Sweden) and three Baltic countries (Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania)—holds a progressive stance on this issue. In addition, active work is underway in Germany.

“We had a Ukrainian-Dutch conference, and this was also discussed during our talks with Germany. In fact, the outlook is very positive,” Kachka stated.

“I think they’ll have a clearer picture by May. And they’re also looking toward 2027, when the issue of EU accession will be resolved. There is a lot of speculation about whether it will be this way or that way. It hasn’t been finalized yet. But what is certain is that no one has ruled out the idea that by 2027 we will have some model for EU accession. The question now is: which one?”

He noted that conditions are not yet in place for all benchmarks to be fulfilled for discussing accession terms, but there is understanding in the EU that some response is needed.

“When will this happen? From what we can see, May is the month when certain approaches are being developed, and the Council of the EU is also actively working on this,” the minister added. “So I think that by the first half of the year, we’ll have a clear picture of what’s going on and how it will unfold.”

On March 17, Ukrainian Prime Minister Yulia Svyrydenko announced that the Ukrainian delegation in Brussels had received the European Union’s conditions for accession regarding the final three negotiation clusters.

The next steps are the successful closure of the clusters and the signing of the accession treaty, which will be the final step toward Ukraine’s full membership in the EU.

On March 19, 25 EU leaders (excluding Hungary and Slovakia) adopted a statement calling for the immediate opening of negotiation clusters regarding Ukraine’s EU membership.

Earlier, President Volodymyr Zelenskyy stated that Ukraine expects to join the European Union in 2027.

For his part, German Chancellor Friedrich Merz noted that any country wishing to join the EU must first meet the Copenhagen criteria, and this process typically takes several years.

He emphasized that Ukraine needs a vision that will pave the way for accession in the long term. According to Merz, Ukraine’s accession to the EU as of Jan. 1, 2027 is unrealistic.
