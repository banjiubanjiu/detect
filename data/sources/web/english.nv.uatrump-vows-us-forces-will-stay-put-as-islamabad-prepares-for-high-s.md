American troops will remain near Iran until a final peace agreement is reached, and if there is no deal, the war will resume with renewed vigor, U.S. President Donald Trump wrote on Truth Social on April 8.

All American ships, planes, weapons, and military personnel will be "in their places" until a "real deal" is reached, he claimed.

"If for any reason this [peace deal — Ed.] does not happen, which is highly unlikely, then the shooting will start — bigger, better, and stronger than anyone has ever seen."

He added that Iran must not have any nuclear weapons, and the Strait of Hormuz "will be open and safe."

"Meanwhile, our great military is preparing and resting, eagerly anticipating, actually, its next conquest," the U.S. president added.

Negotiations between the U.S. and Iran are scheduled to take place on April 11 in Islamabad, Pakistan, CNN wrote.

Trump stated on the night of April 8 that he had agreed to a two-week ceasefire with Iran on the condition of the immediate opening of Hormuz Strait. The U.S. has already "achieved and exceeded all military goals" and has also "made significant progress" in reaching a final peace agreement, he pointed out.

Trump later declared a "final" U.S. victory in the war against Iran. Iran's Supreme National Security Council, in turn, also declared "victory" amid the ceasefire, stating that "the enemy [was driven] to a state of historic helplessness and final defeat."

Iran claimed its oil refinery on Lavan Island was shelled on April 8, calling it a violation of the truce. Tehran later accused the United States of a new violation of the ceasefire agreement due to massive Israeli attacks on Lebanon. The White House stated that the agreement does not cover Lebanon.

Iran also attacked Kuwait, the UAE, and Bahrain with missiles and drones on April 8.
