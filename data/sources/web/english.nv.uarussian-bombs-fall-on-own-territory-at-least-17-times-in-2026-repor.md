# Russian aerial bombs fall on own territory at least 17 times in 2026

April 7, 2026, 01:56 PM

![Image 6: Site of the suspected fall of a Russian aerial bomb in Belgorod, Russia, on Jan. 22, 2026 (Photo: Nastoyashchiy Gladkov / Telegram)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/270/574/original/7998eb2d4f7c0e585b7e9d956da59998.png?q=85&stamp=20260407165527&w=900&f=webp)

Site of the suspected fall of a Russian aerial bomb in Belgorod, Russia, on Jan. 22, 2026 (Photo: Nastoyashchiy Gladkov / Telegram)

At least 17 Russian aerial bombs have accidentally fallen on Russian regions and Russian-occupied areas of Ukraine in 2026, [the Russian Telegram channel Astra reported on April 7](https://t.me/astrapress/109176), citing data compiled from its sources.

A FAB-500 bomb fell unintentionally near the village of Strelnikov in Belgorod Oblast on March 7, bringing the total number of such incidents this year to at least 17, according to the report.

Russia recorded at least 143 similar cases in 2025 and at least 165 in 2024, the outlet said.

Russian authorities have attempted to conceal such incidents and have blamed Ukrainian attacks when civilians are affected by falling bombs, Astra reported.

A pro-war Telegram channel close to Russia’s Aerospace Forces,Fighterbomber, acknowledged in January 2026 that Russian aerial bombs regularly fall within the city of Belgorod.

A bomb accidentally dropped by Russian aviation near a power station in Belgorod caused a blackout in the city and nearby areas on Dec. 6, 2025, the Telegram channel Pepel Belgorod reported.

A crater believed to have been caused by a munition was discovered on Gubkin Street in Belgorod on Jan. 21, 2026.

Authorities did not specify the origin of the munition, but local sources suggested it was an aerial bomb likely belonging to Russia, Astra reported.
