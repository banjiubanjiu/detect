# Ukraine proposes using Black Sea expertise to support shipping in Strait of Hormuz

April 8, 2026, 01:39 PM

Cargo ship in the Persian Gulf, near the Strait of Hormuz, March 11, 2026 (Photo: REUTERS/Stringer)

Ukraine is ready to share its Black Sea experience to help ensure freedom of navigation in the Strait of Hormuz, Foreign Ministry spokesman Heorhii Tykhyi said at a briefing on Apr. 8.

He stressed that Ukraine has participated at both political and military levels in meetings on ensuring freedom of navigation in the Strait of Hormuz under the leadership of the United Kingdom.

According to him, Foreign Minister Andrii Sybiha took part in political-level meetings, while Ukrainian military representatives joined discussions at the technical and military levels.

“This proposal remains on the table, it remains valid … If Ukraine’s services are needed by our allies, we are ready to provide them,” Tykhyi said.

Later, Iran’s Supreme National Security Council confirmed the two-week ceasefire agreement with the United States. Israel also agreed to suspend its bombing during the talks.
