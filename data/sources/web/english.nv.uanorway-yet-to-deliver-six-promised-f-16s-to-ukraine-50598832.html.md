# Norway yet to deliver six promised F-16s to Ukraine

April 8, 2026, 10:19 PM

![F-16 aircraft (Photo: REUTERS/Jana Rodenbusch)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/271/456/original/05d3f98b95c5154f4242db657903da94.png?q=85&stamp=20260409001130&w=900&f=webp)

F-16 aircraft (Photo: REUTERS/Jana Rodenbusch)

Six F-16 fighter jets [Norway](https://english.nv.ua/business/norway-awards-funding-for-650-mw-poltava-wind-farm-50596554.html) pledged to Ukraine have not yet been delivered, a Western military official working with Ukraine’s Air Force told Norwegian public broadcaster [NRK](https://www.nrk.no/norge/norske-f-16-til-ukraina-har-enna-ikke-kommet-fram-1.17837044#fmin_bekrefter) on April 8.

“These six jets would have saved lives this winter and better protected infrastructure,” the unnamed

“We could have shot down more Russian missiles and drones.”

Prime Minister Jonas Gahr Støre announced during a visit to Ukraine in August 2023 that Norway would transfer six F-16s. But several NRK sources said two of the jets have been in repair in Belgium for more than a year. The other four were unable to fly and were shipped in pieces to the Belgian company Sabena in April 2025.

NRK reported that the repair work on the four jets sent to Belgium is extensive and would take about a year if it started now. The broadcaster cited capacity problems at Sabena for F-16 maintenance and training as a key reason the Norwegian jets have not reached Ukraine.

Norway’s Defense Minister, Tore Sandvik, confirmed the aircraft remain under repair in Belgium. He said Norway had moved its best F-16s to Romania.

“Thirty-two jets that were in the best condition, as well as workshop equipment and spare parts, were later sold to Romania to support an allied country and strengthen NATO in southern Europe,” Sandvik said.

Sandvik added that the jets donated to Ukraine were known to need significant repairs. That contrasted, he said, with Denmark and the Netherlands, which continued operating F-16s in 2023 and could have handed over aircraft directly from active service.
