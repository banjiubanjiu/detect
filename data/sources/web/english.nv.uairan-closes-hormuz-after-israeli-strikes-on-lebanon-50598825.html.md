# Iran closes Strait of Hormuz after Israeli strikes on Lebanon

April 8, 2026, 07:34 PM

![Image 6: REUTERS/Stringer](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/271/439/original/07c06bb66429f11161cb7ebb40ebc69f.png?q=85&stamp=20260408231504&w=900&f=webp)

REUTERS/Stringer

[Iranian](https://english.nv.ua/nation/ukraine-welcomes-ceasefire-agreement-between-u-s-and-iran-50598596.html) authorities said they again closed the Strait of Hormuz to shipping in response to a new series of Israeli strikes on Hezbollah assets in Lebanon, the Associated Press [reported](https://apnews.com/live/iran-war-israel-trump-04-08-2026#0000019d-6e30-d1f7-a9bf-6efead9f0000) on April 8.

The AP said Israel struck several commercial and residential areas in Beirut. At least 112 people were killed and hundreds were wounded in what the AP described as "one of the bloodiest days" in the latest war between Israel and Hezbollah.

"The fresh violence threatened to scuttle what U.S. Vice President JD Vance called a 'fragile' deal," the report added.

White House spokeswoman Karoline Leavitt told reporters the ceasefire announced by U.S. President Donald Trump on April 7 is contingent on Hormuz remaining open to civilian shipping without any additional tolls Iran said it plans to collect.
