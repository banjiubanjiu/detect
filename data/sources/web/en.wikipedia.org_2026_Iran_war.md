On 28 February 2026, the [United States](/wiki/United_States "United States") and [Israel](/wiki/Israel "Israel") started a war with surprise airstrikes on sites and cities across [Iran](/wiki/Iran "Iran"), [assassinating](/wiki/Assassination_of_Ali_Khamenei "Assassination of Ali Khamenei") Supreme Leader [Ali Khamenei](/wiki/Ali_Khamenei "Ali Khamenei") and [several other Iranian officials](/wiki/List_of_Iranian_officials_killed_during_the_2026_Iran_war "List of Iranian officials killed during the 2026 Iran war") and inflicting [dozens of civilian casualties](#Civilians). Iran responded with missile and drone strikes against Israel, [US bases](/wiki/List_of_American_military_installations#Foreign_bases_and_facilities "List of American military installations"), and US-allied countries in the [Middle East](/wiki/Middle_East "Middle East"), and by closing the [Strait of Hormuz](/wiki/Strait_of_Hormuz "Strait of Hormuz").

After the [Middle Eastern crisis](/wiki/Middle_Eastern_crisis_(2023%E2%80%93present) "Middle Eastern crisis (2023–present)") began in 2023, Iran and Israel exchanged [missile strikes in 2024](/wiki/2024_Iran%E2%80%93Israel_conflict "2024 Iran–Israel conflict"), and Israel and the [US launched airstrikes](/wiki/2025_United_States_strikes_on_Iranian_nuclear_sites "2025 United States strikes on Iranian nuclear sites") against Iran in the [Twelve-Day War](/wiki/Twelve-Day_War "Twelve-Day War") in June 2025. In January 2026, Iranian security forces [killed thousands of protesters](/wiki/2026_Iran_massacres "2026 Iran massacres") in their crackdown of the [largest Iranian protests](/wiki/2025%E2%80%932026_Iranian_protests "2025–2026 Iranian protests") since the [1979 Islamic Revolution](/wiki/1979_Islamic_Revolution "1979 Islamic Revolution"). US president [Donald Trump](/wiki/Donald_Trump "Donald Trump") responded by threatening military action against Iran and launching the [largest US military buildup in the region](/wiki/2026_United_States_military_buildup_in_the_Middle_East "2026 United States military buildup in the Middle East") since the US-led [2003 invasion of Iraq](/wiki/2003_invasion_of_Iraq "2003 invasion of Iraq"). In mid-February, the US and Iran began a new round of [indirect nuclear negotiations](/wiki/2025%E2%80%932026_Iran%E2%80%93United_States_negotiations "2025–2026 Iran–United States negotiations").[[92]](#cite_note-110)

The surprise US-Israeli attack, launched during the nuclear negotiations, killed Khamenei, other Iranian officials, and civilians.[[93]](#cite_note-111)[[94]](#cite_note-112) Subsequent attacks damaged military bases, government facilities, [schools](/wiki/2026_Minab_school_attack "2026 Minab school attack"), hospitals, and cultural heritage sites.[[95]](#cite_note-113) In retaliation, Iran [launched](/wiki/2026_Iranian_strikes_on_Israel "2026 Iranian strikes on Israel") hundreds of [drones](/wiki/Unmanned_aerial_vehicles_in_the_Iranian_military "Unmanned aerial vehicles in the Iranian military") and [ballistic missiles](/wiki/Ballistic_missile "Ballistic missile") at targets in Israel and [at US military bases](/wiki/2026_Iranian_strikes_on_Arab_countries "2026 Iranian strikes on Arab countries") in [Bahrain](/wiki/2026_Iranian_strikes_on_Bahrain "2026 Iranian strikes on Bahrain"), [Jordan](/wiki/2026_Iranian_strikes_on_Jordan "2026 Iranian strikes on Jordan"), [Kuwait](/wiki/2026_Iranian_strikes_on_Kuwait "2026 Iranian strikes on Kuwait"), [Qatar](/wiki/2026_Iranian_strikes_on_Qatar "2026 Iranian strikes on Qatar"), [Saudi Arabia](/wiki/2026_Iranian_strikes_on_Saudi_Arabia "2026 Iranian strikes on Saudi Arabia"), and the [United Arab Emirates](/wiki/2026_Iranian_strikes_on_the_United_Arab_Emirates "2026 Iranian strikes on the United Arab Emirates").[[96]](#cite_note-114)[[97]](#cite_note-115) [A drone struck](/wiki/2026_drone_strikes_on_Akrotiri_and_Dhekelia "2026 drone strikes on Akrotiri and Dhekelia") Britain's [Akrotiri](/wiki/Akrotiri_and_Dhekelia "Akrotiri and Dhekelia") military base on [Cyprus](/wiki/Cyprus_(island) "Cyprus (island)"), and missiles were [shot down](/wiki/2026_interceptions_of_Iranian_missiles_in_Turkey "2026 interceptions of Iranian missiles in Turkey") over [Turkey](/wiki/Turkey "Turkey"). Other strikes hit civilian infrastructure including in [Azerbaijan](/wiki/2026_alleged_Iranian_strikes_on_Azerbaijan "2026 alleged Iranian strikes on Azerbaijan"),[[98]](#cite_note-116) [Kurdistan](/wiki/2026_Iranian_strikes_on_the_Kurdistan_Region "2026 Iranian strikes on the Kurdistan Region"), and [Oman](/wiki/2026_Iranian_strikes_on_Oman "2026 Iranian strikes on Oman").[[99]](#cite_note-117) Iran denied striking Azerbaijan, Oman, and Turkey, saying they were [false-flag](/wiki/False_flag "False flag") attacks.[[100]](#cite_note-118)[[101]](#cite_note-Khamenei-119) Two ballistic missiles were reportedly launched at [Diego Garcia](/wiki/Diego_Garcia "Diego Garcia") military base on the [Chagos Islands](/wiki/Chagos_Islands "Chagos Islands"),[[102]](#cite_note-120)[[103]](#cite_note-bbc21march-121) which Iran also denied.[[104]](#cite_note-Falseflag-122) The [conflict between Hezbollah and Israel](/wiki/Hezbollah%E2%80%93Israel_conflict_(2023%E2%80%93present) "Hezbollah–Israel conflict (2023–present)") escalated into the [2026 Lebanon war](/wiki/2026_Lebanon_war "2026 Lebanon war"), killing more than 1,000 militants and civilians by late March.[[105]](#cite_note-123)[[106]](#cite_note-124)[[107]](#cite_note-125)[[108]](#cite_note-126)

[Trump administration](/wiki/Second_presidency_of_Donald_Trump "Second presidency of Donald Trump") officials have offered diverse and changing [explanations for starting the war](/wiki/Rationale_for_the_2026_Iran_war "Rationale for the 2026 Iran war"),[[109]](#cite_note-127) such as to pre-empt Iranian retaliation against US assets after an expected Israeli attack on Iran,[[110]](#cite_note-128)[[111]](#cite_note-129)[[112]](#cite_note-130)[[113]](#cite_note-131) to ward off an imminent Iranian threat, to destroy [Iran's missile](/wiki/Ballistic_missile_program_of_Iran "Ballistic missile program of Iran") and [military capabilities](/wiki/Military_of_Iran "Military of Iran"), to prevent Iran obtaining a [nuclear weapon](/wiki/Nuclear_weapon "Nuclear weapon"),[[114]](#cite_note-fullStatement-132) to secure Iran's [oil resources](/wiki/Oil_resources "Oil resources"),[[115]](#cite_note-133)[[116]](#cite_note-134) and to [achieve regime change](/wiki/Regime_change_efforts_in_the_2026_Iran_war "Regime change efforts in the 2026 Iran war") by bringing the [Iranian opposition](/wiki/Iranian_opposition "Iranian opposition") to power.[[117]](#cite_note-135)[[118]](#cite_note-136)[[119]](#cite_note-137)[[120]](#cite_note-138) Iranian[[121]](#cite_note-Scahill-2026-139) and some US officials rejected claims that Iran had been preparing an attack.[[122]](#cite_note-140) The [International Atomic Energy Agency](/wiki/International_Atomic_Energy_Agency "International Atomic Energy Agency") said that while Iran has an "ambitious" nuclear program and has refused to allow inspections of its damaged sites since the 2025 war, there was no evidence of a structured nuclear weapons program when the 2026 war began.[[123]](#cite_note-141) [UN](/wiki/UN "UN") secretary-general [Antonio Guterres](/wiki/Antonio_Guterres "Antonio Guterres") and several uninvolved countries condemned the US–Israeli strikes; the [United Nations Security Council](/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council "United Nations Security Council") later passed [a resolution](/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_2817 "United Nations Security Council Resolution 2817") condemning Iran's retaliatory strikes on the Gulf states.[[124]](#cite_note-142) Critics of the war, including legal and international relations experts, have described the attacks as illegal under US law, an act of [imperialism](/wiki/American_imperialism "American imperialism") and a violation of Iran's [sovereignty](/wiki/Sovereignty "Sovereignty")[[125]](#cite_note-143)[[126]](#cite_note-144) under [international law](/wiki/International_law "International law").[[127]](#cite_note-guardian2march-145)

[The war's economic impact](/wiki/Economic_impact_of_the_2026_Iran_war "Economic impact of the 2026 Iran war"), described as the world's largest supply disruption since the [1970s energy crisis](/wiki/1970s_energy_crisis "1970s energy crisis"),[[128]](#cite_note-146) includes surges in [oil](/wiki/Price_of_oil "Price of oil") and [gas prices](/wiki/Natural_gas_prices "Natural gas prices"), wide [disruptions in aviation](/wiki/Economic_impact_of_the_2026_Iran_war#Aviation "Economic impact of the 2026 Iran war") and tourism, and [volatility in financial markets](/wiki/Economic_impact_of_the_2026_Iran_war#Financial_markets "Economic impact of the 2026 Iran war"). Oil and gas shipments were disrupted when Iran [closed the Strait of Hormuz](/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis "2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis") and Israel and Iran attacked energy facilities.[[129]](#cite_note-147)[[130]](#cite_note-uf-148)[[131]](#cite_note-149) On 19 March, the cost of the war to the US military was estimated at US$18 billion,[[132]](#cite_note-guardianCost-150) and [the Pentagon](/wiki/The_Pentagon "The Pentagon") requested an additional US$200 billion.[[133]](#cite_note-i598-151)

Background

Main articles: [Rationale for the 2026 Iran war](/wiki/Rationale_for_the_2026_Iran_war "Rationale for the 2026 Iran war") and [Regime change efforts in the 2026 Iran war](/wiki/Regime_change_efforts_in_the_2026_Iran_war "Regime change efforts in the 2026 Iran war")

### Iran's relations with the United States and Israel

Further information: [Iran–United States relations](/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93United_States_relations "Iran–United States relations") and [Iran–Israel relations](/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Israel_relations "Iran–Israel relations")

A US- and UK-backed [coup d'état in 1953](/wiki/1953_Iranian_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat "1953 Iranian coup d'état") deposed the democratically elected Iranian prime minister [Mohammad Mosaddegh](/wiki/Mohammad_Mosaddegh "Mohammad Mosaddegh") due to his [nationalization of the oil industry](/wiki/Nationalization_of_the_Iranian_oil_industry "Nationalization of the Iranian oil industry") and strengthened the rule of the [Shah](/wiki/Shah "Shah"), [Mohammad Reza Pahlavi](/wiki/Mohammad_Reza_Pahlavi "Mohammad Reza Pahlavi").[[134]](#cite_note-News1828-152)[[135]](#cite_note-153)[[136]](#cite_note-154) Israel maintained close ties with Iran as part of their [alliance of the periphery](/wiki/Alliance_of_the_periphery "Alliance of the periphery") strategy.[[137]](#cite_note-rishikasingh-155) Resentment of the Shah's deference to Western interests and his autocratic rule led to the [1979 revolution](/wiki/1979_revolution "1979 revolution") in which he was overthrown,[[134]](#cite_note-News1828-152)[[138]](#cite_note-Hourcade-2026-156) after which Iran became an [Islamic republic](/wiki/Islamic_republic "Islamic republic"). The new regime, seeing both the United States and Israel (themselves close allies) as meddling in the region, and viewing Israel as an occupier of Palestinian land, severed diplomatic ties with both nations and held the [American embassy staff hostage](/wiki/Iran_hostage_crisis "Iran hostage crisis") for over a year.[[134]](#cite_note-News1828-152)[[137]](#cite_note-rishikasingh-155)

During the [Iran–Iraq War](/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Iraq_War "Iran–Iraq War") in the 1980s, the [US provided economic, intelligence, and indirect military support to Iraq](/wiki/United_States_support_for_Iraq_during_the_Iran%E2%80%93Iraq_War "United States support for Iraq during the Iran–Iraq War"). Toward the end of the war in 1988, a US warship was struck by an Iranian mine; in response, the US [attacked Iran's navy](/wiki/Operation_Praying_Mantis "Operation Praying Mantis"). A few months later, the US mistakenly shot down a [civilian Iranian flight](/wiki/Iran_Air_Flight_655 "Iran Air Flight 655").[[139]](#cite_note-MacFarquhar1926-157) Throughout this period, Iran started a ballistic missile program to deter Iraqi [missile attacks on Iranian cities](/wiki/War_of_the_Cities "War of the Cities") and to compensate for its lack of modern air force due to sanctions.[[140]](#cite_note-158) Iran began to engage in [proxy conflicts](/wiki/Proxy_conflicts "Proxy conflicts") in the region, backing [Hezbollah](/wiki/Hezbollah "Hezbollah") in Lebanon. In 2002, Iran's nuclear program was revealed, which was met with [economic sanctions](/wiki/International_sanctions_against_Iran "International sanctions against Iran") and a [US–Israel cyberattack](/wiki/Operation_Olympic_Games "Operation Olympic Games") on Iran.[[139]](#cite_note-MacFarquhar1926-157) In the 2000s, Iran began supporting militias fighting the US in Iraq. These proxies became part of an informal [Axis of Resistance](/wiki/Axis_of_Resistance "Axis of Resistance") committed primarily to countering the influence of the US and Israel in the Middle East. The conflict between the US and Iran became direct in January 2020 when President Trump, during his first term, ordered the [assassination of Qasem Soleimani](/wiki/Assassination_of_Qasem_Soleimani "Assassination of Qasem Soleimani"), commander of the [Quds Force](/wiki/Quds_Force "Quds Force").[[134]](#cite_note-News1828-152)[[141]](#cite_note-159)

Tensions between Iran and the US and Israel further escalated following the [October 7 attacks](/wiki/October_7_attacks "October 7 attacks") on Israel and the start of the [Gaza war](/wiki/Gaza_war "Gaza war") in 2023, during which Israel weakened Iranian-backed militias across the Middle East, including [Hamas](/wiki/Hamas "Hamas") in Gaza,[[s]](#cite_note-162) Hezbollah, and others. [Israeli strikes on the Iranian consulate in Damascus](/wiki/Israeli_airstrike_on_the_Iranian_consulate_in_Damascus "Israeli airstrike on the Iranian consulate in Damascus") and the [assassination of Ismail Haniyeh](/wiki/Assassination_of_Ismail_Haniyeh "Assassination of Ismail Haniyeh") in Iran were met with strikes on Israel in [April](/wiki/April_2024_Iranian_strikes_on_Israel "April 2024 Iranian strikes on Israel") and [October](/wiki/October_2024_Iranian_strikes_on_Israel "October 2024 Iranian strikes on Israel"). Israel waged the [Twelve-Day War](/wiki/Twelve-Day_War "Twelve-Day War") on Iran in June 2025, which included [an American airstrike](/wiki/United_States_strikes_on_Iranian_nuclear_sites "United States strikes on Iranian nuclear sites") aimed at destroying Iran's nuclear facilities.[[144]](#cite_note-163)

### Iran nuclear issue

Further information: [Iran and weapons of mass destruction § Nuclear weapons](/wiki/Iran_and_weapons_of_mass_destruction#Nuclear_weapons "Iran and weapons of mass destruction")

See also: [Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons § Iran](/wiki/Treaty_on_the_Non-Proliferation_of_Nuclear_Weapons#Iran "Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons")

Iran's [AMAD Project](/wiki/AMAD_Project "AMAD Project") was suspended pursuant to [Ali Khamenei's fatwa against nuclear weapons](/wiki/Ali_Khamenei%27s_fatwa_against_nuclear_weapons "Ali Khamenei's fatwa against nuclear weapons") in 2003. Khamenei, the [supreme leader of Iran](/wiki/Supreme_leader_of_Iran "Supreme leader of Iran") at the time and one of the world's most senior Shiite religious leaders, issued his religious ruling banning the development of nuclear weapons of any kind.[[145]](#cite_note-164)[[146]](#cite_note-165) Iran has said it is not seeking nuclear weapons, and that its enrichment efforts are to generate [nuclear power](/wiki/Nuclear_power "Nuclear power") for civilian use.[[147]](#cite_note-166) Some UK and US analysts concluded Iran is pursuing a strategy of [nuclear hedging](/wiki/Nuclear_hedging "Nuclear hedging").[[148]](#cite_note-167) [UN Security Council](/wiki/UN_Security_Council "UN Security Council") (UNSC) concerns about the [nuclear program of Iran](/wiki/Nuclear_program_of_Iran "Nuclear program of Iran") from 2006[[t]](#cite_note-171) led to the creation of the [Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action](/wiki/Joint_Comprehensive_Plan_of_Action "Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action") (JCPOA) in 2015.[[152]](#cite_note-172) The [US withdrew from the JCPOA](/wiki/United_States_withdrawal_from_the_JCPOA "United States withdrawal from the JCPOA") in 2018[[153]](#cite_note-173)[[138]](#cite_note-Hourcade-2026-156) and re-imposed [sanctions on Iran](/wiki/United_States_sanctions_against_Iran "United States sanctions against Iran"), as part of a US turn toward the use of force rather than diplomacy in its relations with Iran. The [first Trump administration](/wiki/First_Trump_administration "First Trump administration") adopted a "[maximum pressure](/wiki/Maximum_pressure_campaign "Maximum pressure campaign")" strategy.[[154]](#cite_note-174) The [Biden administration](/wiki/Biden_administration "Biden administration") did not relax the economic sanctions against Iran, and instead implemented more measures.[[155]](#cite_note-175)

The [second Trump administration](/wiki/Second_Trump_administration "Second Trump administration") reimposed the "maximum pressure" approach on Iran.[[156]](#cite_note-176)[[138]](#cite_note-Hourcade-2026-156) [Secretary of the Treasury](/wiki/Secretary_of_the_Treasury "Secretary of the Treasury") [Scott Bessent](/wiki/Scott_Bessent "Scott Bessent") labelled the collapse of the Iranian currency in December 2025 as the "grand culmination" of this strategy.[[157]](#cite_note-177)[[158]](#cite_note-178) At the [State of the Union Address](/wiki/2026_State_of_the_Union_Address "2026 State of the Union Address") on 24 February 2026, President Trump stated that Iran had restarted its nuclear program and was developing missiles capable of striking the US.[[159]](#cite_note-179) A few days later, the [International Atomic Energy Agency](/wiki/International_Atomic_Energy_Agency "International Atomic Energy Agency") (IAEA) reported that Iran had stored [highly enriched uranium](/wiki/Highly_enriched_uranium "Highly enriched uranium") in an underground facility that was undamaged in the previous round of fighting.[[160]](#cite_note-180)[[161]](#cite_note-181) The IAEA said that it had no evidence that Iran has an organized nuclear weapons program or is building an atomic bomb,[[162]](#cite_note-182)[[163]](#cite_note-183)[[164]](#cite_note-184) but could not be sure that Iran's broader nuclear program was "exclusively peaceful" at the time of reportage as the agency was denied access.[[165]](#cite_note-185)

### Israel nuclear issue

Further information: [Israel and nuclear weapons](/wiki/Israel_and_nuclear_weapons "Israel and nuclear weapons") and [Middle East nuclear weapon free zone](/wiki/Middle_East_nuclear_weapon_free_zone "Middle East nuclear weapon free zone")

Israel is [widely believed to possess nuclear weapons](/wiki/Israel_and_nuclear_weapons "Israel and nuclear weapons"), with a stockpile estimated between 90 and 400 nuclear warheads.[[177]](#cite_note-197) Israel is the only [nuclear-armed country](/wiki/List_of_states_with_nuclear_weapons "List of states with nuclear weapons") that [does not officially acknowledge](/wiki/Israel_and_nuclear_weapons#Policy "Israel and nuclear weapons") its nuclear weapons capability.[[178]](#cite_note-Kristensen-2022-198) Some scholars have argued that Israel's nuclear weapons have incentivized Iran to develop its own nuclear program, seen as maintaining a balance of [nuclear deterrence](/wiki/Deterrence_theory "Deterrence theory"), or causing a regional [nuclear arms race](/wiki/Nuclear_arms_race "Nuclear arms race").[[179]](#cite_note-199)[[180]](#cite_note-200)[[181]](#cite_note-201)[[182]](#cite_note-202) Citing security threats, Israel rejects international calls to accede to the [Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons](/wiki/Treaty_on_the_Non-Proliferation_of_Nuclear_Weapons "Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons") or to participate in negotiations of a [Middle East nuclear weapon free zone](/wiki/Middle_East_nuclear_weapon_free_zone "Middle East nuclear weapon free zone").[[183]](#cite_note-203)[[184]](#cite_note-204)[[185]](#cite_note-s316-205) This has frustrated both US[[178]](#cite_note-Kristensen-2022-198) and UK[[186]](#cite_note-206) diplomatic efforts on such a zone. Scholars also suggest US tacit acceptance of the Israeli nuclear weapons capability is seen as a double standard that undermines its diplomatic credibility in Middle East [nonproliferation](/wiki/Nonproliferation "Nonproliferation") negotiations.[[187]](#cite_note-207)[[178]](#cite_note-Kristensen-2022-198)

Prelude

Main article: [Prelude to the 2026 Iran war](/wiki/Prelude_to_the_2026_Iran_war "Prelude to the 2026 Iran war")

### Anti-government protests in Iran and initial US deployments

Further information: [2025–2026 Iranian protests](/wiki/2025%E2%80%932026_Iranian_protests "2025–2026 Iranian protests")

Beginning in late December 2025, massive [nationwide anti-government protests](/wiki/2025%E2%80%932026_Iranian_protests "2025–2026 Iranian protests") erupted in Iran. Originally driven by discontent over the [economic crisis](/wiki/Iranian_economic_crisis "Iranian economic crisis"), they escalated into explicit calls for the overthrow of the [Islamic Republic government](/wiki/Government_of_the_Islamic_Republic_of_Iran "Government of the Islamic Republic of Iran"). The protests became the largest in scale since the 1979 revolution with 5 million Iranians protesting.[[188]](#cite_note-208)[[189]](#cite_note-209) The Iranian government responded with [massacres of protesters](/wiki/2026_Iran_massacres "2026 Iran massacres"), with the deadliest incidents occurring on 8 to 10 January 2026.[[190]](#cite_note-210) The US-based [Human Rights Activists News Agency](/wiki/Human_Rights_Activists_News_Agency "Human Rights Activists News Agency") stated it had documented at least 7,007 deaths and was reviewing an additional 11,744,[[191]](#cite_note-HRA50DayReport-211) the Iranian government said it was 3,117, while various non-government affiliated Iranian health officials gave a figure of 32,000 deaths.[[192]](#cite_note-212)[[193]](#cite_note-213)

On 2 January 2026, Trump threatened a "locked and loaded" military intervention in Iran if the government decided to kill peaceful protesters.[[194]](#cite_note-214) On 13 January, he expressed support for Iranian anti-government protesters and pledged that "help is on the way" for them,[[195]](#cite_note-215) and later, on 23 January, Trump announced that a US "armada" was heading to the Middle East, including the aircraft carrier [USS *Abraham Lincoln*](/wiki/USS_Abraham_Lincoln_(CVN-72) "USS Abraham Lincoln (CVN-72)") and several guided-missile destroyers.[[196]](#cite_note-216)[[197]](#cite_note-217) On 13 February, Trump ordered the aircraft carrier [USS *Gerald R. Ford*](/wiki/USS_Gerald_R._Ford "USS Gerald R. Ford") and its supporting warships to sail to the Middle East.[[198]](#cite_note-218)

### 2026 nuclear negotiations

Further information: [2025–2026 Iran–United States negotiations](/wiki/2025%E2%80%932026_Iran%E2%80%93United_States_negotiations "2025–2026 Iran–United States negotiations")

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/President_Trump_Gaggles_with_Press_Before_Departing_the_White_House%2C_Feb._27%2C_2026.webm/250px--President_Trump_Gaggles_with_Press_Before_Departing_the_White_House%2C_Feb._27%2C_2026.webm.jpg)](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/f/f6/President_Trump_Gaggles_with_Press_Before_Departing_the_White_House%2C_Feb._27%2C_2026.webm/President_Trump_Gaggles_with_Press_Before_Departing_the_White_House%2C_Feb._27%2C_2026.webm.480p.vp9.webm)

27 February: President Trump briefly discusses the United States' relationship with Iran with the press, roughly 24 hours before the war would begin.

On 6 February 2026, Iran and the US held indirect nuclear negotiations in [Oman](/wiki/Oman "Oman")'s capital, [Muscat](/wiki/Muscat "Muscat"). Iran emphasized that progress depends on consultations back in capitals.[[199]](#cite_note-Ravid-2026-219) A second round of nuclear talks was scheduled in Geneva.[[199]](#cite_note-Ravid-2026-219) Between 15 and 20 February, Iran increased its oil exports to three times the normal rate, and reduced its oil storage.[[200]](#cite_note-220)

Just before the strikes began, on 27 February 2026, Omani foreign minister [Badr Al-Busaidi](/wiki/Badr_Al-Busaidi "Badr Al-Busaidi") said a "breakthrough" had been reached and Iran had agreed both to never stockpile enriched uranium and to full verification by the IAEA;[[201]](#cite_note-Al_Jazeera_English-3-221) furthermore, Iran had agreed to irreversibly downgrade its current enriched uranium to "the lowest level possible". Al-Busaidi said peace was "within reach".[[202]](#cite_note-222)[[201]](#cite_note-Al_Jazeera_English-3-221) Talks were expected to resume on 2 March.[[201]](#cite_note-Al_Jazeera_English-3-221) After the US and Israel attacked Iran, Al-Busaidi said that he was dismayed and that "active and serious negotiations" had been undermined.[[203]](#cite_note-223)

US Middle East envoy [Steve Witkoff](/wiki/Steve_Witkoff "Steve Witkoff") said Iran had begun recent nuclear talks by insisting on its "inalienable right" to [enrich uranium](/wiki/Enriched_uranium "Enriched uranium"), rejecting a US proposal for zero enrichment, and even boasting that its 460 kilograms of 60% enriched uranium could produce 11 nuclear bombs.[[204]](#cite_note-224)[[205]](#cite_note-225) Diplomats with knowledge of the Iran talks said Witkoff undermined the negotiations by misrepresenting the key exchange, stating that the US negotiators misunderstood the Iranian proposal, including Iran's offer to suspend uranium enrichment for several years and why Iran did not trust the US offer of nuclear fuel supplies.[[206]](#cite_note-226)

### US military buildup

Further information: [2026 United States military buildup in the Middle East](/wiki/2026_United_States_military_buildup_in_the_Middle_East "2026 United States military buildup in the Middle East")

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Abraham_Lincoln_Carrier_Strike_Group_Conducts_Photo_Exercise.jpg/250px-Abraham_Lincoln_Carrier_Strike_Group_Conducts_Photo_Exercise.jpg)](/wiki/File:Abraham_Lincoln_Carrier_Strike_Group_Conducts_Photo_Exercise.jpg)

6 February: [Carrier Strike Group 3](/wiki/Carrier_Strike_Group_3 "Carrier Strike Group 3") sails in formation in the [Arabian Sea](/wiki/Arabian_Sea "Arabian Sea") during the US military buildup

On 14 February, the US publicly began preparing its campaign.[[207]](#cite_note-227) By 19 February the [US buildup](/wiki/2026_United_States_military_buildup_in_the_Middle_East "2026 United States military buildup in the Middle East") was described as the largest in the Middle East since the [2003 invasion of Iraq](/wiki/2003_invasion_of_Iraq "2003 invasion of Iraq").[[208]](#cite_note-228) Netanyahu called Trump on 23 February to inform him about Khamenei's upcoming meeting with his top advisors and its location.[[209]](#cite_note-229)[[210]](#cite_note-230) On 24 February, during [the State of the Union Address](/wiki/2026_State_of_the_Union_Address "2026 State of the Union Address"), Trump accused Iran of reviving efforts to build nuclear weapons and advanced missile capabilities that could threaten the US, Europe, and US bases overseas. He warned that the US was prepared to act if necessary.[[211]](#cite_note-231) American intelligence reports suggested that alleged threats of long-range Iranian ballistic missiles were unfounded, with such capabilities requiring up until 2035 should Iran have decided to pursue the project.[[212]](#cite_note-232)

On 25 February 2026, Iranian foreign minister [Abbas Araghchi](/wiki/Abbas_Araghchi "Abbas Araghchi") stated that a "historic" agreement with the United States to avert military conflict was "within reach" ahead of renewed talks in Geneva. Araghchi emphasized that diplomacy must be prioritized to avoid further escalation. Despite high tensions and a significant US military buildup in the region, Araghchi reiterated on social media that Iran holds a "crystal clear" position against developing nuclear weapons, while defending its right to peaceful nuclear technology.[[213]](#cite_note-233)

According to *[The Wall Street Journal](/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal "The Wall Street Journal")*, US senator [Lindsey Graham](/wiki/Lindsey_Graham "Lindsey Graham") made the most compelling case to Trump for an assault on Iran.[[214]](#cite_note-234) According to *[The Washington Post](/wiki/The_Washington_Post "The Washington Post")*, crown prince of Saudi Arabia [Mohammed bin Salman](/wiki/Mohammed_bin_Salman "Mohammed bin Salman") had multiple phone calls with Trump urging him to attack Iran,[[215]](#cite_note-235) and Trump's decision to attack Iran came after the [Saudi Arabian](/wiki/Saudi_Arabia_lobby_in_the_United_States "Saudi Arabia lobby in the United States") and [Israeli](/wiki/Israel_lobby_in_the_United_States "Israel lobby in the United States") governments lobbied him repeatedly to make the move.[[216]](#cite_note-236) The claim was denied by Saudi Arabia.[[217]](#cite_note-237)

[![Benjamin Netanyahu (right) shakes hands with American politician Marco Rubio (left).](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Marco_Rubio_visits_Israel_from_February_15_to_17%2C_2025_-_18.jpg/120px-Marco_Rubio_visits_Israel_from_February_15_to_17%2C_2025_-_18.jpg)](/wiki/File:Marco_Rubio_visits_Israel_from_February_15_to_17,_2025_-_18.jpg)

Israeli prime minister [Benjamin Netanyahu](/wiki/Benjamin_Netanyahu "Benjamin Netanyahu") successfully lobbied the Trump administration for military intervention.

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Donald_Trump_state_visit_to_Saudi_Arabia%2C_2025-05-13_P20250513DT-1367_%28cropped%29.jpg/120px-Donald_Trump_state_visit_to_Saudi_Arabia%2C_2025-05-13_P20250513DT-1367_%28cropped%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:Donald_Trump_state_visit_to_Saudi_Arabia,_2025-05-13_P20250513DT-1367_(cropped).jpg)

Saudi crown prince [Mohammed bin Salman](/wiki/Mohammed_bin_Salman "Mohammed bin Salman") reportedly urged Trump to strike Iran despite public calls for diplomacy.

*[The Guardian](/wiki/The_Guardian "The Guardian")* reported a few days before the attack that the decision would be determined by the outcome of a meeting in [Geneva](/wiki/Geneva "Geneva"), in which the US would be led by Steve Witkoff and [Jared Kushner](/wiki/Jared_Kushner "Jared Kushner").[[218]](#cite_note-238) On the morning of the attack, some of Iran's top military and intelligence leaders, including Khamenei, were gathered together in the national security council offices for meetings.[[219]](#cite_note-FT01-239) US and Israeli military intelligence officers identified that the senior Iranian leaders would gather at three meetings that could be struck simultaneously.[[220]](#cite_note-240) Khamenei and his officials were above ground and in broad daylight. It is unknown how US and Israeli leadership were aware of their exact location.[[221]](#cite_note-Halbfinger-2026-241)

A few days before the attack, senior advisors for the Trump administration said it would be better if Israel strikes Iran first, so that the United States would have a better justification for going to war after Iran retaliates.[[222]](#cite_note-242) US secretary of state [Marco Rubio](/wiki/Marco_Rubio "Marco Rubio") later disclosed that the initial US attack on Iran was due to an Israeli intention to attack Iranian leadership, which would have jeopardized US forces in the region.[[223]](#cite_note-243) He also stated that the United States may have followed Israel into the conflict;[[224]](#cite_note-244) though Trump disagreed.[[225]](#cite_note-245)

### First week (28 February – 6 March)

Further information: [Assassination of Ali Khamenei](/wiki/Assassination_of_Ali_Khamenei "Assassination of Ali Khamenei"), [2026 Lebanon war](/wiki/2026_Lebanon_war "2026 Lebanon war"), and [2026 Iranian strikes on the United Arab Emirates](/wiki/2026_Iranian_strikes_on_the_United_Arab_Emirates "2026 Iranian strikes on the United Arab Emirates")

28 February: Trump and members of his cabinet oversee the war on Iran from the [Mar-a-Lago](/wiki/Mar-a-Lago "Mar-a-Lago") resort in [Palm Beach, Florida](/wiki/Palm_Beach,_Florida "Palm Beach, Florida").[[u]](#cite_note-246)

On 27 February at 3:38 p.m. [EST](/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone#Eastern_Standard_Time "Eastern Time Zone") (11:08 p.m. [IRST](/wiki/Iran_Standard_Time "Iran Standard Time")), Donald Trump, traveling on [Air Force One](/wiki/Air_Force_One "Air Force One") to [Corpus Christi](/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas "Corpus Christi, Texas"), Texas, gave the order to proceed with **Operation Epic Fury**.[[226]](#cite_note-247)[[219]](#cite_note-FT01-239) US missiles, drones, and [Israeli fighter jets](/wiki/Israeli_Air_Force#Current_inventory "Israeli Air Force") began striking Iran the next day, around 9:45 a.m. IRST (1:15 a.m. EST).[[227]](#cite_note-248)[[228]](#cite_note-249) The strikes took place during active negotiations over Iran's nuclear program.[[229]](#cite_note-250)[[230]](#cite_note-251) The attack coincided with the beginning of the holy month of [Ramadan](/wiki/Ramadan "Ramadan"), when Muslim people practice [fasting](/wiki/Islamic_fasting "Islamic fasting") between dawn and sunset.[[231]](#cite_note-252)

The operation was codenamed **Operation Roaring Lion**[[v]](#cite_note-253) by Israel.[[232]](#cite_note-254) The usage of the word lion refers to both [pre-revolutionary Iran](/wiki/Lion_and_Sun_flag "Lion and Sun flag") and the Jewish people.[[233]](#cite_note-255) The [Israeli Air Force](/wiki/Israeli_Air_Force "Israeli Air Force") (IAF) said that it had struck 500 military targets in western and central Iran in the largest combat sortie in its history.[[234]](#cite_note-256) Iranian naval vessels were also targeted.[[235]](#cite_note-257) Israel later said its initial strikes used over 1,200 bombs in 24 hours.[[236]](#cite_note-258) A US official said dozens of US strikes were carried out by planes based around the Middle East and from one or more aircraft carriers.[[237]](#cite_note-259)

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/U_S_Forces_Launch_Operation_Epic_Fury_%289542157%29.jpg/250px-U_S_Forces_Launch_Operation_Epic_Fury_%289542157%29.jpg)](/wiki/File:U_S_Forces_Launch_Operation_Epic_Fury_(9542157).jpg)

27 February: US aircraft sit on the flight deck of [USS *Abraham Lincoln*](/wiki/USS_Abraham_Lincoln_(CVN-72) "USS Abraham Lincoln (CVN-72)")

According to Iran International, quoting the [Iranian Students' News Agency](/wiki/Iranian_Students%27_News_Agency "Iranian Students' News Agency"), thousands of [Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps](/wiki/Islamic_Revolutionary_Guard_Corps "Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps") (IRGC) personnel, including several senior officials,[[238]](#cite_note-260) were killed or wounded[*[vague](/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness "Wikipedia:Vagueness")*] as several military bases were attacked.[[239]](#cite_note-Iran_International-2026a-261) Several strikes hit Tehran's [Pasteur Street](/wiki/Pasteur_Street "Pasteur Street") district, where the presidential palace is, and the [National Security Council](/wiki/Supreme_National_Security_Council "Supreme National Security Council").[[240]](#cite_note-262)[[241]](#cite_note-263)

The strikes were coupled with cyberattacks on Iranian infrastructure, media, and phone apps, with messages calling on Iranians to rise up against their government.[[242]](#cite_note-264)[[243]](#cite_note-JPost_hack-265)[[244]](#cite_note-266) The cyberattacks resulted in a near-total internet blackout in Iran, lasting over 60 hours with connectivity dropping to as low as 1% of normal levels, disrupting government communications, state media, and public services.[[245]](#cite_note-267)[[243]](#cite_note-JPost_hack-265) [Iran has since restricted internet access](/wiki/2026_Internet_blackout_in_Iran "2026 Internet blackout in Iran") to its people, allowing access only for government-approved users.[[246]](#cite_note-268)[[247]](#cite_note-bm-269)

Israeli military officials said months-long planning preceded their strikes, allowing them to pinpoint their targets, attain "tactical surprise", and gain US support.[[248]](#cite_note-270) Attacking during the morning, rather than at night which was when most of Israel's previous attacks on Iran took place, added to the element of surprise.[[221]](#cite_note-Halbfinger-2026-241) After the strikes began, Israel declared a state of emergency, citing expectation of an Iranian retaliation, and warned its citizens to remain in protected areas.[[249]](#cite_note-Liebermann-2026-271)[[250]](#cite_note-272) Israel called up 20,000 reservists in addition to the 50,000 already on duty.[[251]](#cite_note-273) The [Israeli Ministry of Health](/wiki/Israeli_Ministry_of_Health "Israeli Ministry of Health") moved its hospital operations underground.[[252]](#cite_note-274) Israel warned Iranian civilians residing near military industries and infrastructure to evacuate immediately.[[239]](#cite_note-Iran_International-2026a-261)

28 February: Trump addresses the nation about the strikes.

At 2:30 a.m. EST on 28 February, Donald Trump released an eight-minute video statement on [Truth Social](/wiki/Truth_Social "Truth Social"), saying that the purpose of the US strikes in Iran was effectively[*[vague](/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness "Wikipedia:Vagueness")*] [regime change](/wiki/Regime_change "Regime change"). Trump said that Iran's "menacing activities" endangered the US and its allies. He cited the [Iran hostage crisis](/wiki/Iran_hostage_crisis "Iran hostage crisis"), support for proxies such as [Hamas](/wiki/Hamas "Hamas") and [Hezbollah](/wiki/Hezbollah "Hezbollah"), and [its killings of protesters](/wiki/Human_rights_in_Iran "Human rights in Iran").[[253]](#cite_note-CBS_Major-Combat-Operations-275) Trump cited Iran calling "[Death to America](/wiki/Death_to_America "Death to America")" and its history of attacking US civilians and soldiers as evidence of Iran's intent against the United States.[[114]](#cite_note-fullStatement-132)[[254]](#cite_note-276) Trump and [Benjamin Netanyahu](/wiki/Benjamin_Netanyahu "Benjamin Netanyahu") both warned of the potential threat of nuclear weapons in Iran and called on the Iranian people to overthrow their government.[[253]](#cite_note-CBS_Major-Combat-Operations-275)[[255]](#cite_note-277) *[The Times of Israel](/wiki/The_Times_of_Israel "The Times of Israel")* reported that Defense Minister [Israel Katz](/wiki/Israel_Katz "Israel Katz") labeled the strikes a "pre-emptive attack" intended to "remove threats to the State of Israel".[[249]](#cite_note-Liebermann-2026-271) Iran rejected claims that it intended to attack the United States, citing the aggressive posture of the US armed forces as evidence.[[121]](#cite_note-Scahill-2026-139)

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/US_bases_in_the_Middle_East.svg/250px-US_bases_in_the_Middle_East.svg.png)](/wiki/File:US_bases_in_the_Middle_East.svg)

Major [US military bases and installations](/wiki/List_of_American_military_installations "List of American military installations") in the Middle East, including bases used during the strikes

Iranian forces reacted within hours by launching missiles and drones. Iran codenamed its military response **Operation True Promise IV**.[[w]](#cite_note-278)[[x]](#cite_note-279) Iranian state media also dubbed the events as the **Ramadan War**,[[y]](#cite_note-280) a term similar to the one used by Egypt and Syria during the [Yom Kippur War](/wiki/Yom_Kippur_War "Yom Kippur War") of 1973 against Israel.[[256]](#cite_note-281)

Iran struck Israeli targets in [Tel Aviv](/wiki/Tel_Aviv "Tel Aviv") and [Haifa](/wiki/Haifa "Haifa")[[257]](#cite_note-BBC_Tehran_response-282) as well as multiple countries throughout the Persian Gulf region.[[258]](#cite_note-283) Major targets included Bahrain's capital [Manama](/wiki/Manama "Manama"),[[259]](#cite_note-284)[[260]](#cite_note-285) [Kuwait International Airport](/wiki/Kuwait_International_Airport "Kuwait International Airport"), the United Arab Emirates' capital [Abu Dhabi](/wiki/Abu_Dhabi "Abu Dhabi"),[[261]](#cite_note-286)[[262]](#cite_note-287) [Riyadh](/wiki/Riyadh "Riyadh") and [Eastern Province](/wiki/Eastern_Province,_Saudi_Arabia "Eastern Province, Saudi Arabia") in Saudi Arabia,[[263]](#cite_note-Naar-2026-288) and [Erbil International Airport](/wiki/Erbil_International_Airport "Erbil International Airport") and the [US Consulate General in Erbil](/wiki/Consulate_General_of_the_United_States_in_Erbil "Consulate General of the United States in Erbil") in the Kurdistan Region of Iraq.[[264]](#cite_note-289)

Iran reported striking the US bases of [Al Udeid Air Base](/wiki/Al_Udeid_Air_Base "Al Udeid Air Base") in Qatar, [Ali Al Salem Air Base](/wiki/Ali_Al_Salem_Air_Base "Ali Al Salem Air Base") in Kuwait,[[265]](#cite_note-290) [Al Dhafra Air Base](/wiki/Al_Dhafra_Air_Base "Al Dhafra Air Base") in the UAE, and the US Navy [Fifth Fleet](/wiki/Fifth_Fleet "Fifth Fleet") headquarters in Bahrain.[[266]](#cite_note-291) Local sources said Iran used [Shahed drones](/wiki/Shahed_drones "Shahed drones"), which are among the weapons most used by Iran and its proxies, including Hamas and Hezbollah.[[267]](#cite_note-292) On 1 or 2 March, Iran struck the radar bases for US [THAAD](/wiki/Terminal_High_Altitude_Area_Defense "Terminal High Altitude Area Defense") missile defense systems in Jordan, the UAE and Saudi Arabia, destroying at least one of them.[[268]](#cite_note-293)

1 March: President Trump delivers an update on the strikes, including the assassination of Ali Khamenei.

Iran's top security official, [Ali Larijani](/wiki/Ali_Larijani "Ali Larijani"), announced the [Interim Leadership Council](/wiki/Interim_Leadership_Council "Interim Leadership Council") on 1 March and accused the US and Israel of trying to dismantle Iran. He warned "secessionist groups" of severe consequences if they take action.[[269]](#cite_note-Reuters-2026-294) Trump relayed to [NBC News](/wiki/NBC_News "NBC News") that "a large amount of leadership" in Iran had been killed.[[270]](#cite_note-295) Israel launched a new wave of strikes against Iranian targets.[[269]](#cite_note-Reuters-2026-294) Iran launched missiles and drones on Bahrain, Israel, Jordan, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE.[[271]](#cite_note-296)[[272]](#cite_note-297) The United Kingdom initially reported there were strikes against Cyprus,[[273]](#cite_note-298)[[274]](#cite_note-299) but later confirmed that there were not.[[275]](#cite_note-300)  Two oil tankers, the [Palau](/wiki/Palau "Palau")-flagged *Skylight* and the [Marshall Islands](/wiki/Marshall_Islands "Marshall Islands")-flagged *MKD VYOM*, were targeted off the coast of Oman.[[276]](#cite_note-301)[[277]](#cite_note-302)[[278]](#cite_note-303) Shipping through the Strait of Hormuz slowed to a standstill,[[279]](#cite_note-304) with 150 freight ships, including many oil tankers, stalled.[[280]](#cite_note-305)

To avert further Iranian strikes, the [E3](/wiki/EU_three#E3 "EU three") (the UK, France, and Germany) resolved to back, if needed, "proportionate military defensive measures" against drones and ballistic missiles, signaling the potential for direct involvement.[[281]](#cite_note-306) British prime minister [Keir Starmer](/wiki/Keir_Starmer "Keir Starmer") said that US can use British bases for "defensive" strikes on Iran[[282]](#cite_note-307) and disclosed that Ukrainian and other specialists would aid Gulf efforts to foil Iranian drone strikes.[[283]](#cite_note-308) [CNN](/wiki/CNN "CNN") reported that Saudi Crown Prince Salman, with US backing, vowed to employ military force against further Iranian incursions, calling Iranian strikes "cowardly" due to Saudi airspace being closed to US and Israeli attackers.[[284]](#cite_note-309) On 1 March, Trump announced that the US had accepted an Iranian proposal to further negotiations.[[285]](#cite_note-310) However, Ali Larijani subsequently ruled out talks.[[286]](#cite_note-311)

2 March: Montage of [US Central Command](/wiki/U.S._Central_Command "U.S. Central Command") strikes

On 2 March, the [US embassy in Kuwait](/wiki/US_embassy_in_Kuwait "US embassy in Kuwait") was struck and subsequently closed indefinitely; no casualties were reported.[[287]](#cite_note-312)[[288]](#cite_note-Reals-313) The US-flagged tanker *Stena Imperative* and the Honduras-flagged tanker *Athe Nova* were also struck.[[289]](#cite_note-314) A senior IRGC official who is an advisor to the IRGC commander said he would set fire onto any ship coming through the Strait of Hormuz, and added that no oil will leave the area.[[290]](#cite_note-315) Qatar shot down two [Iranian Su-24 bombers](/wiki/Sukhoi_Su-24 "Sukhoi Su-24"), making it the first nation to shoot down an Iranian aircraft in the conflict.[[291]](#cite_note-Salem-2026-316) The Qatari Ministry of Defense announced that [Ras Laffan Industrial City](/wiki/Ras_Laffan_Industrial_City "Ras Laffan Industrial City") and [Mesaieed Industrial Area](/wiki/Mesaieed_Industrial_Area "Mesaieed Industrial Area") were struck by two Iranian drones.[[292]](#cite_note-bloomberg4march-317) On 6 March, it was reported that according to satellite imagery analysis by both *[Bloomberg](/wiki/Bloomberg_News "Bloomberg News")* and the Energy Economics and Society Research Institute in Tokyo, Ras Laffan, the main gas facility in Qatar, appeared to have not been damaged before the "unprecedented shutdown" which sent fuel prices higher.[[293]](#cite_note-318)

2 March: Damage to residential buildings in [Sanandaj](/wiki/Sanandaj "Sanandaj"), Iranian Kurdistan

The United States and Israel attacked the [Natanz Nuclear Facility](/wiki/Natanz_Nuclear_Facility "Natanz Nuclear Facility")[[294]](#cite_note-319) and the Iranian media reported damage to the Khatam-al-Anbia and Gandhi hospitals.[[295]](#cite_note-320) A [friendly fire](/wiki/Friendly_fire "Friendly fire") incident took place when the pilot of an [F/A-18](/wiki/F/A-18 "F/A-18") of the [Kuwait Air Force](/wiki/Kuwait_Air_Force "Kuwait Air Force") shot down three US [F-15E](/wiki/F-15E "F-15E") fighters.[[296]](#cite_note-Hindustan_Times-2026-321)[[297]](#cite_note-Seligman-2026a-322)[[298]](#cite_note-323)[[288]](#cite_note-Reals-313) Israel and Hezbollah exchanged strikes against one another.[[299]](#cite_note-Kleinbaum-2026-324) Hezbollah began firing rockets and missiles on Israel in retaliation for the killing of Khamenei.[[300]](#cite_note-325)[[301]](#cite_note-326) Hezbollah later claimed that the attack was a "defensive act" after over a year of Israeli [attacks](/wiki/Hezbollah%E2%80%93Israel_conflict_(2023%E2%80%93present) "Hezbollah–Israel conflict (2023–present)") despite a [truce](/wiki/2024_Israel%E2%80%93Lebanon_ceasefire_agreement "2024 Israel–Lebanon ceasefire agreement").[[302]](#cite_note-327) It added that it restarted fighting to force Israel to stop its aggression and evacuate from [seized Lebanese territories](/wiki/Israeli%E2%80%93Lebanese_conflict "Israeli–Lebanese conflict"), emphasizing that the move was unrelated to the war.[[303]](#cite_note-328) Lebanon banned military activities by Hezbollah after the attacks.[[304]](#cite_note-329) An IDF spokesperson issued an emergency statement stating that the attack is to be considered "an official declaration of war by Hezbollah", vowing to "neutralize" the threat.[[299]](#cite_note-Kleinbaum-2026-324) Israel struck [southern Lebanon](/wiki/Southern_Lebanon "Southern Lebanon"),[[305]](#cite_note-330)[[82]](#cite_note-Stepansky-2026a-100)[[306]](#cite_note-331) Beirut, and the [Beqaa Valley](/wiki/Beqaa_Valley "Beqaa Valley").[[299]](#cite_note-Kleinbaum-2026-324) The IDF said that it killed the head of Hezbollah's intelligence headquarters [Hussein Makled](/w/index.php?title=Hussein_Makled&action=edit&redlink=1 "Hussein Makled (page does not exist)") in the strikes.[[307]](#cite_note-332)[[308]](#cite_note-333) In Lebanon, the IDF killed Adham Adnan al-Othman, the head of Palestinian Islamic Jihad's armed wing, [Al-Quds Brigades](/wiki/Al-Quds_Brigades "Al-Quds Brigades"), in Lebanon.[[309]](#cite_note-334)

The Royal Air Force station at [Akrotiri](/wiki/RAF_Akrotiri "RAF Akrotiri"), Cyprus, was targeted by a drone strike around midnight local time, with one causing minor damage.[[310]](#cite_note-335)[[311]](#cite_note-336)[[312]](#cite_note-337)[[313]](#cite_note-338) Another strike on Cyprus led to [Greece](/wiki/Greece "Greece") announcing that they would deploy frigates and [F-16s](/wiki/F-16 "F-16") to defend Cyprus from any further strikes by Iran.[[314]](#cite_note-339) Later in the day IRGC general [Sardar Jabbari](/w/index.php?title=Sardar_Jabbari&action=edit&redlink=1 "Sardar Jabbari (page does not exist)") commented that there was a US presence on the island of Cyprus and that Iran planned to strike the island "with such intensity that the Americans will be forced to leave".[[315]](#cite_note-340)[[316]](#cite_note-341) The pro-Iran militant group [Guardians of the Blood Brigade](/wiki/Iraqi_Guardians_of_Blood_Brigades "Iraqi Guardians of Blood Brigades") claimed responsibility for attacks on the US [Victory Base](/wiki/Victory_Base_Complex "Victory Base Complex") near [Baghdad International Airport](/wiki/Baghdad_International_Airport "Baghdad International Airport") and [Erbil](/wiki/Erbil "Erbil"), [Kurdistan Region](/wiki/Kurdistan_Region "Kurdistan Region"), Iraq.[[82]](#cite_note-Stepansky-2026a-100)[[317]](#cite_note-342)[[318]](#cite_note-343) The [Islamic Resistance in Iraq](/wiki/Islamic_Resistance_in_Iraq "Islamic Resistance in Iraq") claimed responsibility for over 23 drone strikes on US assets in Erbil.[[319]](#cite_note-344)

3 March: [Enghelab Square](/wiki/Enghelab_Square "Enghelab Square"), Tehran

On 3 March, US and Israeli strikes reportedly destroyed the [Supreme National Security Council](/wiki/Supreme_National_Security_Council "Supreme National Security Council") (SNSC) headquarters,[[320]](#cite_note-345) the [Expediency Discernment Council](/wiki/Expediency_Discernment_Council "Expediency Discernment Council") building in Tehran,[[321]](#cite_note-346) and what Israeli officials described as an underground nuclear weapons facility called Min Zadai.[[322]](#cite_note-347) The proximity of these strikes to the [Bushehr Nuclear Power Plant](/wiki/Bushehr_Nuclear_Power_Plant "Bushehr Nuclear Power Plant") (about 12 km (7.5 mi) away) prompted the Russian agency [Rosatom](/wiki/Rosatom "Rosatom") to suspend construction on new units and evacuate non-essential staff due to a loss of communication with Iranian officials.[[323]](#cite_note-348) US officials said US forces severely damaged Iran's naval capabilities, mainly in the Gulf of Oman, where several Iranian warships have reportedly been destroyed and key bases hit.[[324]](#cite_note-349)[[325]](#cite_note-350) Debris from an airstrike damaged [Golestan Palace](/wiki/Golestan_Palace "Golestan Palace"), a [UNESCO World Heritage Site](/wiki/UNESCO_World_Heritage_Site "UNESCO World Heritage Site"),[[326]](#cite_note-351) causing UNESCO to issue a statement that damaging UNESCO property is against international law.[[327]](#cite_note-352)

Israel Katz [authorized a ground invasion of Lebanon](/wiki/2026_Lebanon_war "2026 Lebanon war") on 3 March.[[328]](#cite_note-353)[[329]](#cite_note-354) They were later followed by a ground incursion in southern Lebanon with forces from the [91st Division](/wiki/91st_Division_(Israel) "91st Division (Israel)") with the goal to establish a "security layer" for the Israeli residents of northern settlements against Hezbollah, expecting to destroy their infrastructure in the [Israel-Lebanon border](/wiki/Israel-Lebanon_border "Israel-Lebanon border"). The [Lebanese government](/wiki/Lebanese_government "Lebanese government") reported that such Israeli [preventive attacks](/wiki/Preventive_attack "Preventive attack") reached [Kfarkela](/wiki/Kfarkela "Kfarkela") and [Qouzah](/wiki/Al-Qaouzah "Al-Qaouzah"), provoking the [Lebanese army](/wiki/Lebanese_army "Lebanese army") to do a redeployment from newly established border posts amid escalating Israeli activity in violation of the [2024 Israel–Lebanon ceasefire agreement](/wiki/2024_Israel%E2%80%93Lebanon_ceasefire_agreement "2024 Israel–Lebanon ceasefire agreement").[[330]](#cite_note-355) Israel also reported that it killed [Daoud Alizadeh](/w/index.php?title=Daoud_Alizadeh&action=edit&redlink=1 "Daoud Alizadeh (page does not exist)"), the commander of the Quds Force's Lebanon branch, in Tehran.[[331]](#cite_note-356)

4 March: Buildings in Tehran damaged by US-Israeli strikes

Western diplomats and other sources told *[The Jerusalem Post](/wiki/The_Jerusalem_Post "The Jerusalem Post")* that Qatar had struck Iran after Iran had attempted to strike Doha's airport and Qatar shot down two Iranian [Su-24](/wiki/Su-24 "Su-24") bombers;[[332]](#cite_note-WSJ-2026-357) [Channel 12](/wiki/Channel_12_(Israel) "Channel 12 (Israel)") also reported Qatari strikes.[[333]](#cite_note-358) Qatari Foreign Ministry spokesperson [Majed al-Ansari](/wiki/Majed_al-Ansari "Majed al-Ansari") denied the accusation that Qatar had joined the "campaign targeting Iran".[[334]](#cite_note-359) An IRGC general warned it would hit all economic hubs in the Middle East,[[335]](#cite_note-360)[[336]](#cite_note-361) and Abbas Araghchi said that any defensive European military involvement would be considered an act of war.[[337]](#cite_note-362)[[338]](#cite_note-363)

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Operation-Roaring-Lion-0011.jpg/250px-Operation-Roaring-Lion-0011.jpg)](/wiki/File:Operation-Roaring-Lion-0011.jpg)

An Israeli Air Force [F-35I](/wiki/F-35I "F-35I") "Adir" shot down an Iranian [Yak-130](/wiki/Yak-130 "Yak-130"), the first kill of a crewed aircraft by an [F-35](/wiki/F-35 "F-35").

On 4 March, US secretary of state Marco Rubio announced that the US-Israeli attacks on Iran would increase in their intensity.[[339]](#cite_note-ABC_News-2-364) Israel attacks hit the [Basij](/wiki/Basij "Basij") headquarters.[[340]](#cite_note-365) [Mojtaba Khamenei](/wiki/Mojtaba_Khamenei "Mojtaba Khamenei") survived an airstrike.[[341]](#cite_note-366) The IDF announced that an [F-35I](/wiki/F-35I "F-35I") "Adir" shot down a Russian-made Iranian [Yak-130](/wiki/Yak-130 "Yak-130") fighter jet over Tehran, the first time that an F-35 has ever shot down a crewed fighter jet in air-to-air combat and the first time the Israeli Air Force shot down an aircraft since 1985.[[342]](#cite_note-367)

Iran launched strikes against the [Al Udeid Air Base](/wiki/Al_Udeid_Air_Base "Al Udeid Air Base"),[[343]](#cite_note-368) as well as [Aramco's Ras Tanura](/wiki/2026_Aramco_refinery_attack "2026 Aramco refinery attack") oil refining facility.[[344]](#cite_note-369) The United States ordered the evacuation of non-essential workers in Cyprus in anticipation of Iranian strikes and issued a travel warning for the country.[[345]](#cite_note-370)[[346]](#cite_note-371) A ballistic missile launched from Iranian territory was intercepted by [NATO](/wiki/NATO "NATO") integrated air defense systems as it entered Turkish airspace, marking a significant escalation in the regional conflict.[[347]](#cite_note-372) Turkey asserted its right to self-defense after the missile landed in [Dörtyol](/wiki/D%C3%B6rtyol "Dörtyol"), [Hatay Province](/wiki/Hatay_Province "Hatay Province"),[[348]](#cite_note-373) and NATO secretary general [Mark Rutte](/wiki/Mark_Rutte "Mark Rutte") stated that the alliance was committed to defending Turkey.[[349]](#cite_note-374)

### Second week (7–13 March)

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/P20260307DT-1619_The_Dignified_Transfer_of_Remains_of_Six_U.S._Soldiers.jpg/250px-P20260307DT-1619_The_Dignified_Transfer_of_Remains_of_Six_U.S._Soldiers.jpg)](/wiki/File:P20260307DT-1619_The_Dignified_Transfer_of_Remains_of_Six_U.S._Soldiers.jpg)

Six dead US soldiers from the [2026 Port Shuaiba drone attack](/wiki/2026_Port_Shuaiba_drone_attack "2026 Port Shuaiba drone attack") are transferred to the US on 7 March 2026.

On 7 March, the US sent a third aircraft carrier, [USS *George H. W. Bush*](/wiki/USS_George_H._W._Bush "USS George H. W. Bush"), to the Middle East.[[372]](#cite_note-398)[[373]](#cite_note-399)[[374]](#cite_note-400) The British aircraft carrier [HMS *Prince of Wales*](/wiki/HMS_Prince_of_Wales_(R09) "HMS Prince of Wales (R09)") was placed on advanced readiness to defend British interests in the region.[[375]](#cite_note-401) Pezeskhian told reporters on 7 March that Iran would stop striking neighbors from 7 March onwards unless an attack originated from there.[[376]](#cite_note-Al_Jazeera-2-402) Despite this, [Dubai International Airport](/wiki/Dubai_International_Airport "Dubai International Airport") was later attacked by Iranian drones.[[377]](#cite_note-403) [NDTV](/wiki/NDTV "NDTV") reported that following a week's hostilities, Iran might only have around one thousand ballistic missiles left.[[378]](#cite_note-404) On 7 March, ballistic missiles were fired from Bahrain toward Iran, likely by US forces.[[379]](#cite_note-Missiles_fired_from_Bahrain-405)

On 8 March, Khamenei's second son, [Mojtaba](/wiki/Mojtaba_Khamenei "Mojtaba Khamenei"), was [designated](/wiki/2026_Iranian_supreme_leader_election "2026 Iranian supreme leader election") as Iran's new supreme leader.[[380]](#cite_note-406) Also on 8 March, US Central Command posted a message on [X](/wiki/X_(social_network) "X (social network)") in Persian and Arabic which "strongly urge[s] civilians in Iran to stay at home" and alleging that "[t]he Iranian regime is knowingly endangering innocent lives by using heavily populated civilian areas to conduct military operations, including launching one-way attack drones and ballistic missiles".[[381]](#cite_note-407)

Israeli airstrikes hit some of Iran's oil storage facilities, killing four people.[[382]](#cite_note-408) Strikes on fuel depots near Tehran caused a "river of fire" to pour out along the streets in the surrounding area,[[383]](#cite_note-409) and the city became engulfed in a cloud of thick black smoke, causing toxic [acidic](/wiki/Acid_rain "Acid rain") black rain to fall in the surrounding area.[[384]](#cite_note-b673-410) Residents were advised by Iranian authorities to stay indoors, wear masks when outside, and conserve food and fuel.[[385]](#cite_note-411) Kuwait reported that two of its officers were killed while performing duties.[[386]](#cite_note-412)[[387]](#cite_note-413) The [Saudi Civil Defense](/wiki/Saudi_Civil_Defense "Saudi Civil Defense") agency said that a strike on a compound in [Al-Kharj](/wiki/Al-Kharj "Al-Kharj") killed two people and injured 12 others.[[388]](#cite_note-Al_Jazeera-414) [US Central Command](/wiki/US_Central_Command "US Central Command") announced the death of a seventh US service member from an Iranian attack.[[389]](#cite_note-415)

The United Kingdom's Ministry of Defense confirmed that its forces had intercepted an Iranian drone headed towards Iraq. Additionally, the Ministry declared their intention to send a [Merlin helicopter](/wiki/Merlin_helicopter "Merlin helicopter") to the region to help aid in detecting future aerial threats.[[390]](#cite_note-416) According to the [Iranian Red Crescent Society](/wiki/Iranian_Red_Crescent_Society "Iranian Red Crescent Society"), a total of 65 schools and 32 medical facilities (such as hospitals and pharmacies) have been targeted since the war started, and more than 10,000 civilian sites have been damaged.[[391]](#cite_note-417) On 9 March, NATO confirmed the interception of a second ballistic missile over Turkish air space,[[392]](#cite_note-418) landing in the area of [Gaziantep](/wiki/Gaziantep "Gaziantep").[[393]](#cite_note-419) Meanwhile, Turkey deployed six F-16s and air defence systems to [Northern Cyprus](/wiki/Northern_Cyprus "Northern Cyprus") to ensure its security.[[394]](#cite_note-420)

Trump claimed that "the war is very complete, pretty much", after speaking with Russian president Vladimir Putin,[[395]](#cite_note-421) and said "we already won the war in many ways."[[396]](#cite_note-422) He additionally claimed that the Strait of Hormuz was open but also expressed interest in taking it over from Iran.[[397]](#cite_note-423) Trump later went back on some of his comments during a press conference held later that day, supporting Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth in stating that the war was just the "beginning of building a new country" and that it would end "soon".[[398]](#cite_note-424) However, Trump also said that the US will have the "most intense day" of strikes on Tuesday.[[399]](#cite_note-425) Following Syrian president Ahmed al-Sharaa's support for the Lebanon's objective to disarm Hezbollah,[[400]](#cite_note-Middle_East_Eye-2-426) the group [targeted](/wiki/2026_strikes_on_Syria_during_the_Iran_War "2026 strikes on Syria during the Iran War") the city of [Inkhil](/wiki/Inkhil "Inkhil") in Syria.[[401]](#cite_note-427) About a dozen [B1-B bombers](/wiki/Rockwell_B-1_Lancer "Rockwell B-1 Lancer") arrived at bases in Europe. Five landed at the Fairford Royal Air Force base, UK, joining three [B-52 bombers](/wiki/B-52_bombers "B-52 bombers"), while three others were redirected to [Ramstein Air Base](/wiki/Ramstein_Air_Base "Ramstein Air Base"), Germany.[[402]](#cite_note-428)

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Strait_of_Hormuz_Transits.webp/250px-Strait_of_Hormuz_Transits.webp.png)](/wiki/File:Strait_of_Hormuz_Transits.webp)

Strait of Hormuz transits dropped significantly since the start of the war.[[403]](#cite_note-429)

[GPS jamming](/wiki/GPS_jamming "GPS jamming") of uncertain origin has disrupted navigation of ships near the Strait of Hormuz, which may lead to disastrous accidents for oil tankers.[[404]](#cite_note-430) The [United States Department of State](/wiki/United_States_Department_of_State "United States Department of State") (supported by [Saudi Arabia](/wiki/Saudi_Arabia "Saudi Arabia"))[[405]](#cite_note-431) designated the [Muslim Brotherhood](/wiki/Muslim_Brotherhood "Muslim Brotherhood") branch in [Sudan](/wiki/Sudan "Sudan") (a [Sudanese Islamist](/wiki/Sudanese_Islamist "Sudanese Islamist") organization) as a [terrorist organization](/wiki/Terrorist_organization "Terrorist organization") due to its nexus with the [Axis of Resistance](/wiki/Axis_of_Resistance "Axis of Resistance").[[406]](#cite_note-432) Members of their militia fought alongside the IRGC due to the contacts established with the [Al-Bara' ibn Malik Battalion](/wiki/Al-Bara%27_ibn_Malik_Battalion "Al-Bara' ibn Malik Battalion") during the [Iranian intervention in Sudan](/wiki/Iranian_intervention_in_Sudan "Iranian intervention in Sudan").[[407]](#cite_note-433) On 10 March, a senior IRGC commander stated that Iran was only firing missiles with payloads of 1,000 kg or more.[[408]](#cite_note-ndtv10march-434) AFP reported that Iran's attacks were the "most intense and heaviest" since the start of the war.[[409]](#cite_note-435) This shows a shift in tactics from saturating air defences to high-impact penetration strikes.[[408]](#cite_note-ndtv10march-434)

Following the Syrian presidency's support for the disarmament of Hezbollah and a campaign of mobilization by the Syrian army on the Lebanese border,[[400]](#cite_note-Middle_East_Eye-2-426)[[410]](#cite_note-436) shells from Lebanon landed in the town of [Serghaya](/wiki/Serghaya "Serghaya"), 20 km (12 mi) from Damascus where the [Syrian Armed Forces](/wiki/Syrian_Armed_Forces "Syrian Armed Forces") forces presided.[[411]](#cite_note-437) The strikes were confirmed by the Operations Authority of the [Syrian Army](/wiki/Syrian_Army "Syrian Army").[[412]](#cite_note-438) Later in the day, Israel issued an urgent evacuation notice to southern suburbs of Beirut.[[413]](#cite_note-439)

US admiral [Brad Cooper](/wiki/Brad_Cooper_(admiral) "Brad Cooper (admiral)") reported that Iran's ballistic missile launch rate fell 90% from day one of the war and their drone launch rate fell 83%.[[414]](#cite_note-440) Defense analyst AJ Jaff reported that the missile launch rate fell 92% (480 to 40) and the drone launch rate fell 92% (720 to 60).[[415]](#cite_note-jpost10march-441) On 11 March, eleven B-1 and three B-52 US bombers were deployed to [RAF Fairford](/wiki/RAF_Fairford "RAF Fairford") in the UK, where long-range photography showed bombs being prepared for loading. This followed US war secretary Pete Hegseth's statement that it was the "most intense day of strikes inside Iran", though the targets of the mission remain unknown.[[416]](#cite_note-442)

A branch of [Bank Sepah](/wiki/Bank_Sepah "Bank Sepah") in Tehran was hit by a strike, prompting the IRGC to warn that in the future, they could retaliate by striking US or Israeli banks in the region.[[417]](#cite_note-443) Iran hit [Stryker](/wiki/Stryker_Corporation "Stryker Corporation"), a US-based medical supply company with a cyber attack.[[418]](#cite_note-444) On 12 March, [UNICEF](/wiki/UNICEF "UNICEF") reported that more than 1,100 children were injured or killed (200 reportedly killed in Iran, 91 in Lebanon, 4 in Israel, and 1 in Kuwait), hundreds of thousands displaced, and millions unable to attend schools from the violence of the war since it started. It also noted that these numbers will likely increase.[[419]](#cite_note-445)

Iranian deputy [intelligence minister](/wiki/Ministry_of_Intelligence_(Iran) "Ministry of Intelligence (Iran)") Akbar Ghaffari was killed in an airstrike.[[420]](#cite_note-446) An Iranian attack set two oil tankers on fire off the Iraqi coast, killing one person and prompting Iraq to suspend all oil terminal operations.[[421]](#cite_note-447) The historical [Safavid](/wiki/Safavid "Safavid")-era building of [Rashk-e Jenan](/wiki/Rashk-e_Jenan "Rashk-e Jenan"), also known as the Rashk Palace, was destroyed by direct US-Israeli strikes.[[422]](#cite_note-448) Other buildings that were damaged according to Ruhollah Seyed al-Asgari, the deputy director of cultural heritage for Isfahan province, include [Chehel Sotoun](/wiki/Chehel_Sotoun "Chehel Sotoun") pavilion, the [Rakib Khaneh Mansion](/w/index.php?title=Rakib_Khaneh_Mansion&action=edit&redlink=1 "Rakib Khaneh Mansion (page does not exist)"), the [Timurid Hall](/w/index.php?title=Timurid_Hall&action=edit&redlink=1 "Timurid Hall (page does not exist)"), the [Ashraf Hall](/w/index.php?title=Ashraf_Hall&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ashraf Hall (page does not exist)") and the windows of [Ali Qapu](/wiki/Ali_Qapu "Ali Qapu") palace.[[423]](#cite_note-449) Many of the affected buildings and monuments were legally protected by the [Blue Shield International](/wiki/Blue_Shield_International "Blue Shield International"),[[424]](#cite_note-450) which called damaging such sites a war crime.[[425]](#cite_note-451) Six French soldiers were injured in a drone strike at a base in Mala Qara, Iraqi Kurdistan. French president [Emmanuel Macron](/wiki/Emmanuel_Macron "Emmanuel Macron") announced on 13 March that one French soldier was killed in the attack.[[426]](#cite_note-lm13march-452)

Also on 13 March, to help deal with the economic costs of the war, the US temporarily lifted restrictions on the sale of Russian oil. However, oil prices have barely been affected by this.[[427]](#cite_note-Cooper-453) Iran announced a coordinated offensive by IRGC Navy, the Iranian Army, and Hezbollah against Israel and US bases.[[428]](#cite_note-454) Israel reported that 58 people have been hospitalized due to injuries following a missile strike.[[427]](#cite_note-Cooper-453) Iran again targeted Gulf countries with missiles and drones, causing oil prices to rise. Bahrain reported attacks on fuel tanks. Two people were injured when a "hostile drone" struck a residential building in Kuwait. The Kuwaiti authorities also stated that defense equipment and the country's main international airport were attacked, and six power lines were reported to have gone out of service. AFP reported that explosions were heard in downtown Dubai. At the same time, the British Maritime Trade Operations Agency said a container ship was attacked 35 nautical miles (65 kilometers) north of Jebel Ali in the UAE. According to Al Jazeera Arabic, sirens went off in every city across Jordan.[[429]](#cite_note-455)

For the third time in the war, ballistic missiles entered Turkish air space and were intercepted, with explosions heard in the proximity of the city of [Adana](/wiki/Adana "Adana") as well as sirens from Incirlik Air Base.[[430]](#cite_note-456) Israel struck several IRGC checkpoints in Iran. According to [Reuters](/wiki/Reuters "Reuters"), Israel attacked the checkpoints after receiving tip-offs by on the ground informants. Reuters also reported Israel had struck positions of the Basij used to suppress Iranian protests.[[431]](#cite_note-457)

### Third week (14–20 March)

As of the third week, analysts found little evidence of significant defections or desertions in the Iranian military;[[aa]](#cite_note-467) military defections have been considered a necessary condition for [regime change](/wiki/Regime_change "Regime change").[[441]](#cite_note-468) The US [conducted a large-scale bombing raid](/wiki/2026_Kharg_Island_raid "2026 Kharg Island raid") on [Kharg Island](/wiki/Kharg_Island "Kharg Island"), a key oil export hub home to 90 percent of Iran's oil exports. Over 90 military sites were targeted while the oil infrastructure was not targeted "for reasons of decency", according to Trump. Oil prices have risen over 40 percent since the start of the war.[[442]](#cite_note-469) According to CENTCOM, around 90 military targets were struck, while the island's oil infrastructure was not targeted.[[443]](#cite_note-470) On 14 March, [Kata'ib Hezbollah](/wiki/Kata%27ib_Hezbollah "Kata'ib Hezbollah") security commander [Abu Ali al-Askari](/w/index.php?title=Abu_Ali_al-Askari&action=edit&redlink=1 "Abu Ali al-Askari (page does not exist)") was killed in an airstrike in Baghdad, according to the group.[[444]](#cite_note-471)

The first two floors of the [Royal Tulip Al Rasheed Hotel](/wiki/Royal_Tulip_Al_Rasheed_Hotel "Royal Tulip Al Rasheed Hotel") in Baghdad's [Green Zone](/wiki/Green_Zone "Green Zone") were hit by a drone whilst hosting a European Union and Saudi Arabian delegation. The attack was not claimed by anyone; however, due to the proximity of a similar attack that had happened 48 hours earlier on the US embassy in Baghdad, there were suspicions of it being an Iranian attack.[[445]](#cite_note-472)

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Missile_interception_fragments_of_an_Iranian_missile_hit_Haifa_oil_refineries_in_Haifa_Bay%2C_March%2C_19_2026.jpg/250px-Missile_interception_fragments_of_an_Iranian_missile_hit_Haifa_oil_refineries_in_Haifa_Bay%2C_March%2C_19_2026.jpg)](/wiki/File:Missile_interception_fragments_of_an_Iranian_missile_hit_Haifa_oil_refineries_in_Haifa_Bay,_March,_19_2026.jpg)

An Iranian missile hit Haifa oil refineries in [Haifa Bay](/wiki/Haifa_Bay "Haifa Bay"), 19 March 2026

On 16 March, both China and US-aligned [NATO](/wiki/NATO "NATO") nations in Europe rejected Trump's call to provide military support to reopen the [Strait of Hormuz](/wiki/Strait_of_Hormuz "Strait of Hormuz").[[446]](#cite_note-473)[[447]](#cite_note-474) Trump rebuked his NATO allies, calling their decision a "very foolish mistake".[[448]](#cite_note-475) On 17 March, Trump made a statement on [Truth Social](/wiki/Truth_Social "Truth Social") renouncing NATO's assistance, and unexpectedly, US allies in the Indo-Pacific, namely Japan, South Korea, and [Australia](/wiki/Australian_involvement_in_the_2026_Iran_war "Australian involvement in the 2026 Iran war"), for refusing to join US-led attacks on [Iran](/wiki/Iran "Iran") during the war.[[449]](#cite_note-476)[[450]](#cite_note-477)[[451]](#cite_note-478)[[452]](#cite_note-479) In the same statement, Trump declared that the United States "[does] not need the help of anyone" regarding the war.[[453]](#cite_note-480)

In the early morning of 17 March, several senior Iranian officials, including [Ali Larijani](/wiki/Ali_Larijani "Ali Larijani") and Basij chief [Gholamreza Soleimani](/wiki/Gholamreza_Soleimani "Gholamreza Soleimani") were [targeted by Israeli airstrikes](/wiki/Assassination_of_Ali_Larijani "Assassination of Ali Larijani").[[454]](#cite_note-Iran_International-2026-481)[[455]](#cite_note-482) Both their deaths were later confirmed by Iranian state media.[[456]](#cite_note-sk-483) In response, Iran launched a missile barrage that killed two Israeli civilians in [Ramat Gan](/wiki/Ramat_Gan "Ramat Gan").[[457]](#cite_note-484) The same day, Israel launched a ground invasion of [southern Lebanon](/wiki/Southern_Lebanon "Southern Lebanon").[[458]](#cite_note-485)

On 18 March, in a major escalation,[[459]](#cite_note-blowup-486) Israel [struck](/wiki/2026_South_Pars_field_attack "2026 South Pars field attack") the [South Pars natural gas field](/wiki/South_Pars/North_Dome_Gas-Condensate_field "South Pars/North Dome Gas-Condensate field") in the Persian Gulf and its neighboring refineries in Iran with US coordination.[[460]](#cite_note-oj-487) In retaliation, Iran attacked the world's largest LNG production facility in Qatar. Trump claimed he was not aware of the South Pars attack, promised that there would be no more Israeli attacks on Iranian oil infrastructure, and threatened Iran not to continue attacking Qatari energy infrastructure.[[459]](#cite_note-blowup-486)[[461]](#cite_note-488) On the same day, Israel said it had killed Iranian intelligence minister [Esmaeil Khatib](/wiki/Esmaeil_Khatib "Esmaeil Khatib") in an overnight airstrike.[[462]](#cite_note-489) Pezeshkian confirmed Khatib's killing on the same day.[[463]](#cite_note-490) A hair salon in [Beit Awwa](/wiki/Beit_Awwa "Beit Awwa") was [struck by a projectile](/wiki/2026_Beit_Awwa_salon_strike "2026 Beit Awwa salon strike"), killing four women and injuring six,[[464]](#cite_note-491) with Israeli authorities stating that it was most likely [cluster munitions](/wiki/Cluster_munitions "Cluster munitions") and Palestinian authorities saying it was an errant Israeli interceptor.[[465]](#cite_note-492)[[466]](#cite_note-493)

On 19 March, a U.S. [F-35](/wiki/F-35 "F-35") made an emergency landing after a suspected hit by Iranian forces.[[467]](#cite_note-494)[[468]](#cite_note-cnn19march-495) A [BAZAN Group](/wiki/BAZAN_Group "BAZAN Group") refinery in Haifa was hit during a broader Iranian missile attack on the area as a retaliation for Israeli attacks on South Pars gas facilities.[[469]](#cite_note-496)[[470]](#cite_note-497) Netanyahu insisted that "You can't make a revolution from the air" and that "there must be a ground component — I won't share with you all the options", implying that there was a potential for limited assault operations involving soldiers on the ground in Iran.[[471]](#cite_note-498) The U.S. began [an aerial campaign](/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_campaign "2026 Strait of Hormuz campaign") against Iranian vessels and drones in the Strait of Hormuz as part of an effort to reopen it to international shipping.[[472]](#cite_note-499)

### Fourth week (21–27 March)

Further information: [2026 Strait of Hormuz campaign](/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_campaign "2026 Strait of Hormuz campaign")

Admiral [Brad Cooper](/wiki/Brad_Cooper_(admiral) "Brad Cooper (admiral)") said that the U.S. military had so far struck more than 8,000 Iranian military targets, including 130 vessels.[[473]](#cite_note-500) On 21 March, the U.S. conducted strikes on the [Natanz Nuclear Facility](/wiki/Natanz_Nuclear_Facility "Natanz Nuclear Facility") using [bunker buster](/wiki/Bunker_buster "Bunker buster") bombs to target the site.[[474]](#cite_note-501) Russia condemned the strikes on Natanz as a "blatant violation of international law," while the [International Atomic Energy Agency](/wiki/International_Atomic_Energy_Agency "International Atomic Energy Agency") (IAEA) urged military restraint "to avoid any risk of a nuclear accident".[[475]](#cite_note-502) In response, Iran struck the southern Israeli town of [Dimona](/wiki/Dimona "Dimona"), injuring at least 78 people.[[476]](#cite_note-503) Iran said that it targeted the [Shimon Peres Negev Nuclear Research Center](/wiki/Shimon_Peres_Negev_Nuclear_Research_Center "Shimon Peres Negev Nuclear Research Center").[[477]](#cite_note-504)[[478]](#cite_note-505) Shortly after, Iran conducted another strike on the southern Israeli town of [Arad](/wiki/Arad,_Israel "Arad, Israel"), injuring over 116 people.[[479]](#cite_note-506)

Citing unnamed US officials, CNN and *The Wall Street Journal* reported that Iran unsuccessfully attempted to strike the joint US–UK military base at [Diego Garcia](/wiki/Diego_Garcia "Diego Garcia") on the [Chagos Islands](/wiki/Chagos_Archipelago "Chagos Archipelago") in the [British Indian Ocean Territory](/wiki/British_Indian_Ocean_Territory "British Indian Ocean Territory").[[103]](#cite_note-bbc21march-121) The report said one of the missiles broke apart mid-flight, while another was intercepted by a [SM-3](/wiki/SM-3 "SM-3") air defence missile launched by a US warship.[[480]](#cite_note-507)[[481]](#cite_note-508) Israel said that Iran used a two-stage [intercontinental ballistic missile](/wiki/Intercontinental_ballistic_missile "Intercontinental ballistic missile") to target the island.[[482]](#cite_note-509)[[483]](#cite_note-510) Iran denied launching missiles towards Diego Garcia, saying that it was an Israeli false flag attack.[[104]](#cite_note-Falseflag-122) No evidence has been presented that the alleged Iranian missiles neared Diego Garcia or that one was intercepted.[[484]](#cite_note-511)

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Operation-Roaring-Lion-0065.jpg/250px-Operation-Roaring-Lion-0065.jpg)](/wiki/File:Operation-Roaring-Lion-0065.jpg)

Israeli troops near the Lebanese border on 21 March 2026

The [Houthi movement](/wiki/Houthis "Houthis") in [Yemen](/wiki/Yemen "Yemen") warned that it would respond to any escalation against Iran, including [efforts to reopen the Strait of Hormuz](/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_campaign "2026 Strait of Hormuz campaign"). It specifically warned the two Arab countries offering to join the Strait of Hormuz campaign—Bahrain and the United Arab Emirates—that they "will be the first to lose in this battle".[[485]](#cite_note-512) The Houthis further threatened to join the war in the event that US allies joined the attack on Iran or if the US and Israel used the [Red Sea](/wiki/Red_Sea "Red Sea") to carry out operations.[[486]](#cite_note-513) Lebanon's government expelled the Iranian ambassador.[[487]](#cite_note-sl-514)

Trump issued an ultimatum to Iran, threatening to strike its [power plants](/wiki/Power_plants "Power plants") unless it opened the Strait of Hormuz within 48 hours.[[488]](#cite_note-515) Iran doubled down, threatening to "completely" close the Strait of Hormuz and strike vital infrastructure across the region such as energy and desalination facilities critical for drinking water.[[489]](#cite_note-516) [Iranian opposition](/wiki/Iranian_opposition "Iranian opposition") figure and former crown prince [Reza Pahlavi](/wiki/Reza_Pahlavi "Reza Pahlavi") called on Trump and Netanyahu to target the military while sparing civilian infrastructure which "Iranians will need to rebuild our country."[[490]](#cite_note-517) Halfway through the deadline, Trump announced that he was postponing strikes against Iranian power plants for five days,[[491]](#cite_note-518) and said the US was negotiating with Iran to end the war.[[492]](#cite_note-519) Iran denied the talks ever took place or were taking place and called the president "deceitful".[[493]](#cite_note-520) Iran rejected the 15-point peace plan presented by the US.[[494]](#cite_note-521)[[495]](#cite_note-522) Iran asserted that Lebanon must be included as part of a ceasefire deal, thereby making a ceasefire conditional on an end to the [2026 Lebanon war](/wiki/2026_Lebanon_war "2026 Lebanon war") against Hezbollah.[[496]](#cite_note-523)

On 24 March, Israel or its allies shot down an Iranian missile crossing above Lebanon at high altitude, according to the Lebanese army, which also confirmed that the missile was not targeting Lebanon. This was the first time during the current conflict when a shootdown of an Iranian missile took place over Lebanese territory.[[497]](#cite_note-524)[[498]](#cite_note-525)

A US strike on the Habbaniya military base—which hosts units from the [Popular Mobilization Forces](/wiki/Popular_Mobilization_Forces "Popular Mobilization Forces")—in [Al Anbar Governorate](/wiki/Al_Anbar_Governorate "Al Anbar Governorate") killed seven members of the [Iraqi Armed Forces](/wiki/Iraqi_Armed_Forces "Iraqi Armed Forces") and injured 13 others. In response, Iraqi prime minister [Mohammed Shia' al-Sudani](/wiki/Mohammed_Shia%27_al-Sudani "Mohammed Shia' al-Sudani") summoned the US embassy's [chargé d'affaires](/wiki/Charg%C3%A9_d%27affaires "Chargé d'affaires") in Baghdad.[[499]](#cite_note-526) Iranian state media threatened that Iran could seize Bahraini and Emirati territory if the US "makes any mistakes", while signaling its readiness to open a new front in Yemen by closing the [Bab al-Mandab](/wiki/Bab-el-Mandeb "Bab-el-Mandeb") with the [Houthis](/wiki/Houthis "Houthis").[[500]](#cite_note-527)[[501]](#cite_note-528) Israel said it killed Iran's top naval commander, [Alireza Tangsiri](/wiki/Alireza_Tangsiri "Alireza Tangsiri"), purportedly as part of an effort to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, which Katz said Tangsiri was "directly responsible" for the closure of.[[502]](#cite_note-529)[[503]](#cite_note-530)[[504]](#cite_note-531)

On 27 March, a day after Trump and Pete Hegseth announced the "neutralization" of Iran's military, an Iranian missile and drone strike on [Prince Sultan Air Base](/wiki/Prince_Sultan_Air_Base "Prince Sultan Air Base") damaged several US refueling aircraft and injured 29 US soldiers.[[505]](#cite_note-USW29-532) An [E-3 Sentry](/wiki/Boeing_E-3_Sentry "Boeing E-3 Sentry") was also damaged by the strike.[[506]](#cite_note-533)[[507]](#cite_note-534)[[508]](#cite_note-535) Military analyst Cedric Leighton described the attack as a "a serious blow to [US] surveillance capabilities".[[509]](#cite_note-536) Iran showed no signs of ceding its control over the Strait of Hormuz.[[510]](#cite_note-537) The same day, Iran blocked two Chinese ships from entering the strait and sought to formalize fees for ships passing through it.[[511]](#cite_note-538)[[512]](#cite_note-539) Despite pushes for talks, Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz publicly vowed to "intensify and expand" Israeli attacks in Iran.[[513]](#cite_note-540) The same day, the [Shahid Khondab Heavy Water Complex](/wiki/IR-40 "IR-40") in Arak, which has in the past been used to test uranium enrichment, and the [Ardakan yellowcake production plant](/wiki/Nuclear_facilities_in_Iran#Ardakan "Nuclear facilities in Iran") in Yazd Province were confirmed to have been damaged in Israeli airstrikes.[[514]](#cite_note-struckmarch2026-541)[[515]](#cite_note-march2026struckairstrike-542)

### Fifth week (28 March – present)

Further information: [2026 Houthi strikes on Israel](/wiki/2026_Houthi_strikes_on_Israel "2026 Houthi strikes on Israel")

The Houthis joined the war on 28 March [conducting a ballistic missile attack](/wiki/2026_Houthi_strikes_on_Israel "2026 Houthi strikes on Israel") towards Israel and triggering air raid sirens in [Beersheba](/wiki/Beersheba "Beersheba").[[516]](#cite_note-543) The Houthis said that they had launched ballistic missiles targeting "sensitive" military sites in Israel, and vowed to continue the attacks "until the aggression on all resistance fronts stops." The missile was intercepted and no casualties were reported.[[517]](#cite_note-544) The Houthis subsequently launched a second missile at [Eilat](/wiki/Eilat "Eilat").[[518]](#cite_note-545) In response, the internationally recognized [Yemeni government](/wiki/Presidential_Leadership_Council "Presidential Leadership Council") condemned Iran's "frequent attempts to drag Yemen" into conflict "through its terrorist militias."[[519]](#cite_note-546) On the same day, 2,500 U.S. marines from the [31st Marine Expeditionary Unit](/wiki/31st_Marine_Expeditionary_Unit "31st Marine Expeditionary Unit") arrived in the Middle East. They are intended to be part of the effort to open the Strait of Hormuz.[[520]](#cite_note-547)

> "Since the beginning of the [War of Revival](/wiki/Gaza_war "Gaza war"), we have inflicted ten plagues on the axis of evil. The plague of Hamas in Gaza, the plague of Hezbollah in Lebanon, the plague of Assad in Syria, the plague of the terrorist organizations in [Judea and Samaria](/wiki/Judea_and_Samaria_Area "Judea and Samaria Area"), the plague of the Houthis in Yemen, and five more plagues on Iran."

[Benjamin Netanyahu](/wiki/Benjamin_Netanyahu "Benjamin Netanyahu"), 31 March 2026

[U.S. Central Command](/wiki/United_States_Central_Command "United States Central Command") stated that it had struck over 11,000 targets in Iran since the beginning of the war. On 28 March, joint U.S.–Israeli airstrikes severely damaged several residential and civilian facilities, including the [Iran University of Science and Technology](/wiki/Iran_University_of_Science_and_Technology "Iran University of Science and Technology").[[521]](#cite_note-548) In response, Iran threatened to attack Israeli and American colleges across the Middle East.[[522]](#cite_note-549) Airstrikes also struck a 10,000-cubic-meter water reservoir in [Haftkel](/wiki/Haftkel "Haftkel"), in [Khuzestan province](/wiki/Khuzestan_province "Khuzestan province").[[523]](#cite_note-550)

On 29 March, joint U.S.–Israeli strikes hit a quay at the port city of [Bandar Khamir](/wiki/Bandar_Khamir "Bandar Khamir") in southern Iran, killing at least five people.[[524]](#cite_note-551)[[525]](#cite_note-552) An Iranian missile struck a chemical plant in the [Ne'ot Hovav](/wiki/Ne%27ot_Hovav "Ne'ot Hovav") industrial zone, injuring one person and causing fears of a hazardous leak.[[526]](#cite_note-553) A convoy of the Iraqi Popular Mobilization Forces was seen entering [Khorramshahr](/wiki/Khorramshahr "Khorramshahr") in southwestern Iran.[[527]](#cite_note-554) Eleven-year-old child soldier [Alireza Jafari](/wiki/Alireza_Jafari_(child_soldier) "Alireza Jafari (child soldier)") died following an Israeli drone strike.[[528]](#cite_note-555)

On 30 March, Trump said on Truth Social that if a deal was not reached soon and the Strait of Hormuz reopened, the US would conclude the war by destroying all of Iran's power plants, oil wells, Kharg Island, and possibly all its desalinization plants.[[529]](#cite_note-556) On the same day Turkey and NATO intercepted the fourth missile directed towards Turkish territory. Iran denied firing a missile at Turkey, claiming that it was a [false flag](/wiki/False_flag "False flag") attack.[[530]](#cite_note-557)[[531]](#cite_note-558) The U.S. began flying [B-52 bombers](/wiki/Boeing_B-52_Stratofortress "Boeing B-52 Stratofortress") over Iranian territory for the first time, indicating its confidence of [air supremacy](/wiki/Air_supremacy "Air supremacy") over Iran.[[532]](#cite_note-559) The U.S. used bombers to target an ammunition depot and air base in [Isfahan](/wiki/Isfahan "Isfahan"), causing vast explosions.[[533]](#cite_note-560) U.S-allied Gulf states such as Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Kuwait, and Bahrain reportedly pushed Trump to continue the war until there are significant changes in Iran's leadership.[[534]](#cite_note-561) American journalist [Shelly Kittleson](/wiki/Shelly_Kittleson "Shelly Kittleson") was kidnapped in Baghdad by [Kata'ib Hezbollah](/wiki/Kata%27ib_Hezbollah "Kata'ib Hezbollah")—the strongest faction within the PMF—who demanded that the Iraqi government release several detained members of the group.[[535]](#cite_note-562)

> "We are going to hit them extremely hard. Over the next two to three weeks, we're going to bring them back to the Stone Ages, where they belong."

[Donald Trump](/wiki/Donald_Trump "Donald Trump"), 2 April 2026

On 1 April, [Mehr News Agency](/wiki/Mehr_News_Agency "Mehr News Agency"), a semi-official Iranian government news outlet, reported that policy expert and former foreign minister [Kamal Kharazi](/wiki/Kamal_Kharazi "Kamal Kharazi") was seriously injured and his wife was killed in an airstrike which hit his home.[[536]](#cite_note-563) Iranian officials described the attack as an attempt to derail diplomacy, noting that Kharazi was overseeing a possible meeting between Iranian officials and U.S. Vice President [JD Vance](/wiki/JD_Vance "JD Vance").[[537]](#cite_note-564) Airstrikes on the strategically important [Hengam Island](/wiki/Hengam_Island "Hengam Island") in the Strait of Hormuz caused seven injuries.[[538]](#cite_note-565) Trump indicated that he is no longer concerned about Iran's enriched uranium buried underground.[[539]](#cite_note-566) Netanyahu asserted that the U.S.–Israeli strikes have eliminated Iran's ability to produce nuclear weapons and ballistic missiles, thereby removing "two existential threats" to Israel.[[540]](#cite_note-567)[[541]](#cite_note-568) The Iranian president Pezeshkian published an open letter to US citizens.[[542]](#cite_note-569)

On 2 April, Trump made a speech addressing the nation, in which he said that the strikes have "dramatically curtailed" Iranian missile and drone systems, and added that the attacks on Iran will escalate over the next two to three weeks to "bring them back to the Stone Ages, where they belong."[[543]](#cite_note-570) Two US strikes on the B1 bridge between Tehran and [Karaj](/wiki/Karaj "Karaj"), which was described as the highest bridge in the Middle East, reportedly killed eight people and wounded 95 others, causing it to collapse.[[544]](#cite_note-571) The IDF also announced that one of its strikes in the [Kermanshah](/wiki/Kermanshah "Kermanshah") area of Iran killed Iranian ballistics missile chief Makram Atimi and several battalion commanders from Atimi's central Iranian ballistic missile unit.[[545]](#cite_note-572)[[546]](#cite_note-573)

Additional senior officials confirmed to have been killed are [Salah Asadi](/wiki/Salah_Asadi "Salah Asadi"), head of intelligence for Iran's emergency command, [Mohammad Shirazi](/wiki/Mohammad_Shirazi_(general) "Mohammad Shirazi (general)"), head of the military office of Khamenei, head of the [Organization of Defensive Innovation and Research](/wiki/Organization_of_Defensive_Innovation_and_Research "Organization of Defensive Innovation and Research") (SPND) [Hossein Jabal Amelian](/wiki/Hossein_Jabal_Amelian "Hossein Jabal Amelian") and former SPND head [Reza Mozaffari Nia](/wiki/Reza_Mozaffari_Nia "Reza Mozaffari Nia").[[239]](#cite_note-Iran_International-2026a-261) On 1 March, chief of staff [Abdolrahim Mousavi](/wiki/Abdolrahim_Mousavi "Abdolrahim Mousavi") and former president [Mahmoud Ahmadinejad](/wiki/Mahmoud_Ahmadinejad "Mahmoud Ahmadinejad") were reported by Iranian state media to have also been killed by strikes.[[560]](#cite_note-587)[[561]](#cite_note-588) However, later reports stated that Ahmadinejad is alive, or at least that reports of his death cannot be confirmed.[[562]](#cite_note-589)

On 28 February, intelligence and military sources told CBS News that 40 Iranian officials had been killed in the strikes but that they were not "clear whether these officials were in one location or multiple locations".[[563]](#cite_note-590)[[564]](#cite_note-591) [Iran's Foreign Ministry](/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_(Iran) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Iran)") stated the military has lost control over several units, that are operating according to old general instructions.[[565]](#cite_note-592) On 13 March, the Israeli military estimated that between 3,000 and 4,000 Iranian soldiers and commanders had been killed.[[566]](#cite_note-593) On 18 March, the [Hengaw Organization for Human Rights](/wiki/Hengaw_Organization_for_Human_Rights "Hengaw Organization for Human Rights") estimated that 5,300+ members of the Iranian military forces had been killed in the attacks, up from 1,300+ on 2 March, 2,100+ on 4 March, 3,900+ on 10 March, 4,400+ on 14 March, and 4,800+ on 17 March.[[567]](#cite_note-594)

On 17 March, Israel [assassinated](/wiki/Assassination_of_Ali_Larijani "Assassination of Ali Larijani") [Ali Larijani](/wiki/Ali_Larijani "Ali Larijani"), [secretary](/wiki/Secretary_of_the_Supreme_National_Security_Council "Secretary of the Supreme National Security Council") of Iran's [Supreme National Security Council](/wiki/Supreme_National_Security_Council "Supreme National Security Council"). This was confirmed by Iran.[[456]](#cite_note-sk-483) His assassination caused anxiety among Iranian officials who were concerned "that Israel would not stop until all of Iran's leaders were killed and the Islamic Republic toppled", as well as questions over who would be targeted next.[[568]](#cite_note-595)

The NGO [HRANA](/wiki/HRANA "HRANA") documented 3,114 deaths in Iran due to airstrikes by 17 March, including 1,354 civilians, 1,138 military personnel, and 622 unclassified. HRANA states that "military casualties are significantly higher than the figures reported in these reports," as confirmations depend largely on government data which is obscured "due to the sensitive nature of military information." Because of HRANA's "limited access to locations where military forces are present, [its] count largely reflects reports of senior officers or military personnel who were present in urban areas."[[569]](#cite_note-HRANA-596)[[ab]](#cite_note-599) On 31 March, Iran International reported that at least 4,700 security forces had been killed.[[572]](#cite_note-600)

#### Civilians

On 28 February, the Shajareh Tayyebeh girl's elementary school in [Minab](/wiki/Minab "Minab") was [hit by the American attacks](/wiki/2026_Minab_school_attack "2026 Minab school attack"), according to Iran.[[573]](#cite_note-601) The school was located close to[[ac]](#cite_note-605) the Sayyid al-Shuhada military complex,[[577]](#cite_note-606) of which it once had been a part.[[576]](#cite_note-Browne-2026-604)[[578]](#cite_note-607) While there is no independent confirmation of the number killed,[[579]](#cite_note-608)[[580]](#cite_note-BBC_News-2026b-609) Iranian state media reported that more than 175 people, mostly school children, were killed and 95 wounded in the strike.[[581]](#cite_note-610) Evidence assembled by US investigators[[582]](#cite_note-611)[[583]](#cite_note-612) and *The New York Times* suggested that the attack was carried out by the US.[[584]](#cite_note-613) A video showed a US Tomahawk missile hitting a building adjacent to the school, adding to evidence indicating the US was responsible for the strike.[[585]](#cite_note-614) On 11 March, *[The New York Times](/wiki/The_New_York_Times "The New York Times")* reported that preliminary findings in an ongoing investigation indicated that the strike was conducted by the US with outdated targeting data.[[586]](#cite_note-615) UN human rights experts characterized the strike as a potential [war crime](/wiki/United_States_war_crimes "United States war crimes") under the [Rome Statute](/wiki/Rome_Statute "Rome Statute").[[587]](#cite_note-616)

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Damages_caused_by_attacks_on_Tehran_22_Avash_25_esfand_1404.webp/250px-Damages_caused_by_attacks_on_Tehran_22_Avash_25_esfand_1404.webp.png)](/wiki/File:Damages_caused_by_attacks_on_Tehran_22_Avash_25_esfand_1404.webp)

Damage to a residential structure in Tehran following aerial attacks on 15 March 2026

At least 20 civilians, mostly families who gathered after breaking their [fast](/wiki/Islamic_fasting "Islamic fasting") at Ramadan,[[588]](#cite_note-BBCd4xo-617) were killed in Tehran's Niloofar Square on 1 or 2 March, according to Iranian-state owned media.[[589]](#cite_note-618) On 3 March, the Red Crescent said that over 600 civilians had been killed,[[590]](#cite_note-619) while the [Human Rights Activists in Iran](/wiki/Human_Rights_Activists_in_Iran "Human Rights Activists in Iran") estimated that 742 civilians had been killed in the attacks.[[569]](#cite_note-HRANA-596) On 7 March, the [Iranian Red Crescent Society](/wiki/Iranian_Red_Crescent_Society "Iranian Red Crescent Society") reported that over 6,668 civilian units were targeted by US-Israeli strikes, including:[[591]](#cite_note-620) 5,535 residential units, 1,041 commercial units, 14 medical centres, 65 schools, and 13 centres affiliated with the Iranian Red Crescent Society. The US reportedly used [double tap](/wiki/Double_tap_strike "Double tap strike") airstrikes to maximize casualties.[[592]](#cite_note-621)[[593]](#cite_note-622) The house of the acclaimed filmmaker [Abbas Kiarostami](/wiki/Abbas_Kiarostami "Abbas Kiarostami") was reportedly damaged on 25 March.[[594]](#cite_note-BBC_25Mar2026-623) As of 23 March, HRANA estimated that at least 15% of the total human casualties had been under the age of 18.[[595]](#cite_note-624)

An Israeli airstrike on 9 March in Resalat neighbourhood of Tehran destroyed a Basij-affiliated building as well as three residential buildings, resulting in 40 to 50 deaths. According to a BBC analysis, Israel used [Mark 82](/wiki/Mark_82 "Mark 82") bombs in the attack.[[588]](#cite_note-BBCd4xo-617)

As of 27 March, Iran reports damage to at least 120 [historical sites](/wiki/History_of_Iran "History of Iran") by US-Israeli strikes.[[596]](#cite_note-625)

An analysis by BBC Verify suggest that the deadly attack on residential buildings and a sports hall in the town of [Lamerd](/wiki/Lamerd "Lamerd") had been performed by a [Precision Strike Missile](/wiki/Precision_Strike_Missile "Precision Strike Missile") (PrSM), a state-of-the-art US munition.[[597]](#cite_note-626)

On 2 March 2026, video footage released by Iran International showed the IRGC Malek-Ashtar building in Tehran completely destroyed following a joint US-Israel missile strike on the capital.[[598]](#cite_note-627) In the early hours of 3 March 2026, the complex of the [Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting](/wiki/Islamic_Republic_of_Iran_Broadcasting "Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting") (IRIB), the state broadcaster's headquarters in Tehran, was hit in a separate Israeli air operation.[[599]](#cite_note-628)[[600]](#cite_note-629)

Iranian state-linked media indicated that Iran's parliament building was also targeted by airstrikes.[[601]](#cite_note-630) Trump told reporters that "Just about everything's been knocked out" and that Iran had no navy, air force, air detection, or radar after the attacks.[[602]](#cite_note-Detsch-2026-631) On 3 March, Israel bombed Iran's 84-member [Assembly of Experts](/wiki/Assembly_of_Experts "Assembly of Experts") as [they were in a preliminary meeting to elect the next supreme leader](/wiki/2026_Iranian_supreme_leader_election "2026 Iranian supreme leader election")[[603]](#cite_note-632)[[604]](#cite_note-633) on 8 March.[[605]](#cite_note-634)[[606]](#cite_note-635)

### United States

On 1 March, six US soldiers were killed and more than 30 were injured in an Iranian [drone attack](/wiki/2026_Port_Shuaiba_drone_attack "2026 Port Shuaiba drone attack") against a US military installation near [Camp Arifjan](/wiki/Camp_Arifjan "Camp Arifjan") in Port Shuaiba, [Kuwait](/wiki/Kuwait "Kuwait"). All of those killed were soldiers of the [United States Army Reserve](/wiki/United_States_Army_Reserve "United States Army Reserve") assigned to the [103rd Expeditionary Sustainment Command](/wiki/103rd_Expeditionary_Sustainment_Command "103rd Expeditionary Sustainment Command"), headquartered in [Des Moines, Iowa](/wiki/Des_Moines,_Iowa "Des Moines, Iowa").[[610]](#cite_note-639)[[611]](#cite_note-640)[[612]](#cite_note-641)[[613]](#cite_note-cbs3march-642)[[614]](#cite_note-643) On 4 March, US Marine Corps Lance Corporal Kevin Melendez died in Saudi Arabia in a non-hostile incident.[[615]](#cite_note-RC1-644) On 8 March, CENTCOM reported a National Guard soldier died from a "health-related incident in Kuwait on March 6 during a medical emergency".[[616]](#cite_note-645) Sgt. Benjamin N. Pennington, a soldier of the [1st Space Brigade](/wiki/1st_Space_Brigade "1st Space Brigade") wounded in an attack against [Prince Sultan Air Base](/wiki/Prince_Sultan_Air_Base "Prince Sultan Air Base") in Saudi Arabia on 1 March, died from his injuries on 9 March.[[617]](#cite_note-646)[[618]](#cite_note-647)[[619]](#cite_note-648)

Six American military airmen were killed on 12 March when their KC-135 aerial refueling aircraft crashed in western Iraq while supporting US military operations. CENTCOM said that the crash was not the result of hostile action and that it had resulted during an incident involving another US aircraft inside "friendly airspace". By 13 March, the US military publicly announced that about 140 American servicemen had been injured during the war.[[620]](#cite_note-AP1-649)

The [USS *Gerald R. Ford*](/wiki/USS_Gerald_R._Ford "USS Gerald R. Ford"), the largest aircraft carrier in the theatre, was damaged by a fire that broke out 12 March. The [U.S. Navy](/wiki/U.S._Navy "U.S. Navy") said the fire, which injured multiple[[ad]](#cite_note-Ford-654) sailors, started in a laundry area and was not combat-related.[[625]](#cite_note-655) The carrier had already been dealing with other mechanical issues, including a malfunctioning sewage treatment system, since before it arrived in the Middle East.[[626]](#cite_note-656) According to multiple reports on 17–18 March, *Gerald R. Ford* paused launching airstrikes and sailed toward the [Crete Naval Base](/wiki/Crete_Naval_Base "Crete Naval Base") for repairs.[[622]](#cite_note-usni170326-651)[[624]](#cite_note-guardian180326-653)[[627]](#cite_note-657)

The first connected Iranian attack struck a residential building in Israel, leaving one Israeli civilian injured.[[628]](#cite_note-658) [Magen David Adom](/wiki/Magen_David_Adom "Magen David Adom") reported that Iran's initial attacks had left 89 injured, with three directly wounded and the rest indirectly, most of them civilians.[[629]](#cite_note-659) On 28 February, a direct hit in [Tel Aviv](/wiki/Tel_Aviv "Tel Aviv") killed a civilian woman and injured 22 others, one seriously.[[630]](#cite_note-660) On 1 March, an Iranian strike hit a synagogue and residential buildings in [Beit Shemesh](/wiki/Beit_Shemesh "Beit Shemesh"), killing nine people and injuring 49 others.[[631]](#cite_note-661) On 9 March, two workers were killed in [Yehud](/wiki/Yehud,_Israel "Yehud, Israel"), outside of Tel Aviv, after being struck by submunitions from an Iranian missile equipped with a [cluster bomb](/wiki/Cluster_bomb "Cluster bomb") warhead while working outdoors at a construction site.[[632]](#cite_note-662)[[633]](#cite_note-663)

On 17 March, a cluster munition from the warhead of an Iranian missile hit an apartment building in [Ramat Gan](/wiki/Ramat_Gan "Ramat Gan") and killed two residents in their 70s, who were found just outside their safe room.[[634]](#cite_note-664) A statement from the [Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps](/wiki/Islamic_Revolutionary_Guard_Corps "Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps") called the barrage that killed the two civilians "revenge for the blood of martyr Dr. [Ali Larijani](/wiki/Ali_Larijani "Ali Larijani") and his companions", who had been killed a day before the Iranian attack.[[635]](#cite_note-665) According to [CNN](/wiki/CNN "CNN"), the use of cluster bombs is against international law, with N.R. Jenzen-Jones of [Armament Research Services](/wiki/Armament_Research_Services "Armament Research Services") telling CNN that this type of warhead is being used "primarily to sow terror amongst a civilian population."[[636]](#cite_note-666) The executive director of the [Arms Control Association](/wiki/Arms_Control_Association "Arms Control Association") called the Iranian targeting of cluster munition warheads at residential sections of [Israel](/wiki/Israel "Israel") during the war as deliberate, saying that "Iran appears to be launching them into relatively populated areas, probably with the goal of producing potential civilian harm."[[637]](#cite_note-667) Neither Iran nor Israel is party to the [Convention on Cluster Munitions](/wiki/Convention_on_Cluster_Munitions "Convention on Cluster Munitions"),[[638]](#cite_note-668) and both Israel and Iran are among 17 countries that either produce cluster munitions, or reserve the right to do so.[[639]](#cite_note-669)

Israeli broadcaster [Kan 11](/wiki/Kan_11 "Kan 11") reported that 90, 65, 25, and then 20 missiles were launched at Israel from Iran on each of the first four days of the war.[[640]](#cite_note-670) Although the number of missiles in each volley has decreased, the missiles themselves have increased in size and impact.[[641]](#cite_note-671) On 9 March, Israel said that Lebanon has made more attacks against them than Iran has over the past week.[[642]](#cite_note-672)

### Lebanon

### In Iran

#### Leadership

See also: [2026 Iranian supreme leader election](/wiki/2026_Iranian_supreme_leader_election "2026 Iranian supreme leader election")

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Alireza_Arafi_13990824_0146818_crop.jpg/120px-Alireza_Arafi_13990824_0146818_crop.jpg)](/wiki/File:Alireza_Arafi_13990824_0146818_crop.jpg)

The three members of the [Interim Leadership Council](/wiki/Interim_Leadership_Council "Interim Leadership Council") – President [Masoud Pezeshkian](/wiki/Masoud_Pezeshkian "Masoud Pezeshkian"), Chief Justice [Gholam-Hossein Mohseni-Eje'i](/wiki/Gholam-Hossein_Mohseni-Eje%27i "Gholam-Hossein Mohseni-Eje'i") and Ayatollah [Alireza Arafi](/wiki/Alireza_Arafi "Alireza Arafi")

The death of Ali Khamenei triggered an election for a new supreme leader. Under the terms of Iran's constitution, an [Interim Leadership Council](/wiki/Interim_Leadership_Council "Interim Leadership Council") was established on 1 March to exercise the functions of Iran's [head of state](/wiki/Head_of_state "Head of state") until a new supreme leader is elected.[[269]](#cite_note-Reuters-2026-294)

[Mojtaba Khamenei](/wiki/Mojtaba_Khamenei "Mojtaba Khamenei") was elected on 8 March 2026 to replace his father as supreme leader,[[713]](#cite_note-751) and the IRGC, as well as Iran's top leaders (including [Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf](/wiki/Mohammad_Bagher_Ghalibaf "Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf"), [Ali Larijani](/wiki/Ali_Larijani "Ali Larijani"), and [Masoud Pezeshkian](/wiki/Masoud_Pezeshkian "Masoud Pezeshkian")[[714]](#cite_note-Sabah-2026-752)) pledged their allegiance to him.[[715]](#cite_note-753)

In late March the NYT described Iran's leadership as paralyzed with severely disrupted decision-making process. It also reported that the damage to communications infrastructure caused paranoia and internal power struggles.[[716]](#cite_note-754)

#### Civilian life

See also: [2025–2026 Iranian protests § After the strikes](/wiki/2025%E2%80%932026_Iranian_protests#After_the_strikes "2025–2026 Iranian protests")

As the US and Israel executed joint strikes targeting Iranian military and institutional targets, Iran's capital reportedly became a [ghost town](/wiki/Ghost_town "Ghost town"), as civilians feared going outside or to their daily jobs.[[717]](#cite_note-755) Reports have emerged that prisoners in [Evin Prison](/wiki/Evin_Prison "Evin Prison") have been receiving limited bread and water since the onset of the war.[[718]](#cite_note-756) After the strikes, schools were ordered closed while banks and government offices remained in operation at reduced capacity. Local media reported that subway and bus services would remain operational.[[719]](#cite_note-757)

Amid the renewed "near total" [internet blackout in Iran](/wiki/2026_Internet_blackout_in_Iran "2026 Internet blackout in Iran"), [NetBlocks](/wiki/NetBlocks "NetBlocks") reported that internet connectivity in Iran dropped to 4% of ordinary levels.[[580]](#cite_note-BBC_News-2026b-609)[[720]](#cite_note-758)[[721]](#cite_note-759) While the internet blackout has exceeded 240 hours, making it the second longest ever, the government started handing out "white sim cards", which bypass filters, to government supporters, in order to allow them to promote its messaging.[[722]](#cite_note-760)[[247]](#cite_note-bm-269) As news of Khamenei's death broke, security forces were deployed to prevent an uprising, with footage showing them opening fire on celebrants in the streets[[723]](#cite_note-761)[[724]](#cite_note-762)[[725]](#cite_note-763) and shooting at people chanting behind the windows of their homes.[[726]](#cite_note-764)

During the first two weeks of March 2026, over 70,000 [Afghan refugees](/wiki/Afghans_in_Iran "Afghans in Iran") returned to Afghanistan, frequently under forced circumstances, exacerbating the country's existing [severe humanitarian crisis](/wiki/2025%E2%80%932026_hunger_crisis_in_Afghanistan "2025–2026 hunger crisis in Afghanistan").[[727]](#cite_note-765) Arafat Jamal, the UNHCR Representative in Afghanistan, described returnees as "fleeing one war only to encounter another," noting that they faced limited options amid heightened [border tensions](/wiki/2026_Afghanistan%E2%80%93Pakistan_conflict "2026 Afghanistan–Pakistan conflict") and military confrontations between Afghanistan and Pakistan.[[728]](#cite_note-766)

According to *[The Guardian](/wiki/The_Guardian "The Guardian")*, the war has overshadowed the persecution and executions of political prisoners in Iran, including those involved in the mass protests of January 2026. This has allowed the Iranian regime to continue carrying out death sentences, and other severe punishments with little international attention or scrutiny of these [human rights abuses](/wiki/Human_rights_in_Iran "Human rights in Iran").[[729]](#cite_note-767)

#### Military

On 26 March, it was reported that children as young as 12 years of age may join Iran's war support, leading to concerns of the use of [child soldiers](/wiki/Child_soldiers "Child soldiers").[[730]](#cite_note-768)[[731]](#cite_note-769)[[732]](#cite_note-770) Days later, rights groups stated an 11-year-old was killed while on duty at a Tehran checkpoint.[[733]](#cite_note-771)

On 29 March, footage showed Iraq's [Popular Mobilization Forces](/wiki/Popular_Mobilization_Forces "Popular Mobilization Forces") deployed in Iran.[[734]](#cite_note-772)[[735]](#cite_note-773)[[736]](#cite_note-774)

#### Kurdish–Iranian crisis

Further information: [2026 Kurdish–Iranian crisis](/wiki/2026_Kurdish%E2%80%93Iranian_crisis "2026 Kurdish–Iranian crisis")

According to *[The Wall Street Journal](/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal "The Wall Street Journal")*, Trump had been open to supporting armed militias, particularly [Kurds in Iran](/wiki/Kurds_in_Iran "Kurds in Iran") that have been [in armed conflict with Tehran for decades](/wiki/Kurdish_separatism_in_Iran "Kurdish separatism in Iran")[[332]](#cite_note-WSJ-2026-357) and have historically been a regional ally of the United States.[[737]](#cite_note-775) However, Trump later said he had ruled out sending the Kurds to Iran.[[738]](#cite_note-776)

#### Cultural heritage sites

Several historic and cultural sites, including [UNESCO World Heritage Sites](/wiki/UNESCO_World_Heritage_Sites "UNESCO World Heritage Sites"), were damaged during the war. On 2 March, a strike on [Arg Square](/wiki/Arg_Square "Arg Square") damaged nearby [Golestan Palace](/wiki/Golestan_Palace "Golestan Palace"), prompting [UNESCO](/wiki/UNESCO "UNESCO") to issue a statement of concern.[[739]](#cite_note-777)[[740]](#cite_note-778) On 5 March, the [Azadi Sport Complex](/wiki/Azadi_Sport_Complex "Azadi Sport Complex") was bombed.[[741]](#cite_note-779) An 8 March strike on [Falak-ol-Aflak](/wiki/Falak-ol-Aflak "Falak-ol-Aflak"), which was marked with a [blue shield emblem](/wiki/Hague_Convention_for_the_Protection_of_Cultural_Property_in_the_Event_of_Armed_Conflict "Hague Convention for the Protection of Cultural Property in the Event of Armed Conflict"), damaged several sections of the site.[[742]](#cite_note-ArtNewspaper310-780) Strikes on [Isfahan](/wiki/Isfahan "Isfahan") on 9 March damaged [Naqsh-e Jahan Square](/wiki/Naqsh-e_Jahan_Square "Naqsh-e Jahan Square"), [Chehel Sotoun](/wiki/Chehel_Sotoun "Chehel Sotoun"), [Ali Qapu](/wiki/Ali_Qapu "Ali Qapu"), the [Shah Mosque](/wiki/Shah_Mosque_(Isfahan) "Shah Mosque (Isfahan)"), [Jameh Mosque](/wiki/Jameh_Mosque_of_Isfahan "Jameh Mosque of Isfahan"), and [Teymouri Hall](/wiki/Natural_History_Museum_of_Isfahan "Natural History Museum of Isfahan").[[743]](#cite_note-781)[[742]](#cite_note-ArtNewspaper310-780) On 11 March, UNESCO urged protection for Iran's [heritage sites](/wiki/Heritage_sites "Heritage sites") and World Heritage Sites that have been damaged or are under high risk due to the war, alongside other [historic sites](/wiki/Historic_site "Historic site") in Israel, Lebanon, and across the Middle East.[[744]](#cite_note-782)

### In Israel

Israel declared a nationwide [state of emergency](/wiki/State_of_emergency "State of emergency"), saying that the strikes on Iran were the largest that it had ever launched.[[249]](#cite_note-Liebermann-2026-271)[[745]](#cite_note-783) Israeli schools and workspaces were closed and public gatherings were canceled.[[746]](#cite_note-784)

### In Palestine

See also: [Gaza war](/wiki/Gaza_war "Gaza war")

The Israeli [Coordinator of Government Activities in the Territories](/wiki/Coordinator_of_Government_Activities_in_the_Territories "Coordinator of Government Activities in the Territories") closed multiple aid crossings in the [Gaza Strip](/wiki/Gaza_Strip "Gaza Strip") (specifically interrupting free circulation on the [Egypt–Gaza border](/wiki/Egypt%E2%80%93Gaza_border "Egypt–Gaza border")).[[747]](#cite_note-785) Israeli forces suspended [United Nations](/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations") humanitarian movements and postponed planned rotations of international humanitarian staff (including medical evacuations and the return of people into Gaza), which the [United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs](/wiki/United_Nations_Office_for_the_Coordination_of_Humanitarian_Affairs "United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs") condemned.[[748]](#cite_note-786)

[Israeli settler violence](/wiki/Israeli_settler_violence "Israeli settler violence") against [Palestinians](/wiki/Palestinians "Palestinians") in [occupied West Bank](/wiki/Occupied_West_Bank "Occupied West Bank") has increased; settlers have taken advantage of curbs on movement imposed during the renewed [Israeli state of emergency](/wiki/Israeli_state_of_emergency "Israeli state of emergency"). The renewed blocking of West Bank roads (in the context of the [2026 Lebanon war](/wiki/2026_Lebanon_war "2026 Lebanon war")) has delayed medics from assisting injured Palestinians.[[749]](#cite_note-787) The EU stated on 10 March that the Israeli actions were unacceptable and urged the Israeli authorities to make immediate efforts to prevent the violence against Palestinian civilians under [international law](/wiki/International_law "International law"), warning that "impunity for such acts risks provoking further violence".[[750]](#cite_note-788)

### In Gulf states

The [Arab states of the Persian Gulf](/wiki/Arab_states_of_the_Persian_Gulf "Arab states of the Persian Gulf") started a campaign of prosecution against civilians and foreign citizens that shared or reposted rumours from unverified sources about the Iranian attacks, with the stated main goal to curb misinformation that could harm public order.[[751]](#cite_note-789) Bahrain's Cyber Crime Directorate also started a campaign of arresting civilians in the country, which has a large [Shiite minority](/wiki/Shia_in_Bahrain "Shia in Bahrain"), who allegedly expressed "their support for the Iranian shelling targeting the Kingdom of Bahrain", describing such activities as treason and as promotion of hostile acts against the state.[[752]](#cite_note-790) Trump said "Even if we knew Gulf countries would be hit, big deal we did what we have to do".[[653]](#cite_note-UrasEtAl-683)

The war has global economic consequences: The conflict led to immediate surges in [oil](/wiki/Price_of_oil "Price of oil") and [gas prices](/wiki/Natural_gas_prices "Natural gas prices"), widespread disruptions in aviation and tourism, and heightened volatility in financial markets.[[753]](#cite_note-Oxford_Economics-2026-791)[[754]](#cite_note-Reuters-792) The head of [International Energy Agency](/wiki/International_Energy_Agency "International Energy Agency") (IEA) described the situation caused by the war as the "greatest global energy security challenge in history".[[755]](#cite_note-793)

#### Disruption of international trade

Analysts projected potential global inflation increases and risks of recession if disruptions persisted, particularly through [closures](/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis "2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis") of key shipping routes like the [Strait of Hormuz](/wiki/Strait_of_Hormuz "Strait of Hormuz") and strikes on gas and oil facilities.[[753]](#cite_note-Oxford_Economics-2026-791)[[754]](#cite_note-Reuters-792) Oil prices rose following a reduction in traffic through the strait, and strikes on oil facilities in countries such as Saudi Arabia and the UAE.[[756]](#cite_note-794) Oil prices went over US$100 per barrel[[647]](#cite_note-AP_News-677) to $114 per barrel,[[647]](#cite_note-AP_News-677) the highest prices since the [COVID-19 pandemic](/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic"). An expert warned that if this continues, this could lead into a worldwide [economic recession](/wiki/Economic_recession "Economic recession").[[757]](#cite_note-795) Gas prices also rose,[[292]](#cite_note-bloomberg4march-317) and analysts said raising the world's prices was a part of the Iranian government's plan to apply pressure on the world to stop the war.[[758]](#cite_note-796) Toward the end of March there were significant shortages of domestic [petroleum products](/wiki/Petroleum_product "Petroleum product") in [southeast Asia](/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia").[[759]](#cite_note-bbc-20260324-797)

The guided-missile destroyer [USS *Bainbridge*](/wiki/USS_Bainbridge_(DDG-96) "USS Bainbridge (DDG-96)") passes through the [Suez Canal](/wiki/Suez_Canal "Suez Canal") during Operation Epic Fury, March 2026

On 6 March, Qatar's energy minister [Saad al-Kaabi](/wiki/Saad_al-Kaabi "Saad al-Kaabi") warned that if the war continues other Gulf energy producers may be forced to halt exports and declare [force majeure](/wiki/Force_majeure "Force majeure"), and that "this will bring down economies of the world".[[760]](#cite_note-798) Stock markets experienced declines, with the [Dow Jones Industrial Average](/wiki/Dow_Jones_Industrial_Average "Dow Jones Industrial Average") falling over 400 points (one percent) on 2 March.[[761]](#cite_note-799) Broader economic forecasts warned of inflationary pressures and slowed global growth if the conflict prolonged.[[762]](#cite_note-800)

The UN [World Food Programme](/wiki/World_Food_Programme "World Food Programme") and various economic analysts have warned that the war is driving significant, long-term increases in global [food prices](/wiki/Food_prices "Food prices").[[763]](#cite_note-801)[[764]](#cite_note-802) A near-total halt of tanker traffic in the Strait of Hormuz has disrupted the supply of fuel and essential [fertilizers](/wiki/Fertilizer "Fertilizer"), threatening global [food security](/wiki/Food_security "Food security") and echoing the [2022 food crisis](/wiki/2022%E2%80%932023_global_food_crises "2022–2023 global food crises").[[765]](#cite_note-803)[[766]](#cite_note-804) The Fertilizer Institute (TFI) stated that nearly 50% of global [urea](/wiki/Urea "Urea") and [sulfur](/wiki/Sulfur "Sulfur") exports, as well as 20% of global liquefied natural gas (LNG)—a key feedstock for [nitrogen fertilizers](/wiki/Nitrogen_fertilizer "Nitrogen fertilizer")—transit through the Strait of Hormuz.[[767]](#cite_note-805)

#### Airspace closures

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Grounded_airliners_at_Manchester_Airport_Feb_2026.jpg/250px-Grounded_airliners_at_Manchester_Airport_Feb_2026.jpg)](/wiki/File:Grounded_airliners_at_Manchester_Airport_Feb_2026.jpg)

Iran's airspace was largely empty of civilian aircraft following the strikes as regional states closed airspace.[[768]](#cite_note-806)[[769]](#cite_note-807) Bahrain, Iraq, Israel, Kuwait, Qatar, Syria and the UAE closed their respective airspace following the attacks, with multiple [airliners](/wiki/Airliner "Airliner") being redirected to other destinations.[[770]](#cite_note-808)[[771]](#cite_note-809)[[772]](#cite_note-810) Some regional-based and International airlines suspended services to the Middle East in view of the war.[[773]](#cite_note-811) According to a *[Wirtschaftswoche](/wiki/Wirtschaftswoche "Wirtschaftswoche")* analysis, prolonging the conflict would mean a "catastrophe" for [Gulf states](/wiki/Arab_states_of_the_Persian_Gulf "Arab states of the Persian Gulf") such as Qatar and the UAE.[[774]](#cite_note-812)

#### Strait of Hormuz

Main article: [2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis](/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis "2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis")

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Hormuz_map.png/250px-Hormuz_map.png)](/wiki/File:Hormuz_map.png)

The [Strait of Hormuz](/wiki/Strait_of_Hormuz "Strait of Hormuz") highlighted by a red box, at the entrance to the Persian Gulf

The current soft closure in Hormuz, alongside the fears of a declared blockade, has caused an increase in [financial risk](/wiki/Financial_risk "Financial risk") due to the impact in the prices for energy, shipping, insurance, aviation, etc. The war has provoked [speculation](/wiki/Speculation "Speculation") in the [world economy](/wiki/World_economy "World economy"), caused by the possibility of prolonged supply disruption, which will have broader consequences for [inflation](/wiki/Inflation "Inflation"), currencies, and emerging markets. One-fifth of global petroleum liquids consumption and one-quarter of global seaborne oil trade is done by the extraction of oil in the [Persian Gulf Basin](/wiki/Persian_Gulf_Basin "Persian Gulf Basin") and its passage through the [Strait of Hormuz](/wiki/Strait_of_Hormuz "Strait of Hormuz").[[775]](#cite_note-813) Mojtaba Khamenei, the new Supreme Leader, said the Strait of Hormuz would continue to be closed to pressure the US.[[776]](#cite_note-Trew-814) However, Iran said that ships can pass through the Hormuz as long as they cooperate with the [Iranian navy](/wiki/Iranian_navy "Iranian navy").[[776]](#cite_note-Trew-814) Araghchi said the Hormuz would be open overall but closed to Iran's enemies.[[653]](#cite_note-UrasEtAl-683)

#### Strait of Bab-el-Mandeb

See also: [Red Sea crisis](/wiki/Red_Sea_crisis "Red Sea crisis")

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/2023_Israel%E2%80%93Hamas_war_-_Bab-el-Mandeb.svg/250px-2023_Israel%E2%80%93Hamas_war_-_Bab-el-Mandeb.svg.png)](/wiki/File:2023_Israel%E2%80%93Hamas_war_-_Bab-el-Mandeb.svg)

Map of [Houthi attacks on commercial ships](/wiki/Houthi_attacks_on_commercial_vessels "Houthi attacks on commercial vessels") from various countries in the [Bab el-Mandeb](/wiki/Bab_el-Mandeb "Bab el-Mandeb") Strait as of December 2023

[Houthi-controlled Yemen](/wiki/Houthi-controlled_Yemen "Houthi-controlled Yemen") restarted its threats in the [Gulf of Aden](/wiki/Gulf_of_Aden "Gulf of Aden") and [Red Sea](/wiki/Red_Sea "Red Sea"), which had been suspended since 10 October 2025 after the [Gaza peace plan](/wiki/Gaza_peace_plan "Gaza peace plan") proposal.[[777]](#cite_note-815) Some security sources claim that Houthis are planning to do incursions in the [Arabian Sea](/wiki/Arabian_Sea "Arabian Sea") and also use bases from the African side of the Red Sea, helped by their presence and some cells established in [East Africa](/wiki/East_Africa "East Africa").[[778]](#cite_note-816)

As of 4 March, hostilities had not resumed as there was reportedly internal debate inside the group over its response.[[779]](#cite_note-817) On 5 March, Houthi leader [Abdul-Malik al-Houthi](/wiki/Abdul-Malik_al-Houthi "Abdul-Malik al-Houthi") said in a recorded address that his group stood with Iran in the ongoing war and was prepared to take action if developments required it, stating, "Our hands are on the trigger whenever developments require it", and warning that the group remains ready to intervene if the situation escalates further.[[780]](#cite_note-818)[[781]](#cite_note-819)

By 7 March, the Houthis had released only three declarations, with a tone characterized as more disciplined and subdued this time, unlike its usual [bellicose](/wiki/Bellicose "Bellicose") approach on other conflicts of the [Middle Eastern crisis](/wiki/Middle_Eastern_crisis_(2023%E2%80%93present) "Middle Eastern crisis (2023–present)"), seeming more as political and emotional statements in favour of Iran rather than practical and military solidarity. Such pragmatist attitude has been influenced by the [2025 United States–Houthi ceasefire](/wiki/2025_United_States%E2%80%93Houthi_ceasefire "2025 United States–Houthi ceasefire"), not wanting to open multiple fronts in the current [Yemeni civil war](/wiki/Yemeni_civil_war_(2014%E2%80%93present) "Yemeni civil war (2014–present)"), which has been waging since 2014 and to focus on ending the conflict with [Saudi Arabia](/wiki/Saudi-led_intervention_in_the_Yemeni_civil_war "Saudi-led intervention in the Yemeni civil war").[[782]](#cite_note-820)

On 15 March, the *[Associated Press](/wiki/Associated_Press "Associated Press")* reported that the weapons stockpile of the Houthis is running low after repeated attacks on Israel during the [Gaza war](/wiki/Gaza_war "Gaza war"), while Iran's ongoing conflict has further disrupted weapons supplies, though the group retains a significant stockpile of drones. Despite these limitations, the Houthis have been recruiting fighters, expanding local weapons production, and reinforcing positions along the Red Sea coast, including the port city of [Hodeidah](/wiki/Hodeidah "Hodeidah"), signaling potential escalation. Unlike Hezbollah's immediate intervention, the Houthis appear to act according to Iran's gradual deployment strategy, likely intervening only if the conflict widens or if Iran faces a serious threat to its military capabilities.[[783]](#cite_note-821)[[784]](#cite_note-822)

On 16 March, *[The Times](/wiki/The_Times "The Times")* reported that the Houthis are waiting for an approval from Iran to resume their attacks should Iran's control of the Strait of Hormuz be weakened by the US. Recent statistics indicate that nearly 30 oil tankers near the Saudi port city of [Yanbu](/wiki/Yanbu "Yanbu") are currently within the Houthis' attack range.[[785]](#cite_note-823) On 20 March, senior Houthi politburo member Mohammed al-Bukhaiti declared that "All options at the military level are possible"[[786]](#cite_note-824)[*[better source needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Questionable_sources "Wikipedia:Verifiability")*] and mentioned that the group was considering a [naval blockade](/wiki/Naval_blockade "Naval blockade") and would specifically target vessels belonging to "aggressor countries" involved in military actions against "Iran, Lebanon, Palestine and Iraq", to support Iran's ongoing "confrontation with the United States and Israel."[[787]](#cite_note-825)

#### Internet infrastructure

Further information: [Cyberwarfare during the 2026 Iran war](/wiki/Cyberwarfare_during_the_2026_Iran_war "Cyberwarfare during the 2026 Iran war")

Three [Amazon Web Services](/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services "Amazon Web Services") data centers in the United Arab Emirates were struck and damaged as a result of [drone strikes](/wiki/Drone_strikes "Drone strikes"), leading to outages of web infrastructure within the Middle East,[[788]](#cite_note-826) causing "sparks and fire" as well as "major structural damage" for 2 of the data centers, with internal water levels reaching up to 4 cm, alongside the mechanical failure of cooling and air systems.[[789]](#cite_note-Kim-2026-827) As the targeted data centers within the United Arab Emirates were in different [availability zones](/wiki/Availability_zone "Availability zone"), and [Amazon](/wiki/Amazon_(company) "Amazon (company)") infrastructure was only designed to withstand redundancy of a single zone,[[790]](#cite_note-828) fundamental web infrastructure including [S3 storage](/wiki/Amazon_S3 "Amazon S3"), [EC2 compute](/wiki/Amazon_Elastic_Compute_Cloud "Amazon Elastic Compute Cloud") and [DynamoDB databases](/wiki/Amazon_DynamoDB "Amazon DynamoDB") suffered complete outages within the Middle Eastern region.[[789]](#cite_note-Kim-2026-827)

#### Military costs

On 2 March, four days into the war, the [Center for American Progress](/wiki/Center_for_American_Progress "Center for American Progress") already estimated the cost to the US at $5 billion.[[791]](#cite_note-829) Analysts for CNN said that the cost was at least US$890 million per day.[[792]](#cite_note-830)
The first 6 days of the offensive cost the United States US$12.7 billion the [Center for Strategic and International Studies](/wiki/Center_for_Strategic_and_International_Studies "Center for Strategic and International Studies") (CSIS) estimated.[[132]](#cite_note-guardianCost-150) with munitions alone costing at least US$11.3 billion, per the Pentagon.[[793]](#cite_note-usanews-831) By 19 March, twenty-one days into the war, *[The Guardian](/wiki/The_Guardian "The Guardian")* extrapolated this cost to likely be over US$18 billion.[[132]](#cite_note-guardianCost-150) The same day, the Pentagon requested US$200 billion to fund the war.[[133]](#cite_note-i598-151)

On 11 March, *[The New York Times](/wiki/The_New_York_Times "The New York Times")* reported that Iranian attacks caused damage to at least 17 US sites in the Middle East.[[794]](#cite_note-832) In comparison, the monetary costs of Iranian attacks have been much lower than the costs of neutralizing them: PATRIOT and THAAD interceptors are US$4 million and US$12 million, respectively, as opposed to US$50,000 for a Shahed drone.[[408]](#cite_note-ndtv10march-434)

Further information: [Reactions to the 2026 Iran war](/wiki/Reactions_to_the_2026_Iran_war "Reactions to the 2026 Iran war") and [Media coverage of the 2026 Iran war](/wiki/Media_coverage_of_the_2026_Iran_war "Media coverage of the 2026 Iran war")

### Iran

#### Government

The [Supreme National Security Council](/wiki/Supreme_National_Security_Council "Supreme National Security Council") said Iran was targeted by a "brutal air operation" carried out by the US and Israel, saying: "This occurred once again during negotiations, and the enemy imagines that the resilient Iranian nation will surrender to their petty demands through these cowardly actions."[[679]](#cite_note-Mohamed-716) [Abbas Araghchi](/wiki/Abbas_Araghchi "Abbas Araghchi") called the attacks "wholly unprovoked, illegal, and illegitimate".[[795]](#cite_note-833) Iranian officials have rejected Trump's attempt to insert himself into the government's succession plans.[[796]](#cite_note-834) Pezeshkian said that US should take their unconditional surrender demand "to their grave".[[797]](#cite_note-The_Indian_Express-2026-835)

Pezeshkian apologized for the attacks on some neighboring nations, stating that Tehran would suspend them until an attack originated from there and attributed the strikes to "miscommunication in the ranks".[[797]](#cite_note-The_Indian_Express-2026-835)[[376]](#cite_note-Al_Jazeera-2-402) At a later date, Pezeshkian said that Iran has no choice but to target other Muslim countries if their territory is being used to conduct the war against Iran.[[798]](#cite_note-836) On 15 March, the Revolutionary Guard said that it would try to assassinate Netanyahu.[[799]](#cite_note-837)

On 19 March, [Esmail Baghaei](/wiki/Esmail_Baghaei "Esmail Baghaei"), the [Spokesperson for the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Iran](/wiki/Spokesperson_for_the_Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_of_Iran "Spokesperson for the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Iran"), accused the US and Israel of "terrorist acts" by assassinating top figures in Iran in an "illegal war". He said that Australia's military assets in the Gulf were legitimate targets for Iran's self-defence, after the [Australian Government](/wiki/Australian_Government "Australian Government") had deployed a [surveillance aircraft](/wiki/Surveillance_aircraft "Surveillance aircraft") along with personnel and defensive [air-to-air missiles](/wiki/Air-to-air_missile "Air-to-air missile") to the UAE.[[800]](#cite_note-abcint-838)[[801]](#cite_note-839) On 20 March, [Abolfazl Shekarchi](/wiki/Abolfazl_Shekarchi "Abolfazl Shekarchi") issued an escalatory warning aimed at U.S. and Israeli military and political personnel involved in the strike against Iran. In a statement released via state media, he claimed Iran was tracking the movements of "officials, commanders, and pilots" beyond active conflict zones and warned that public and recreational locations would no longer be safe for them. Analysts interpreted this as a signal of Iran's intent to expand asymmetric "shadow war" tactics beyond the battlefield.[[802]](#cite_note-840) Iran denied targeting Turkey and Oman, saying that they were "Israeli false flag" attacks.[[101]](#cite_note-Khamenei-119)

#### Civilians

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Funeral_of_the_martyrs_of_Minab_Primary_School_for_Girls_6.jpg/250px-Funeral_of_the_martyrs_of_Minab_Primary_School_for_Girls_6.jpg)](/wiki/File:Funeral_of_the_martyrs_of_Minab_Primary_School_for_Girls_6.jpg)

Funeral procession for victims of the [airstrike on the Minab girls' school](/wiki/2026_Minab_school_airstrike "2026 Minab school airstrike"), Iran, 3 March 2026

A holistic picture of civilian reactions in Iran is difficult to obtain due to the [government's control over the circulation of information](/wiki/Censorship_in_Iran "Censorship in Iran"), the [renewed internet blackout](/wiki/2026_Internet_blackout_in_Iran#Renewed_blackout "2026 Internet blackout in Iran"), and the ongoing heated nature of the war.[[803]](#cite_note-841) In the initial aftermath of the strikes, there was evidence of both celebration and mourning at the killing of Iranian leadership.[[804]](#cite_note-Bloomberg_News-2026-842) Photos and videos showed people inside Iran celebrating the attacks, hopeful that they will bring forth an end to the ruling government,[[805]](#cite_note-843)[[806]](#cite_note-844) as well as pro-government rallies with participants waving the [Islamic Republic flag](/wiki/Flag_of_Iran "Flag of Iran") and holding portraits of Ali Khamenei, while demonstrating against the US and Israeli attack.[[807]](#cite_note-845)[[808]](#cite_note-846)[[809]](#cite_note-847) In multiple instances, videos showed Iranians conducting protests against the government from the windows of their homes.[[810]](#cite_note-848)[[811]](#cite_note-849) There was no immediate evidence of an uprising against the government.[[804]](#cite_note-Bloomberg_News-2026-842)

During the 17 March [Chaharshanbe Suri](/wiki/Chaharshanbe_Suri "Chaharshanbe Suri") festivities ahead of the [Nowruz](/wiki/Nowruz "Nowruz") holiday, many Iranians defied orders from the authorities and celebrated in the streets, with footage showing security forces firing gunshots to disperse crowds.[[812]](#cite_note-850)[[813]](#cite_note-851) Civilians also expressed fear, grief and anger at the US–Israeli strikes, and at civilian casualties caused by them.[[814]](#cite_note-852)[[815]](#cite_note-853) Some civilians who had opposed the Islamic Republic and supported regime change later recoiled at the prolonged and violent nature of the war. An Iranian sociologist said that the war was causing a [rally around the flag effect](/wiki/Rally_around_the_flag_effect "Rally around the flag effect").[[816]](#cite_note-Bozorgmehr-854) Trump's vow on 1 April to send Iran "back to the Stone Age", which opposed the initial [Make Iran Great Again](/wiki/Make_Iran_Great_Again "Make Iran Great Again") and the vows to the [Iranian perotrstors](/wiki/2026_Iranian_protests "2026 Iranian protests") that "help is on the way", met with strong reactions among Iranians[[817]](#cite_note-855) and analysts.[[818]](#cite_note-856)

[![](//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Iran_pro-Pahlavi_march_in_New_York_City_3%2C_March_1_2026.jpg/250px-Iran_pro-Pahlavi_march_in_New_York_City_3%2C_March_1_2026.jpg)](/wiki/File:Iran_pro-Pahlavi_march_in_New_York_City_3,_March_1_2026.jpg)

Iranian diaspora in the US celebrated the death of Khamenei in [New York City](/wiki/New_York_City "New York City") on 1 March 2026.[[819]](#cite_note-857) Many fly variants of the [Lion and Sun flag](/wiki/Lion_and_Sun_flag "Lion and Sun flag").

Following the US–Israeli airstrikes on Iran, some[*[who?](/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions "Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch")*] of the [Iranian diaspora](/wiki/Iranian_diaspora "Iranian diaspora") showed support for regime change in Iran[[820]](#cite_note-wi-858) and framed the war as a liberation rather than an invasion.[[821]](#cite_note-st-859) [Celebratory rallies](/wiki/2026_Iranian_diaspora_protests#Following_the_Israeli–United_States_strikes_on_Iran "2026 Iranian diaspora protests") were held worldwide by many members of the Iranian diaspora, where Iranians gathered waving anti–Islamic Republic symbols, including the [Lion and Sun flag](/wiki/Lion_and_Sun_flag "Lion and Sun flag").[[822]](#cite_note-860)[[823]](#cite_note-861)[[824]](#cite_note-862)

[Reza Pahlavi](/wiki/Reza_Pahlavi "Reza Pahlavi"), the most prominent leader of the [opposition](/wiki/Iranian_opposition "Iranian opposition") and son of the former [Shah of Iran](/wiki/Shah_of_Iran "Shah of Iran"), expressed support for strikes targeting government and military sites, while also urging that civilians not be harmed. He called on members of Iran's military and security forces to stand with the public rather than the ruling authorities, and urged Iranians to prepare for the resumption of the [2025–2026 Iranian protests](/wiki/2025%E2%80%932026_Iranian_protests "2025–2026 Iranian protests") as the Islamic Republic was "collapsing".[[825]](#cite_note-pahlavi1march-863) He stated that he is prepared for [his proposed transitional government](/wiki/Iran_Prosperity_Project "Iran Prosperity Project") to take over should the Islamic Republic be overthrown.[[826]](#cite_note-864)[[827]](#cite_note-865)

Following the death of Khamenei, he said in an interview with the program *[60 Minutes](/wiki/60_Minutes "60 Minutes")* that Iranians should be ready to take to the streets at an appropriate moment to advance political change. Pahlavi also stated that he did not intend to seek political office or restore [the monarchy](/wiki/Monarchism_in_Iran "Monarchism in Iran"), but aimed instead to help guide Iran through a peaceful transition to a democratic system.[[825]](#cite_note-pahlavi1march-863) Kurdish Iranian dissidents in Iraq denied any attack plans, but said they would support a US invasion of Iran.[[828]](#cite_note-866) On 7 March, an Iranian Kurd leader in Iraq said that ground operation into Iran is "highly likely".[[829]](#cite_note-867)

On 3 March, Trump said on Truth Social: "Their air defense, Air Force, Navy, and Leadership is gone. They want to talk. [Opinion polling](/wiki/Opinion_polling "Opinion polling") showed that most Americans were opposed to US military action against Iran.[[840]](#cite_note-abc-polls-iran-878) *[The Guardian](/wiki/The_Guardian "The Guardian")* found 27% of Americans were supportive of the US operation while 43% were opposed and 29% were uncertain.[[841]](#cite_note-879) NBC found 52% of registered voters were against military action, while 41% supported it.[[842]](#cite_note-880) Most Americans believed that Trump had not explained the goals behind attacking Iran.[[613]](#cite_note-cbs3march-642) Polls showed splits on the issue along political and demographic lines. #### Politicians

Some members of the US congress called for a vote on the [War Powers Resolution](/wiki/War_Powers_Resolution "War Powers Resolution"), also known as the War Powers Act,[[846]](#cite_note-884) that some other members asserted could have effectively ended the attack on Iran.[[847]](#cite_note-885) The resolution failed,[[848]](#cite_note-886) mainly due to Republican opposition.[[849]](#cite_note-887) Most House Democrats and two Republicans[[am]](#cite_note-889) supported the Resolution, arguing that the military actions go beyond the president's authority to make war.[[851]](#cite_note-Lillis-2026-890) Most Republicans and four Democrats[[an]](#cite_note-892) voted down the Resolution, believing the War Powers Resolution empowers the president to engage in limited armed conflict.[[851]](#cite_note-Lillis-2026-890) A similar resolution was voted down in the Senate, mostly along party lines.[[853]](#cite_note-893) National Counterterrorism Center Director [Joe Kent](/wiki/Joe_Kent "Joe Kent") resigned in March 2026, stating his belief that Iran posed no imminent threat and the war was started due to the [Israel lobby in the United States](/wiki/Israel_lobby_in_the_United_States "Israel lobby in the United States").[[854]](#cite_note-894) As of 19 March, the Pentagon requested an additional $200 billion in funding from the White House; while Republican lawmakers like [Ken Calvert](/wiki/Ken_Calvert "Ken Calvert") voiced support, and Democrats like [Betty McCollum](/wiki/Betty_McCollum "Betty McCollum") demanded to see a 2026 general budget first.[[855]](#cite_note-ap-895) Representative [Thomas Massie](/wiki/Thomas_Massie "Thomas Massie") and former Representative [Marjorie Taylor Greene](/wiki/Marjorie_Taylor_Greene "Marjorie Taylor Greene") argued Trump started the war as a distraction from the [Epstein files](/wiki/Epstein_files "Epstein files").[[856]](#cite_note-896)

### Israel

Netanyahu said the goal of the strikes was to "remove the existential threat posed by the terrorist regime in Iran", stating that "our joint action will create the conditions for the brave Iranian people to take their destiny into their own hands."[[859]](#cite_note-899) Foreign Minister [Gideon Sa'ar](/wiki/Gideon_Sa%27ar "Gideon Sa'ar") said that military action against Iran was urgently needed "despite the significant risks involved", adding that "delay would have allowed the Iranian regime to reach a level of immunity for its nuclear programme, as well as to engage in the mass production of long-range ballistic missiles".[[860]](#cite_note-900) Minister of Science and Technology [Gila Gamliel](/wiki/Gila_Gamliel "Gila Gamliel") said that [2026 Israeli legislative election](/wiki/2026_Israeli_legislative_election "2026 Israeli legislative election") will likely be brought forward to late June or July to allow Netanyahu's bloc to leverage the war.[[861]](#cite_note-901) Opposition leaders supported the strikes.[[862]](#cite_note-902)

On 4 March 2026, Defense Minister Israel Katz disclosed that Israel had initially planned to strike Iran in mid-2026.[[863]](#cite_note-903) On 5 March 2026, Katz said that Netanyahu set an aim of assassinating Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei in November 2025.[[864]](#cite_note-904) On 18 March 2026, Katz said that Netanyahu and he authorized the military to assassinate any senior Iranian official with no further approval.[[865]](#cite_note-905) [Mossad](/wiki/Mossad "Mossad") Chief [David Barnea](/wiki/David_Barnea "David Barnea") told Netanyahu that Mossad could spark protests against the Iranian government at the beginning of the war.[[866]](#cite_note-906) A March 2026 survey by the [Israel Democracy Institute](/wiki/Israel_Democracy_Institute "Israel Democracy Institute") (IDI) found that 82% of the Israeli public supported ongoing military operations, including 93% of [Jewish Israelis](/wiki/Jewish_Israelis "Jewish Israelis") and 26% of [Arab Israelis](/wiki/Arab_Israelis "Arab Israelis").[[867]](#cite_note-907)

### Legality

#### United States law

Many legal and military experts said that US actions in Iran are a type that require congressional authorization. They also noted the frequent use of the word "war" in Trump administration communication about the strikes, adding further credibility to the claim that the Trump administration had bypassed congressional war powers. Rubio said that the Trump administration had followed the [War Powers Resolution](/wiki/War_Powers_Resolution "War Powers Resolution"), but questioned whether it was legally binding.[[918]](#cite_note-rl-958) Multiple American presidents and administrations have launched wars or military operations without congressional approval,[[919]](#cite_note-959) however legal experts have argued that Trump's actions in Iran can be distinguished from prior non-authorized uses of force because the "speculative pre-emptive" justification for this war is comparatively weak when measured against past domestic and international legal rationales.[[920]](#cite_note-960)

#### International law

See also: [United States war crimes § 2026 Iran war](/wiki/United_States_war_crimes#2026_Iran_war "United States war crimes"), and [Israeli war crimes](/wiki/Israeli_war_crimes "Israeli war crimes")

The 2026 US-Israel attacks on Iran are widely considered illegal under international law, as they violate the [UN Charter](/wiki/UN_Charter "UN Charter")'s prohibition on the [use of force](/wiki/Use_of_force_in_international_law "Use of force in international law"), because the attacks fail to meet the criteria for [self-defense](/wiki/Self-defence_in_international_law "Self-defence in international law") and lack authorization from the [United Nations Security Council](/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council "United Nations Security Council").[[921]](#cite_note-sbs1march-961)[[127]](#cite_note-guardian2march-145) There is no recognized legal basis for preventive war or forcible [regime change](/wiki/United_States_involvement_in_regime_change#2026:_Iran "United States involvement in regime change").[[922]](#cite_note-962) The attacks have been, therefore, considered a [war of aggression](/wiki/War_of_aggression "War of aggression"),[[923]](#cite_note-963)[[924]](#cite_note-964)[[925]](#cite_note-965) which is the supreme [war crime](/wiki/War_crime "War crime").[[926]](#cite_note-judgment-966)[[927]](#cite_note-967)

[Ben Saul](/wiki/Ben_Saul "Ben Saul"), United Nations special rapporteur on Human Rights and Counter-terrorism, stated that Iran had not enriched uranium to the point of building a nuclear device, that experts agreed Iran did not possess a nuclear weapon, and that the case was nowhere close to being self-defense against an imminent attack.[[921]](#cite_note-sbs1march-961) Don Rothwell, a professor of international law at the [Australian National University](/wiki/Australian_National_University "Australian National University"), stated that the UN Security council did not pass a resolution to attack Iran and had no legal basis for justifying use of force.[[921]](#cite_note-sbs1march-961) The *Law Society Journal* noted that striking Iran during active negotiations was against the principles of good faith outlined in Article 2(2) of the UN Charter.[[928]](#cite_note-968) According to *[The Guardian](/wiki/The_Guardian "The Guardian")*, under international law Iran has the right to attack US and Israeli military targets after their attacks on Iran, but Iranian attacks against civilians and against countries not party to the conflict are illegal.[[127]](#cite_note-guardian2march-145) [The blocking of the strait of Hormuz](/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis "2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis") by Iran was described as a violation of the [UN Convention on the Law of the Sea](/wiki/UN_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea "UN Convention on the Law of the Sea").[[929]](#cite_note-969)

#### Jus in Bello

The placing, by the U.S., of U.S. military personnel in hotels in densely populated areas in the Gulf States during in the war appear to violate the U.S. military's own laws of war. "It is unconscionable that U.S. forces would knowingly put civilians at risk by leaving their bases and moving to hotels in the densely populated city centers," stated a crisis researcher at [Amnesty International](/wiki/Amnesty_International "Amnesty International"), a leading international human rights organization. The U.S. Law of War Manual and the [Protocol I to the Geneva Conventions](/wiki/Protocol_I_to_the_Geneva_Conventions "Protocol I to the Geneva Conventions") require that military personnel not be stationed amid civilian areas.[[930]](#cite_note-970)

### Geopolitical implications

For broader coverage of this topic, see [American expansionism under Donald Trump](/wiki/American_expansionism_under_Donald_Trump "American expansionism under Donald Trump").

Numerous critics and analysts identified the US-Israeli attack with a more open embrace of [expansionism](/wiki/American_expansionism_under_Donald_Trump "American expansionism under Donald Trump")[[931]](#cite_note-971) and [imperialism by the Trump administration](/wiki/American_imperialism#Trump_administration "American imperialism").[[932]](#cite_note-972)[[933]](#cite_note-Martin-2026-973) The US–Israeli attack is widely considered a "[war of choice](/wiki/War_of_aggression "War of aggression")".[[934]](#cite_note-974) A number of US and Israeli officials, including Hegseth,[[935]](#cite_note-975) have made [religious justifications for the war](/wiki/Religious_justifications_for_the_2026_Iran_war "Religious justifications for the 2026 Iran war").[[936]](#cite_note-976)[[937]](#cite_note-977) According to some analysts, the war has been a setback for Russian influence in the region. However, the analysts also noted that it may distract the [United States](/wiki/United_States "United States") from the [war in Ukraine](/wiki/Russo-Ukrainian_war_(2022%E2%80%93present) "Russo-Ukrainian war (2022–present)").[[938]](#cite_note-978)

The war has been described as having various benefits and drawbacks for Chinese geopolitical interests. The [BBC](/wiki/BBC "BBC") reported that the closure of the [Strait of Hormuz](/wiki/Strait_of_Hormuz "Strait of Hormuz") and the war in Iran may weaken Chinese interests in countries that rely on Gulf countries' oil.[[939]](#cite_note-ub-979) US aggression in Iran played into Chinese rhetoric that frames China as "a pillar of stability in contrast to an unpredictable America." Analysts noted that China has interests both in Iran and in the Gulf states, and that Gulf states may seek closer ties with China after facing attacks from Iran because of their US-alignment. However, analysts also pointed to the loss of discounted sanctioned oil and the inflationary impact of oil scarcity as potential economic drawbacks to the war.[[940]](#cite_note-980)

[Gilbert Achcar](/wiki/Gilbert_Achcar "Gilbert Achcar") said that the US was practicing a modernized version of [gunboat diplomacy](/wiki/Gunboat_diplomacy "Gunboat diplomacy").[[941]](#cite_note-981) Gokay and Hamourtziadou connected the attack on Iran with a longstanding pattern of US interventions in the region. They also connected the attack on Iran in the midst of negotiations to the history of United States treaty-breaking during [westward expansion](/wiki/Westward_expansion "Westward expansion").[[942]](#cite_note-Hamourtziadou-2026-982) Saeed Shah wrote that many in the [Global South](/wiki/Global_South "Global South") do not view the war as having the moral purpose that the US and many of its allies asserted, and that they instead see the war as a failure of diplomacy, and an act of aggression against a weaker nation. International relations experts said that the erosion of international law was making nations in the Global South feel insecure, and more likely to support a coalition against US interests.[[943]](#cite_note-983)

An analysis from [Iran International](/wiki/Iran_International "Iran International") suggests that [Saudi Arabia](/wiki/Saudi_Arabia "Saudi Arabia") and the [United Arab Emirates](/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates "United Arab Emirates") may be positioning themselves for a more active role in fighting against Iran.[[944]](#cite_note-984) The quicker Iranian response relative to that of the [Twelve-Day War](/wiki/Twelve-Day_War "Twelve-Day War") suggests a change in Iran's command structure.[[945]](#cite_note-985)[[946]](#cite_note-986)[[257]](#cite_note-BBC_Tehran_response-282)[[947]](#cite_note-987) [Reuters](/wiki/Reuters "Reuters") said that by sending missiles at Gulf states, Iran caused them to realize that Iran poses a threat to them, and could thus cause them to support the US-Israel strikes.[[948]](#cite_note-988) Former CIA director [David Petraeus](/wiki/David_Petraeus "David Petraeus") echoed these sentiments, stating that Iran's targeting of other Gulf states was likely a strategic error that could pull additional countries into the war.[[949]](#cite_note-989)

According to [Negar Mortazavi](/wiki/Negar_Mortazavi "Negar Mortazavi") at the [Center for International Policy](/wiki/Center_for_International_Policy "Center for International Policy"), the Houthis joining the war was "no surprise".[[950]](#cite_note-990) After the Houthis joined the war, Al-Muslimi, the [Chatham House](/wiki/Chatham_House "Chatham House") researcher, said that the Houthis are capable of not only destabilizing the Middle East, but the entire world.[[951]](#cite_note-991)

Based on the Israeli political landscape and enthusiasm for the war [Daniel Levy](/wiki/Daniel_Levy_(political_analyst) "Daniel Levy (political analyst)") wrote on 30 March, that the nations' interests may be opposed to all who are seeking an end to the war. Nonetheless he saw the Lebanon theatre of war as another campaign with no clear endgame. He concluded that "Israel will continue to encourage and provoke escalation and to undermine any negotiation or ceasefire talks, including nudging the US towards ground operations." He saw Israel's actions to actively accelerate US global decline.[[952]](#cite_note-992)
