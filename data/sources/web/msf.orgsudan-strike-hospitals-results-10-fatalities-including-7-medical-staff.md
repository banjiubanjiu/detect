# Strike on Al‑Jabalain hospital results in 10 fatalities, including seven medical staff

© Frederic Seguin/MSF

Voices from the Field 2 April 2026

On 2 April 2026, a drone attack struck Al-Jabalain hospital in White Nile state, Sudan. Esperanza Santos, Médecins Sans Frontières' (MSF's) head of emergencies for Sudan, reacts to this appalling attack.

Once again, in [Sudan](https://www.msf.org/sudan "Sudan"), healthcare has been struck at its heart. On 2 April 2026, a drone attack reportedly carried out by the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) [targeted](https://www.msf.org/attacks-medical-care "Attacks on medical care") Al‑Jabalain hospital in White Nile state. There were two [strikes](https://www.msf.org/conflict-sudan "Conflict in Sudan"), one hit the operating theatre and the other one the maternity ward.

This unacceptable [assault](https://www.msf.org/war-and-conflict "War and conflict") resulted in at least 10 fatalities, including seven medical staff, some of whom have previously worked with Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF). The attack is even more appalling as it occurred during a children’s immunisation campaign. Our thoughts go to the families of the deceased, including our former colleagues.

As part of our emergency response, MSF is supplying fuel to enable four Ministry of Health ambulances to transfer patients from Al‑Jabalain to Kosti, around 80 kilometres away. At least 19 of the injured have been transferred to Kosti. We have provided mass‑casualty kits and dressing material to support the teaching hospital.

On 20 March 2026, a deadly attack carried out by the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) targeting El-Daein hospital, East Darfur state, resulted in the deaths of 70 people, including 15 children. MSF sent a truck of medical supplies to assist the hospital in managing casualties.

MSF is outraged by these repeated attacks on healthcare, which have escalated dangerously in recent weeks. Health facilities, medical staff, and patients must always be protected. We call on the RSF and the SAF to immediately stop this spiral of violence against medical facilities.
