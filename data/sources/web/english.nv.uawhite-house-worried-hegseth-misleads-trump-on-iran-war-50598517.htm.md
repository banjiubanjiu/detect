# White House worried Hegseth misleads Trump on Iran war — WaPo

April 7, 2026, 10:21 PM

![Donald Trump and Pete Hegseth (Photo: REUTERS/Carlos Barria)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/270/777/original/1ce326e998ca3f0b42e142f3da163e4e.png?q=85&stamp=20260408003258&w=900&f=webp)

Donald Trump and Pete Hegseth (Photo: REUTERS/Carlos Barria)

White House officials are concerned that boastful briefings by Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth are misleading [President Donald Trump](https://english.nv.ua/nation/trump-threatens-iran-says-a-whole-civilization-will-die-tonight-50598446.html) about U.S. successes in the war with Iran, The Washington Post [reported](https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/2026/04/07/hegseth-iran-rhetoric/) on April 7, citing unnamed U.S. officials.

“Pete is not speaking truth to the president,” the report quoted one source.

“As a result, the president is out there repeating misleading information.”

Kelly Grieco, a military analyst at the Stimson Center, told The Washington Post that while U.S. strikes have significantly degraded Iran’s capabilities, losing an F-15E is the kind of event that occurs “when you have air superiority but don’t have air supremacy.”

Anonymous White House sources quoted by The Washington Post said Hegseth has been manipulating metrics to present to the president a narrative of rapid elimination of Iran’s missile arsenal. Though Hegseth has said Iranian military programs are “largely collapsing,” U.S. intelligence assessments cited by the Post indicate more than half of Iran’s missile launchers and thousands of drones remain intact.

The Post also reported skepticism after Hegseth told reporters March 31 that the number of Iranian missile and drone launches had fallen to its lowest level in any 24-hour period since the war began. Hegseth presented the figure as evidence that relentless U.S. and Israeli strikes were diminishing Iran’s ability to sustain attacks. U.S. military analysts have warned against using launch counts as a success metric, saying Iran has shifted to an economy-and-precision strategy.

“They’re focusing on smaller, more targeted salvos,” Grieco told The Washington Post.

“They’re picking targets and looking for gaps in defenses.”
