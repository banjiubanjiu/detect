# Ukraine downplays Vance accusation of election interference in Hungary

41

U.S. Vice President JD Vance and Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orbán in Budapest, Hungary, on April 7, 2026 (Photo: REUTERS/Jonathan Ernst)

Kyiv does not overstate the importance of U.S. Vice President JD Vance’s accusation that elements within Ukrainian security services are attempting to influence the outcomes of Hungarian and American elections, Ukraine’s Foreign Ministry spokesperson Heorhii Tykhyi told a briefing on April 8.

Some journalists at the joint press conference with Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orbán had “systematically tried to provoke” Vance into sharp statements, while Vance “almost always responded neutrally,” the spokesman said.

“We, to be honest, do not exaggerate the significance of this statement,” Tykhyi said.

“I urge everyone to watch the full press conference — then the vice president’s attitude will become obvious — rather than draw conclusions from a small snippet.”
