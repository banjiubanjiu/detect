# Ust-Luga port hit again as Ukraine targets Russian energy assets

April 7, 2026, 04:14 PM

![Image 6: Fire at the Ust-Luga port following a drone attack on April 7, 2026 (Photo: General Staff of the Armed Forces of Ukraine)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/270/621/original/27d22ae83499a78b7ffd87bbbf5212f6.png?q=85&stamp=20260407181421&w=900&f=webp)

Fire at the Ust-Luga port following a drone attack on April 7, 2026 (Photo: General Staff of the Armed Forces of Ukraine)

Ukrainian Defense Forces struck the Ust-Luga Oil terminal in Russia’s Leningrad Oblast overnight on April 7, [the General Staff of the Armed Forces of Ukraine said.](https://t.me/GeneralStaffZSU/37008)

At least three storage tanks belonging to Transneft-Baltika were hit, the General Staff said. The facility is a key element of Russia’s oil export infrastructure, which generates revenue used to finance the war against Ukraine, officials added.

Three large-capacity tanks were also damaged during a separate attack on the Transneft-Port Primorsk facility on April 5, causing a fire involving petroleum products, the General Staff said. The affected tanks, each with a capacity of 20,000 cubic meters, are used for long-term storage of oil and other flammable liquids.

The same day, strikes on the Lukoil-Nizhegorodnefteorgsintez refinery damaged key crude processing units, including AVT-6 and AVT-1, as well as unit 19/6 used in bitumen production, the General Staff said.

A Ukrainian Telegram channel,Exilenova+, reported a renewed attack on the Ust-Luga port on April 7 after partial operations had resumed.

Leningrad Oblast Governor Alexander Drozdenko confirmed a drone attack, claiming that air defenses shot down 22 unmanned aerial vehicles in the region.

The port had begun restoring oil exports at minimal levels after several days of disruption, Bloomberg reported on April 5.

Ukraine’s Security Service previously confirmed that long-range drones struck infrastructure at the Ust-Luga port in late March, with satellite images showing large fires at both Ust-Luga and Primorsk ports.

Drones attacked Leningrad Oblast again on March 31, marking the fifth strike on the Ust-Luga port within 10 days, Reuters reported.

Russian Baltic Sea ports have reduced oil shipments to their lowest levels since the start of Russia’s full-scale invasion of Ukraine in 2022 following the series of strikes, Bloomberg reported.
