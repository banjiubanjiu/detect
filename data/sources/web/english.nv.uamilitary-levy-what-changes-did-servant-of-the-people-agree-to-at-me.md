# Ukrainian ruling party agrees to back key tax bills needed for IMF and EU funds

April 7, 2026, 01:40 PM

![Prime Minister Yulia Svyrydenko (center) (Photo: Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/270/480/original/b8e5de5391700c704c43e718c98ed2f8.png?q=85&stamp=20260407150152&w=900&f=webp)

Prime Minister Yulia Svyrydenko (center) (Photo: Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine)

The Servant of the People parliamentary faction has agreed to support two government-backed tax bills critical for securing continued IMF and EU funding, but has not yet reached consensus on a third measure to scrap VAT exemptions on international parcels worth up to €150 ($173), sources in the presidential party told NV on April 7.

The agreements were reached during an evening meeting on April 6 between Prime Minister Yulia Svyrydenko, Finance Minister Sergii Marchenko, Vice Prime Minister for European and Euro-Atlantic Integration Taras Kachka and Servant of the People lawmakers.

According to two anonymous sources in the faction, the party will back legislation to extend the 5% military tax for three years after the war (bill No. 15110) and to introduce taxation on digital platforms, known as the “OLX law” (bill No. 15111). Discussions continue on the bill to cancel VAT privileges for imported parcels up to €150 (bill No. 15112).

“The military levy and digital platforms—yes, [the SOTP faction will support those]; there’s still discussion regarding parcels,” the source said.

Another participant in the meeting from the Servant of the People faction confirmed to NV, on condition of anonymity, his fellow faction member’s assessment. “The meeting was constructive; we’ll see the results on the scoreboard today and tomorrow when key laws for securing funding from the EU’s Ukraine Facility Plan (UFP) and the IMF are considered,” he said.

Another source from the Servant of the People faction told NV that Svyrydenko remained silent throughout the faction meeting. “David [Arakhamia, head of the Servant of the People faction in the Verkhovna Rada] put all his efforts into convincing the faction,” the source stated. “Marchenko and Kachka did the same.”

NV reached out to the Prime Minister’s press office requesting comment on the results of the meeting with the presidential faction; however, as of the publication of this article, the newsroom had not received a response from the government.

Earlier, the head of the presidential faction announced a joint meeting between Servant of the People faction and top government officials. “It will be dedicated to a week of voting on bills related to the fulfillment of international obligations—the IMF, Ukraine Facility, World Bank, and all others. The meeting will be entirely devoted to these issues, as many international payments are tied to them,” said David Arakhamia.
