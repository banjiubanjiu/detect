# Ukrainian gas prices will fall slower than global oil

April 8, 2026, 05:24 PM

![Image 6: SOPA Images / Alamy / Reuters](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/271/408/original/d1c3dea5470b1a0265eea60d3c4fba0a.png?q=85&stamp=20260408210059&w=900&f=webp)

SOPA Images / Alamy / Reuters

Falling global crude and refined-product prices will not lead to a rapid drop in retail prices at [Ukrainian gas stations](https://english.nv.ua/business/ukrainian-government-expects-cheaper-fuel-amid-middle-east-truce-50598665.html), a source in a major gas station network told NV Business on April 8.

The executive explained that local vendors have already contracted large volumes at higher prices, even if they are selling the fuel at razor-thin margins.

“The price that would be fair today is higher than what you see now; we're operating at minimal margins, holding back increases," he said.

He added that recent bulk purchases of diesel and gasoline were made at prices above current market quotes. That means, even as global prices fall, the market will spend some time drawing down those expensive stocks. During crises, companies traditionally boost purchases to avoid even pricier contracts later.

The source also cited assessments from international fuel producers. He said he had spoken with U.S. and European producers, both of whom are skeptical about a quick recovery.

"They say quotes have gone down, but if you look at the math: before the war in Iran it was about $700 per ton, then $1,600, now $1,300," he said, referring to refined-product quotations at European hubs. "So quotes are still nearly twice as high."

He said the market needs time to rebalance. Prices will likely remain relatively stable while costly inventories are sold, and new deliveries establish a more even pricing base.

"The market needs to reach a plateau," he said.
