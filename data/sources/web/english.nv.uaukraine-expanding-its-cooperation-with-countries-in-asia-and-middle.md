# Asian countries show strong interest in Ukraine’s wartime tech expertise

April 8, 2026, 03:28 PM

![Image 6: Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Ukraine (Photo: Enrico Strocchi / Flickr)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/271/304/original/2ed5d6b8952c45fdf7a99ec8e653256f.png?q=85&stamp=20260408172409&w=900&f=webp)

Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Ukraine (Photo: Enrico Strocchi / Flickr)

Countries across Asia are showing considerable interest in Ukraine’s wartime experience and technological capabilities, Ukraine’s Foreign Ministry spokesman Heorhii Tykhyi said at a briefing on April 8.

He pointed out that it extends beyond the countries of the Middle East.

“Regarding Asian countries, we are indeed observing a very high level of interest in Ukrainian experience and technological capabilities — and not only from the states of the Middle East,” said Tykhyi.

“The situation in the Persian Gulf countries, where Ukraine has demonstrated its new role and new subjectivity, has resonated in many other regions of the world. From Africa and Latin America to Asia, everyone sees what Ukraine is capable of.”

Kyiv has already received requests for contacts, including invitations for Foreign Minister Andrii Sybiha to visit several Asian countries.

Progress on these matters is expected in the coming weeks.

“Ukraine will conclude such security agreements with those countries that, of course, share our values, respect us, and whom we respect,” he said. “Naturally, this is a tool of external support.”

The spokesman also addressed plans for a visit to China, saying both sides are working to coordinate timing for the Ukrainian foreign minister’s trip.

“In our opinion, when this visit takes place, it should become an important element in supporting Ukrainian-Chinese dialogue,” Tykhyi said. “The agenda would include more active Chinese involvement in international efforts to stop Russia’s armed aggression against the people of Ukraine, as well as stepped-up practical cooperation in trade, economic and humanitarian spheres.”

On April 1, President Volodymyr Zelenskyy said Ukraine is cooperating with Saudi Arabia, the United Arab Emirates, Qatar and Jordan, and is in communication with Bahrain, Kuwait and Iraq on countering Iranian Shahed drones.
