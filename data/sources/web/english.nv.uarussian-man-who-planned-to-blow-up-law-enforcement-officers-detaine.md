# SBU nabs Russian citizen in western Ukraine over booby-trap plot

April 8, 2026, 12:35 PM

![Image 6: The 59-year-old native of Cherkasy Oblast held Russian citizenship and had ties to the FSB (Photo: Security Service of Ukraine in Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast/Facebook)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/271/185/original/8a4d5229011885a731f8fb17b6593761.png?q=85&stamp=20260408145711&w=900&f=webp)

Ukraine’s SBU Security Service and National Police detained a 59-year-old Russian citizen in Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast who had manufactured an explosive device and set a booby trap at the entrance to his house to target law enforcement officers, authorities [reported](https://www.facebook.com/100079927150199/posts/956840823656814/?mibextid=wwXIfr&rdid=Z2T3AEoBfW1RKmbR#) on April 8.

The man, born in Cherkasy Oblast and living in the Ivano-Frankivsk municipality, held Russian citizenship and had contacts with Russia’s Federal Security Service (FSB).

![Image 7: Security Service of Ukraine in Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast/Facebook](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/MediaPhoto/images/000/583/001/original/3e96832624ca8d9699f634b36ab8586a.png?q=85&stamp=20260408145711&f=webp)

 Photo: Security Service of Ukraine in Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast/Facebook 

According to the SBU, he left for Russian-occupied territory in Zaporizhzhya Oblast in the first year of the full-scale invasion and moved to Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast in 2025.

Investigators said the suspect previously worked as an engineer in the oil and gas industry in the occupied territory. After the full-scale war began, he frequently visited occupied areas, where he came to the attention of Russian special services.

During a search of his home, officers found a tripwire connected to an explosive device already set in combat position at the front door. The device was designed to detonate when law enforcement entered the premises.

![Image 9: Security Service of Ukraine in Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast/Facebook](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/MediaPhoto/images/000/583/002/original/3d1dc8a462a2fa1e5fed59a6c477bf8b.png?q=85&stamp=20260408145712&f=webp)

 Photo: Security Service of Ukraine in Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast/Facebook 

The man had drawn the diagram himself, built the device, and filled it with self-tapping screws to increase its lethal effect. Experts classified it as a high-danger explosive.

Items seized during the search included hand-drawn diagrams of the explosive device, a sapper tester for electric detonation control, and mobile phones with SIM cards from Russian operators.

![Image 10: Security Service of Ukraine in Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast/Facebook](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/MediaPhoto/images/000/583/003/original/1ba2679d56030a3b96687941f17fbde2.png?q=85&stamp=20260408145713&f=webp)

 Photo: Security Service of Ukraine in Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast/Facebook 

The suspect has been charged under Part 1 of Article 263 of the Criminal Code of Ukraine (illegal handling of weapons, ammunition or explosive substances). A court has ordered him held in custody.
