# Russia halts oil exports from Novorossiysk terminal after Ukrainian drone attack

April 7, 2026, 04:45 PM

![The aftermath of the strike on the port of Novorossiysk (Photo: Radio Liberty / Telegram)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/270/647/original/221a541b262324328e21cd25f523a5fa.png?q=85&stamp=20260407190127&w=900&f=webp)

The aftermath of the strike on the port of Novorossiysk (Photo: Radio Liberty / Telegram)

Russia has suspended crude oil exports from the Sheskharis terminal in the Black Sea port of Novorossiysk after a major Ukrainian drone attack sparked a large fire, two industry sources [told](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/russias-black-seas-sheskharis-terminal-halts-loadings-after-drone-attack-sources-2026-04-07/) Reuters on April 7.

The terminal, which typically handles 700,000 barrels of crude oil per day, is a key Russian export hub in the Black Sea. The suspension of its operations will increase the strain on Russian infrastructure, which has been repeatedly targeted by attacks, according to Reuters.

Sources reported that oil exports from Russia’s main Baltic port, Ust-Luga, have also been suspended since last week following heavy strikes and fires.

Sources stated that Russia will be forced to cut production as stocks pile up and storage facilities fill, while international demand for alternatives to Middle Eastern oil remains high, and it could maximize the economic benefits from the price spike caused by the war in Iran.

According to sources, the port has not yet assessed the extent of the damage. A previous attack in early March led to a five-day halt in shipments.

Russian Urals crude, Kazakhstani KEBCO crude, and sweet Siberian Light crude are transshipped through Novorossiysk.

On April 6, Telegram channels reported that the Sheskharis oil terminal in Novorossiysk had been attacked.

Later, Robert “Magyar” Brovdi, commander of the Unmanned Systems Forces, stated that the Russian frigate Admiral Makarov had also been hit there.

Later, the General Staff of the Armed Forces of Ukraine confirmed that the Ukrainian Defense Forces had carried out successful strikes on the Sheskharis oil terminal in Novorossiysk. The General Staff stated that a large-scale fire broke out on the terminal’s grounds.

According to NV sources in the SBU Security Service, the strike damaged six of the seven oil loading stands used for loading and unloading oil from tankers. Strikes on the terminal’s ground infrastructure were also recorded: the pipeline system’s control unit and the oil metering station were hit, and large-scale fires broke out at the impact sites.
