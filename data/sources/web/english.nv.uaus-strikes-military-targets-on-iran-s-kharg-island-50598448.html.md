# U.S. strikes military targets on Iran’s Kharg Island in Persian Gulf

April 7, 2026, 03:14 PM

Oil terminals on Kharg Island, February 25, 2026 (Photo: Planet Labs PBC/Handout via REUTERS)

The United States struck military targets on Iran’s Khark Island in the Persian Gulf, [Axios reporter Barak Ravid said on X, citing a U.S. official](https://x.com/BarakRavid/status/2041476986049036588) on Apr. 7.

[According to Anadolu Agency,](https://www.aa.com.tr/en/middle-east/explosions-rock-iran-s-kharg-island-after-us-israeli-strikes-media/3896128)Iranian media also reported Tuesday, April 7, that explosions were heard on Kharg Island following several U.S.-Israeli airstrikes.

In addition, a senior U.S. official [told](https://x.com/JenGriffinFNC/status/2041479295667736673) Fox News correspondent Jennifer Griffin that the United States carried out strikes on dozens of military sites on Kharg Island overnight.

Targets included bunkers, a radar station and an ammunition depot. Landing docks were not an intended target. The source said they would only have been struck if Iranian forces were firing in their immediate vicinity.

The official added that the strikes were carried out solely by the United States, not Israel.

“This is a message to the Iranians,” the senior U.S. official said.

According to a source in U.S. military circles, if strikes are being carried out on Iran’s railways, they are not being conducted by the United States.

U.S. President Donald Trump has repeatedly warned that the United States could strike power plants, bridges and other infrastructure in Iran if Tehran does not reach a deal or reopen the Strait of Hormuz.

On April 5, he said Iran had until Tuesday, 8:00 p.m. ET, to reach an agreement.

“Tuesday will be ‘Power Plant Day’ and ‘Bridge Day’ in Iran, all at once. It will be something!!! Open the strait, you insane bastards, or you will live in hell. Just watch! Praise Allah,” Trump wrote on Truth Social.

Tehran responded that if the United States continues strikes on Iranian infrastructure, the “gates of hell” would open for the United States and Israel. According to CNN, Tehran said it would fully reopen the Strait of Hormuz only after receiving financial compensation for war-related damages.

On April 6, Axios reported that the United States and Iran, through intermediaries, are discussing terms of a potential 45-day ceasefire that could lead to a final end to the war.

The same day, Reuters, citing sources, reported that Iran rejected a proposal to end the war with the United States and Israel that included reopening the Strait of Hormuz in exchange for a ceasefire.

Later, CNN reported that Trump did not support the plan presented by the mediators.

On April 6, Trump said Iran could be destroyed overnight and added that this could happen as early as Tuesday.
