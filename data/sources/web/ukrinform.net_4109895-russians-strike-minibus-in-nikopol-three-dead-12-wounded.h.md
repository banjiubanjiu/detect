# Russians strike minibus in Nikopol: Three dead, 12 wounded

According to Ukrinform, Oleksandr Ganzha, head of the Dnipropetrovsk Regional Military Administration, reported this on Telegram.

“Three people were killed, and 12 more were wounded. The enemy attacked a city bus with an FPV drone in the very center of Nikopol,” he wrote.

The bus was pulling up to a stop. There were people inside the bus and at the stop.

“This was not a random strike. It was deliberate terror against civilians—against people who were simply doing their business,” Ganzha added.

Following Tuesday morning’s Russian strike on a minibus in Nikopol, Dnipropetrovsk region, seven people have so far been rescued, Ukraine’s Minister of Internal Affairs Ihor Klymenko reported on Telegram.

“A brutal strike on civilian public transport. Three people were killed, and another 12 were injured. Rescuers have been working at the scene since the first minutes. Together with medical personnel, they managed to free and rescue seven people,” he stated.

Klymenko assured that the Ministry of Internal Affairs services are doing everything possible to rescue everyone who needs help.

The minister emphasized that Russia is deliberately targeting civilians and that this is no accident—deliberate attacks on civilians are their tactic.

He assured that investigators are documenting every such incident.

Earlier, it was reported that an 11-year-old boy was killed in an attack yesterday in the Pokrovske community of the Synelnykove district. Two women, aged 31 and 61, and a 33-year-old man were hospitalized in moderate condition.
