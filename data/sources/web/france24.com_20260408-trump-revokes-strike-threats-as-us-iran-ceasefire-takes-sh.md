Lebanese army warns displaced against returning south, citing Israeli attacks

Lebanon's army has warned people against returning to the country's south, where the Israeli military is still launching attacks, as Israel said the ceasefire with Iran did not include its conflict with Hezbollah.

"In light of regional developments and reports circulating about a ceasefire, (the army) urges citizens to wait before returning to southern villages and towns and to avoid approaching areas where Israeli occupation forces have advanced... since they may be exposing themselves to the ongoing Israeli attacks," the Lebanese army said in a statement.

Hundreds of thousands of Lebanese have been displaced since Lebanon was drawn into the war on March 2.

US-Iran ceasefire deal must include Lebanon, Macron says

French President Emmanuel Macron has welcomed the US-Iran ceasefire agreement, calling it a "very good thing".

But he also noted that the terms of the deal must be fully respected across the region.

The situation in Lebanon remains critical, said Macron, hours after Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu said Lebanon was not included in the agreement. The US-Iran deal must include Lebanon, Macron added.

Israel renews strikes on southern Lebanon

Israel has renewed its strikes on southern Lebanon, according to Lebanese state media, after Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu insisted the Iran war truce does not include Lebanon.

The Israeli army also issued an evacuation warning for a building near the southern city of Tyre.

Lebanese armed group Hezbollah has not claimed any operations since the truce was announced. Three Lebanese sources told Reuters that Hezbollah halted fire on northern Israel and on Israeli troops in Lebanon in the early hours of Wednesday as part of the US-Iran ceasefire agreement.

Netanyahu 'failed politically, failed strategically', Israeli opposition leader says

Israel's main opposition leader Yair Lapid has sharply criticised the ceasefire with Iran, accusing Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu of failing to achieve the war's objectives.

"There has never been a political disaster like this in our entire history. Israel was not even close to the table when decisions were made concerning the core of our national security," Lapid wrote on X.

"The army carried out everything that was asked of it, and the public showed remarkable resilience, but Netanyahu failed politically, failed strategically, and did not achieve any of the goals he himself set."

US-Iran ceasefire ‘a major problem domestically’ for Netanyahu

Reporting from Jerusalem, FRANCE 24’s Noga Tarnopolsky says the US-Iran ceasefire is likely to be “a major problem domestically” for Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu.

Within Netanyahu’s Likud party, there have been some criticisms of the agreement, Tarnopolsky notes. Oren Hazan, a Likud parliamentarian who is considered “very close to Netanyahu, posted a message on social media slamming US President Donald Trump as going “from hero to zero”, she reports.

Netanyahu’s base “was expecting regime change, was expecting something very dramatic” with the Iran war. The two-week ceasefire agreement falls short of their expectations, Tarnopolsky notes.

Gemany, Oman, Ukraine among countries welcoming ceasefire

Hours after President Donald Trump agreed to a two-week ceasefire if Iran opened the Strait of Hormuz and Tehran agreed to pause fighting, several countries have welcomed the pause in hostilities.

German Chancellor Friedrich Merz said his government welcomed the two-week ceasefire agreement.

"The aim now must be to negotiate a lasting end to the war in the coming days," Merz said in a statement. "This can only be achieved through diplomatic channels."

Oman, a Gulf Arab state that was mediating US-Iran indirect talks when the US and Israel launched strikes on Iran on February 28, has welcomed the ceasefire and called for "intensifying efforts" to resolve the crisis.

Ukrainian Foreign Minister Andrii Sybiha has also hailed the ceasefire and calling for similar "decisiveness" in stopping Russia's war in Ukraine.

"American decisiveness works. We believe it is time for sufficient decisiveness to force Moscow to cease fire and end its war against Ukraine," Sybiha wrote in a social media post.

Pakistan to host US-Iran ceasefire talks Friday

Talks to hammer out the details of the US-Iran ceasefire agreement are scheduled for Friday in Pakistan, Iran's southeastern neighbour which played a key role in negotiating the truce agreement.

Pakistan's Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif welcomed the agreement and said Islamabad will host US and Iranian delegations later this week.

"I... extend deepest gratitude to the leadership of both the countries and invite their delegations to Islamabad on Friday, 10th April 2026, to further negotiate for a conclusive agreement to settle all disputes," he said in a post on social media.

"We earnestly hope, that the 'Islamabad Talks' succeed in achieving sustainable peace and wish to share more good news in coming days," Sharif added.

Blasts heard in Bahrain capital Manam, fire put out at facility, interior ministry says

Explosions have been heard in Bahrain's capital Manama, according to journalists, just hours after the US announced a ceasefire with Iran.

The Gulf kingdom's interior ministry said in a statement that civil defence crews had succeeded in controlling a fire that broke out in a facility "as a result of the Iranian aggression". No injuries were reported.

Asian benchmarks jump after oil prices sink in response to the Iran ceasefire

Asian shares surged in Wednesday morning trading, as oil prices plunged after the U.S. and Iran agreed to a two-week ceasefire that includes the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz.

The news pushed down crude prices, with West Texas Intermediate losing almost 20 percent and Brent as much as 16 percent as investors heaved a huge sigh of relief after more than five weeks of war that has hammered supplies.

The euphoria sent equities rocketing on hopes the crisis that has shocked the global economy for more than a month will come to an end.

Seoul jumped more than six percent in opening trade and Tokyo more than five percent, while Taipei added 4.2 percent, and Sydney and Hong Kong advanced more than two percent. Shanghai, Mumbai, Bangkok, Manila, Jakarta, Singapore and Wellington were also sharply higher.

Iran portraying ceasefire as 'victory against the United States and Israel'

Reporting from Tehran FRANCE 24’s Reza Sayah says Iranian state media is portraying the ceasefire agreement as a “victory against the United States and Israel”.

Despite the heavy losses inflicted by massive strikes, the assassination of top leaders, civilian deaths, and destruction of infrastructure, “the Islamic Republic is still standing”, says Sayah. “For Iran, survival was always a victory,” he notes.

Trump says US to help with traffic buildup in Strait of Hormuz

US President Donald Trump said on Wednesday the United States will help with the traffic buildup in the Strait of Hormuz.

"We'll be loading up with supplies of all kinds, and just 'hangin' around' in order to make sure that everything goes well," Trump said.

"There will be lots of positive action! Big money will be made. Iran can start the reconstruction process," he also said.

Malaysia's prime minister welcomes ceasefire

Malaysian Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim on Wednesday welcomed the ceasefire between the United States and Iran, and called for lasting peace in the region.

In a social media post, Anwar said it was imperative that Iran's 10-point proposal to end the war be "translated into a comprehensive peace deal, not just for Iran, but also for Iraq, Lebanon and Yemen".

Netanyahu says Iran ceasefire does not include Lebanon

Israel supports US President Donald Trump's decision to suspend strikes against Iran for two weeks, but said the ceasefire does not include Lebanon, Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's office said on Wednesday.

The office said Israel backed the US move provided Tehran immediately opens the straits and stops attacks against the United States, Israel and countries in the region. The remarks came after Washington announced a two-week suspension of strikes against Iran as part of efforts to de-escalate the conflict and open a window for negotiations.

Israel also said it supports US efforts to ensure Iran no longer poses a nuclear, missile or "terror" threat to US, Israel and Iran's Arab neighbours, adding that Washington had told Israel it was committed to achieving their shared goals in upcoming negotiations.

President Donald Trump told AFP on Tuesday that the United States had won a "total and complete victory" after agreeing a two-week ceasefire deal with Iran.

"Total and complete victory. 100 percent. No question about it," Trump said in a brief telephone interview shortly after the announcement of the truce.

Islamic Resistance in Iraq suspends operations after US-Iran truce

The Islamic Resistance in Iraq, an umbrella group of Iran-backed armed factions, said it would suspend its operations in Iraq and across the region for two weeks, according to a statement issued on Wednesday, in a move that follows announcements of a two-week suspension of hostilities between the United States and Iran.

• US President Donald Trump agreed "to suspend the bombing and attack of Iran for a period of two weeks".

• Iran said it would halt its own attacks on neighbouring countries if attacks against it stop and that safe transit through the Strait of Hormuz would be possible for two weeks in coordination with Iranian armed forces.
