# Ukraine confirms hit on marine oil terminal in occupied Feodosia

April 8, 2026, 11:38 AM

![Robert Brovdi confirmed the night attack on occupied Crimea (Photo: Robert Brovdi / Facebook)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/271/118/original/5ea20b204482e5c2c8f62f837fd8ff5f.png?q=85&stamp=20260408140453&w=900&f=webp)

Robert Brovdi confirmed the night attack on occupied Crimea (Photo: Robert Brovdi / Facebook)

Commander of Ukraine’s Unmanned Systems Forces Robert Brovdi confirmed a strike on the Marine Oil Terminal in occupied Feodosia, saying the facility was hit in an operation reported on Apr. 8.

The attack was carried out by fighters of the 9th Kairos Battalion of the 414th Separate Brigade of Unmanned Systems, known as Madyar’s Birds.

“The SBS birds delivered a ‘fiery Easter egg’ to the oil terminal in Feodosia as part of Easter Delivery,” Robert Brovdi wrote.

He also said the Unmanned Systems Forces struck Russian Buk-M3 and Tor-M2 air defense systems in temporarily occupied areas of Zaporizhzhia and Donetsk oblasts, a Zoopark-1M radar in Zaporizhzhia Oblast, logistics depots of Russian forces in the city of Sukhodilsk in Luhansk Oblast, as well as UAV storage facilities and a drone preflight preparation site in the village of Stepne in Donetsk Oblast.

Overnight into April 8, drones attacked temporarily occupied Feodosia, where a fire broke out at an oil depot.

Overnight into April 7, Ukraine’s Defense Forces struck the Ust-Luga Oil terminal in Russia’s Leningrad Oblast. Three storage tanks belonging to Transneft-Baltika were hit. The General Staff of Ukraine’s Armed Forces said the facility is a key element of Russia’s oil export infrastructure, revenues from which are used to finance its war against Ukraine.

The strike followed a series of attacks on Russian oil facilities.

On March 31, drones targeted Russia’s Leningrad Oblast. Reuters reported it was the fifth strike on the port of Ust-Luga in 10 days.

The Security Service of Ukraine confirmed that long-range drones successfully struck infrastructure at the Ust-Luga port oil terminal. Satellite images showed large fires at the ports of Primorsk and Ust-Luga.
