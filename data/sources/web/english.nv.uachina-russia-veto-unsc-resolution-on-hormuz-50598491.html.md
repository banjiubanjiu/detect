# China, Russia veto UN Security Council resolution on Hormuz

7 April, 08:27 PM

World

![Image 4: UN Security Council (Photo: REUTERS/Amr Alfiky)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/270/717/original/3996b2f435427ee31c24683ebaab50a8.png?q=85&stamp=20260407212522&w=900&f=webp)

UN Security Council (Photo: REUTERS/Amr Alfiky)

China and Russia vetoed a UN Security Council resolution that sought to restore shipping through the [Strait of Hormuz](https://english.nv.ua/nation/us-strikes-military-targets-on-iran-s-kharg-island-50598448.html), Bloomberg [reported](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-07/china-and-russia-veto-security-council-resolution-on-hormuz) on April 7.

The resolution, introduced by Bahrain, received 11 votes in favor of 15 but was blocked by vetoes from China and Russia. The vote came after days of negotiations and pressure from Gulf countries to restore free passage through the strait, which Iran had blocked.

Bahrain’s draft called on countries to “coordinate defensive efforts, as appropriate,” to ensure the safety of navigation in the Strait of Hormuz.

The vote came hours before a deadline set by U.S. President Donald Trump. Trump had said Iran must reopen the strait by 8 p.m. Eastern Time (3 a.m. Kyiv time April 8); he threatened airstrikes on Iran’s civilian infrastructure if it did not.

Speaking to the Security Council immediately after the vote, U.S. Ambassador to the UN Mike Waltz, a supporter of the resolution, said that after the 1979 overthrow of Shah Mohammed Reza Pahlavi the ayatollahs’ first step was “the taking of dozens of Americans hostage.”

“Now they are taking the Strait of Hormuz hostage, and with it they are trying to take the world economy hostage,” Waltz said. “So, colleagues, this may be their last step. We’ll see.”
