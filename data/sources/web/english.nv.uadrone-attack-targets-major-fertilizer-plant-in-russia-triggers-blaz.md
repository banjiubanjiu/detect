# Drones torch chemical plant in Russia’s Voronezh Oblast

April 7, 2026, 05:39 AM

![Image 6: Aftermath of a drone attack on a chemical plant in Russia's Voronezh Oblast (Photo: Astra)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/270/084/original/5a5d761290f8412cf9e04af174015291.png?q=85&stamp=20260407084032&w=900&f=webp)

Aftermath of a drone attack on a chemical plant in Russia's Voronezh Oblast (Photo: Astra)

Minudobreniya, a major chemical plant in southwestern Russia, was targeted in a drone attack on the town of Rossosh late on April 6, the Astra Telegram channel [reported](https://t.me/astrapress/109121) the following morning, sharing a video of the fire at the facility.

According to eyewitnesses, the roof of a finished goods warehouse caught fire.

Operated by a subsidiary of Roskhim, the plant is one of the largest chemical enterprises in Voronezh Oblast. It is reportedly linked to Arkady Rotenberg, a Russian oligarch and close associate of Kremlin dictator Vladimir Putin.

The facility can produce more than 550,000 tons of ammonium nitrate and up to 1.2 million tons of NPK fertilizers annually. Ammonium nitrate can also be used to make explosives.

The plant was previously targeted by Ukrainian drones in December 2025.

Regional Governor Alexander Gusev confirmed the attack, claiming a man was injured when debris from a downed drone ignited the roof of a warehouse.
