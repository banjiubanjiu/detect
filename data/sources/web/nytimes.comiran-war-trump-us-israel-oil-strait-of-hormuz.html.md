April 7, 2026, 7:15 p.m. ET

The U.S.-Israeli war with Iran raged for more than five weeks before a cease-fire was announced on the 39th day.It was the second time in less than a year that President Trump directly involved the United States in a military conflict with Tehran.

Cast by Mr. Trump in part as an effort to spur Iranians [to topple their theocratic leadership](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/22/us/politics/iran-israel-trump-netanyahu-mossad.html), the conflict soon became a regional war that resulted in thousands of deaths, mostly in Iran and Lebanon. Hundreds of thousands of people have been forced from their homes, and the global economy has been badly rattled.

Here are some key moments in the war:

**Feb. 28:** The United States and Israel [launched](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/02/28/world/middleeast/iran-attacks-what-to-know.html) strikes across Iran, hitting a government compound in Tehran and military targets. The blasts killed Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, the nation’s supreme leader for almost 37 years, as well as other high-level military and intelligence leaders.

At least 175 people, most of them likely children, were killed in a [strike on a girls’ elementary school](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/03/01/world/middleeast/girls-school-strike-iran-video.html) in southern Iran, health officials and Iranian state media said. The strike was a [targeting mistake by the U.S. military](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/11/us/politics/iran-school-missile-strike.html), according to U.S. officials familiar with a military investigation.

Iran retaliated by firing missiles and drones at Israel and at U.S. military bases in the region, including in Qatar, Kuwait and the United Arab Emirates.

**March 1:**An Iranian drone attack killed six U.S. soldiers in [Port Shuaiba, Kuwait](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/07/us/politics/trump-dover-dignified-transfer-iran.html), the first Americans to die in the war.
