# Ukraine urges citizens to avoid travel to Hungary ahead of elections

April 8, 2026, 02:19 PM

![Image 6: The Ministry of Foreign Affairs advises against traveling to Hungary due to unpredictable risks (Photo: Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Ukraine)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/271/296/original/d685918acc98217b026519defaccfb99.png?q=85&stamp=20260408171654&w=900&f=webp)

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs advises against traveling to Hungary due to unpredictable risks (Photo: Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Ukraine)

Ukraine's Foreign Ministry has urged citizens to refrain from traveling to Hungary and from registering as observers for the country's upcoming parliamentary elections due to risks of provocations and unpredictable actions by Hungarian authorities, spokesman Heorhii Tykhyi said at a briefing on April 8.

"We previously issued a call for Ukrainians to avoid trips to Hungary," Tykhyi said. "It remains in force simply because we do not know what is in the heads of the Hungarian authorities." He noted that following the recent incident with Oschadbank cash-in-transit crews, there are no guarantees that similar situations will not recur with other Ukrainian citizens on fabricated pretexts.

Tykhyi stressed that, as a responsible state institution, the ministry has openly warned citizens and recommended they not visit the country if possible due to the uncertain situation. The ministry has also officially called on people not to send observers to the Hungarian elections.

"Simply understanding that, unfortunately, Budapest has officially made Ukraine a topic of its election campaign, manipulations and constant provocations, we officially appeal and urge people not to go and not to send observers," he said.

The appeal applies to activists from civil society organizations as well as employees of international bodies.

Hungary's 2026 parliamentary elections are scheduled for April 12 to elect members of the new National Assembly. Prime Minister Viktor Orbán of the Fidesz party has held office since 2010.

## Oschadbank incident

Early on March 6, Hungarian authorities detained two Oschadbank cash-in-transit vehicles and seven Ukrainian crew members during a transit through Budapest.

The vehicles carried $40 million, €35 million and 9 kg of gold. Ukraine's Foreign Minister Andrii Sybiha said Hungarian authorities had effectively taken Ukrainians hostage and stolen the money.

Subsequently, Hungary's National Tax and Customs Administration announced the arrest of seven Oschadbank employees on suspicion of "money laundering." The National Police launched criminal investigations into the abduction of Ukrainian citizens and the theft of an Oschadbank company vehicle.

On the evening of March 6, seven employees of Oschadbank's cash-in-transit service, who had been illegally detained in Hungary while on a business trip, returned to Ukraine.

According to Andriy Pyshnyy, head of the National Bank of Ukraine, one of them was hospitalized after being interrogated by Hungarian authorities.

On March 12, Oschadbank's press service reported that Hungary had returned the cash-in-transit vehicles, though they were damaged. The cash and gold remained seized in Hungary.
