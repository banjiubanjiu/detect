# Dutch banking giant ING cancels sale of Russian subsidiary

April 8, 2026, 01:12 PM

![Image 6: ING Group has canceled the agreement to sell its subsidiary bank in Russia (Photo: REUTERS/Dado Ruvic/Illustration/File Photo)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/271/188/original/855a0d88b1a0e7bbbc2c56db8450d2fb.png?q=85&stamp=20260408150458&w=900&f=webp)

ING Group has canceled the agreement to sell its subsidiary bank in Russia (Photo: REUTERS/Dado Ruvic/Illustration/File Photo)

Dutch financial conglomerate ING Group has canceled the sale of its Russian subsidiary, ING Bank, announced in January 2025, after the prospective Russian buyer failed to secure the required approvals, the company reported on April 7, Kommersant [reported](https://www.kommersant.ru/doc/8571387).

ING stated that obtaining all necessary permits for Global Development, the intended buyer, now appears highly unlikely.

The group added that it remains committed to exiting the Russian market, though any exit route other than a sale would negatively affect its results.

The sale itself would result in a loss of approximately €700 million ($819 million) due to Russian legislation requiring banks owned by entities from “unfriendly countries” to be sold at a significant discount.

The deal was originally expected to close in the third quarter of 2025, but was delayed in September after the buyer failed to obtain approvals. The buyer company was registered only months (Nov. 8, 2024) before the deal was announced, with a charter capital of just 15,000 rubles (about $190).

Russian media reported that objections from Russian authorities to the buyer’s candidacy may have contributed to the collapse.

Under Russian law, such transactions require special approval from President Vladimir Putin and the Central Bank.

Experts in Russia suggested the failed deal could indirectly complicate sales of other foreign-owned banks in the country.

As of Jan. 1, 2026, ING Bank ranked 66th among 305 Russian commercial banks by assets, with more than 101 billion rubles ($1.3 billion).

ING also operates a subsidiary in Ukraine — ING Bank Ukraine — which focuses on corporate and investment banking and is ranked 21st by assets among 60 Ukrainian banks, with 29.7 billion hryvnias ($686 million).
