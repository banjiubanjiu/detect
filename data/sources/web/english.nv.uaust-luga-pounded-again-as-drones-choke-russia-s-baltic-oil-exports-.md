# Drone swarm hammers Russia’s Baltic oil hub again

April 7, 2026, 06:17 AM

![Fire at Russian Baltic Sea port of Ust-Luga, Leningrad Oblast (Photo: Exilenova+/Telegram)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/270/106/original/12a2e29dd10c5e2ae80ed928fa0c1f76.png?q=85&stamp=20260407091805&w=900&f=webp)

Fire at Russian Baltic Sea port of Ust-Luga, Leningrad Oblast (Photo: Exilenova+/Telegram)

The Russian Baltic Sea port of Ust-Luga in Leningrad Oblast has been under drone attack since approximately 5 a.m., the Exilenova+ Telegram channel [reported](https://t.me/exilenova_plus/18546) early on April 7.

According to Andriy Kovalenko, head of Ukraine’s Center for Countering Disinformation under the National Security and Defense Council, the [facility has recently recently partially resumed operations](https://english.nv.ua/nation/ust-luga-partly-resumes-oil-shipments-after-drone-attacks-bloomberg-reports-50597918.html) after a series of five successful strikes in March.

Regional Governor Alexander Drozdenko confirmed the attack, claiming that air defenses shot down 22 unmanned aerial vehicles. He did not provide details on damage to the port infrastructure.

In late March, Ukraine’s Security Service (SBU) confirmed that long-range drones had targeted the Ust-Luga oil terminal. Satellite imagery at the time showed large fires burning at the ports of Primorsk and Ust-Luga.
