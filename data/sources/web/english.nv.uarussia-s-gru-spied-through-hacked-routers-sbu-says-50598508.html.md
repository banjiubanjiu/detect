# Russia's GRU spied through hacked routers, SBU says

April 7, 2026, 07:27 PM

![Image 6: Sora Shimazaki/Pexels](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/270/754/original/11784d5e3b382264cb3a76fa6786ed36.png?q=85&stamp=20260407230005&w=900&f=webp)

Sora Shimazaki/Pexels

Together with the FBI and EU law enforcement, Ukraine’s SBU security service disrupted a Russian military intelligence ([GRU](https://english.nv.ua/nation/russian-spy-from-secret-gru-unit-detained-in-colombia-after-google-translate-exposed-plot-50591733.html)) operation that used compromised Wi‑Fi routers to spy on Europeans, Americans, and Ukrainians, the SBU said on April 7.

The multinational cyber operation uncovered that GRU operatives had hacked office and home Wi‑Fi routers that used outdated security protocols. After gaining access, attackers redirected router traffic through a network of compromised DNS servers. Those servers allowing the perpetrators to capture passwords, authentication tokens and other sensitive information, including emails that are typically protected by encryption.

The SBU said the GRU focused particularly on civil servants, military officers, and defense industry employees.

The operation blocked more than 100 malicious DNS servers and removed hundreds of routers in Ukraine from Russian control, substantially weakening the GRU’s intelligence capabilities.

The SBU urged router owners to update firmware and install security updates. If a manufacturer no longer provides support, the agency recommended replacing the router with a newer model, including devices from a different company.
