**Ukraine to remove 2 million men from wanted lists under new military reform**  

Kyiv, Ukraine — The Ukrainian government is preparing a major conscription reform aimed at removing approximately two million men from the wanted lists of its Territorial Recruitment Centers (TRCs).  

According to David Arakhamia, a key figure in the ruling Servant of the People party, the reform will be presented within a month.  

"The conscription reform will be presented within a month, David Arakhamia said," Fedorov told reporters on April 7.  

The reform is expected to be unveiled within a month, Fedorov said.  

**Key points:**  

- The reform will remove about two million people from the wanted lists of Ukraine's Territorial Recruitment Centers (TRCs).  
- The TRCs are responsible for maintaining lists of individuals subject to military service.  
- The wanted lists include draft dodgers and those who have evaded military service.  
- The reform is part of a broader effort to modernize Ukraine's military recruitment system.  

**Impact:**  

- The removal of two million people from the wanted lists will significantly reduce the burden on Ukraine's Territorial Recruitment Centers.  
- The reform is expected to improve the efficiency and fairness of the military recruitment process.  
- It will also help reduce acts of violence against Territorial Recruitment Center officers.  

**Next steps:**  

- Fedorov will first present the concept to the relevant parliamentary committee, followed by briefings for factions and groups, since legislative changes will be required.  
- The reform is expected to be unveiled within a month.  

**Conclusion:**  

The reform is a significant step towards modernizing Ukraine's military recruitment system and ensuring a more equitable and efficient process for all citizens.
