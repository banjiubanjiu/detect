The **2026 strikes on Syria** occurred within the broader context of the [2026 Iran war](/wiki/2026_Iran_war "2026 Iran war"), a regional conflict involving [Israel](/wiki/Israel "Israel") and the [United States](/wiki/United_States "United States") on one side, and [Iran](/wiki/Iran "Iran") on the other, as well as allied non-state actors from both sides. During the war, [Syria](/wiki/Syria "Syria") remained neutral, but suffered material damage after Iranian missiles were intercepted by Israel and several groups claimed responsibility for attacks in the region. The first strike hitting [Syrian](/wiki/Syria "Syria") territory seemed to have been directed towards [Turkey](/wiki/Turkey "Turkey") and was promptly shot down by [NATO](/wiki/NATO "NATO") forces, however, following strikes directly aimed against [Syrian army](/wiki/Syrian_Army "Syrian Army") positions and strategic locations within Syria proper, especially from Hezbollah and as a consequence of the [2026 Lebanon war.](/wiki/2026_Lebanon_war "2026 Lebanon war")

Background
----------

### Ba'athist Syria

Main articles: [Iran–Syria relations](/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Syria_relations "Iran–Syria relations") and [Iran–Israel conflict during the Syrian civil war](/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Israel_conflict_during_the_Syrian_civil_war "Iran–Israel conflict during the Syrian civil war")

For decades prior, Syria, at the time known as [Ba'athist Syria](/wiki/Ba%27athist_Syria "Ba'athist Syria"), had been Iran’s closest Arab ally and a central component of the "[Axis of Resistance](/wiki/Axis_of_Resistance "Axis of Resistance")",[[1]](#cite_note-1)[[2]](#cite_note-2) a coalition of non-state actors (and state actors, at the time) who are affiliated with Iran. The alliance arguably dated back to 1979 when the Syrian government at the time was the first to recognize the newly established [Islamic Republic](/wiki/Iranian_Revolution "Iranian Revolution") and later supported it during the [Iran-Iraq War](/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Iraq_War "Iran–Iraq War").[[3]](#cite_note-:0-3)

Most recently, Iranian forces, including the [Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps](/wiki/Islamic_Revolutionary_Guard_Corps "Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps") (IRGC), [were deeply embedded in Syria during the Syrian civil war](/wiki/Iranian_intervention_in_the_Syrian_civil_war "Iranian intervention in the Syrian civil war"). This involvement dates back at least as early as in 2013, when Iranian and Hezbollah backing allowed the Syrians to regain control over the strategic [Al-Qusayr District](/wiki/Al-Qusayr_District "Al-Qusayr District").[[4]](#cite_note-4) Syria also helped Iran smuggle weapons to its proxies, most notably Hezbollah.[[3]](#cite_note-:0-3) This military absence was apparent when during the [Twelve Days War](/wiki/Twelve-Day_War "Twelve-Day War"), Syria was not used as a launchpad for Iranian counter offensive strikes against Israel.[[5]](#cite_note-:1-5)

### Fall of Assad

However, following the [fall of the Assad regime](/wiki/Fall_of_the_Assad_regime "Fall of the Assad regime"), the new government adopted policies aimed at reasserting sovereignty and limiting foreign military influence (including Iranian ones), thus requiring foreign armed groups to either integrate into state structures or leave the country.[[6]](#cite_note-6) This was also followed by increased relations with the West and straining relations with Iran and its allies, especially Hezbollah.[[3]](#cite_note-:0-3)[[7]](#cite_note-7) Thus, following the fall of [Bashar al-Assad](/wiki/Bashar_al-Assad "Bashar al-Assad") relations between the Islamic Republic of Iran and the new Syrian government remained frozen.[[8]](#cite_note-8)

### Start of the 2026 Iran war

Despite tension with Iran, however, Syria remained neutral. This neutrality however was not absolute as the Syrian government supported its Arab neighbors following Iranian strikes and also reinforced the border in coordination with [Iraq](/wiki/Iraq "Iraq") and later on with Lebanon, mobilizing its army to defend itself from possible threats.[[5]](#cite_note-:1-5)

On 28 February 2026, various news outlets, including [Ynet](/wiki/Ynet "Ynet"), [Reuters](/wiki/Reuters "Reuters") and [Anadolu Agency](/wiki/Anadolu_Agency "Anadolu Agency"),[[9]](#cite_note-9)[[10]](#cite_note-10)[[11]](#cite_note-11) had reported that an Iranian missile had struck the city of [Suwayda](/wiki/Suwayda "Suwayda"), however, the explosion was later attributed to an attempt by the [National Guard militia](/wiki/National_Guard_(Suwayda) "National Guard (Suwayda)") to dismantle a missile from the old regime by the [Syrian Arab News Agency](/wiki/Syrian_Arab_News_Agency "Syrian Arab News Agency").[[12]](#cite_note-12)

Hostilities
-----------

### February 28

Several Iranian missiles were intercepted by Israel, falling in southern Syria,[[13]](#cite_note-13) an Iranian ballistic missile debris fell on a residential building in [Suwayda](/wiki/Suwayda "Suwayda"), killing at least four civilians,[[14]](#cite_note-14)[[15]](#cite_note-15)[[16]](#cite_note-16) however, the explosion was later attributed to an attempt by the [National Guard](/wiki/National_Guard_(Suwayda) "National Guard (Suwayda)") to dismantle a missile from the [Ba'athist Syria](/wiki/Ba%27athist_Syria "Ba'athist Syria") according to the [Syrian Arab News Agency](/wiki/Syrian_Arab_News_Agency "Syrian Arab News Agency").[[17]](#cite_note-17) Other missile fragments fell in the city of [Quneitra](/wiki/Quneitra "Quneitra") and in the [Yarmouk Basin](/wiki/Yarmuk_(river) "Yarmuk (river)").[[18]](#cite_note-18)

### March 1

A [jihadist](/wiki/Salafi_jihadism "Salafi jihadism") group called "[Hayat Ansar al-Tawhid al-Islamiyah](/wiki/Hayat_Ansar_al-Tawhid_al-Islamiyah "Hayat Ansar al-Tawhid al-Islamiyah")" claimed responsibility for an attack with 8 Grad rockets launched from [Daraa](/wiki/Daraa "Daraa") on the [Israeli-occupied](/wiki/Israeli-occupied_territories "Israeli-occupied territories") [Golan Heights](/wiki/Golan_Heights "Golan Heights").[[19]](#cite_note-19)[[20]](#cite_note-20) Four people were injured after a missile fragment fell in [Ein Tarma](/wiki/Ein_Tarma "Ein Tarma").[[21]](#cite_note-21)

### March 2

The group "[Ajnad Bayt al-Maqdis](/wiki/Ajnad_Bayt_al-Maqdis "Ajnad Bayt al-Maqdis")" claimed responsibility for an attack on a base in [al-Shaddadi](/wiki/Al-Shaddadi "Al-Shaddadi") with two 107mm Katyusha rockets, the group formerly declared loyalty to [Al-Qaeda](/wiki/Al-Qaeda "Al-Qaeda"),[[22]](#cite_note-22) the group stated that the action was carried out "in response to the recent Zio-Crusader campaign led by the criminal Trump and the child-killer Netanyahu against the Islamic Ummah", Ajnad Bayt al-Maqdis declared having activity in Iraq and Syria,[[23]](#cite_note-23) according to the group, the attack was carried out by one of its brigades, "Jund al-Aqsa".[[24]](#cite_note-24) A intercepted missile fell in Al-Fatih, [Jableh](/wiki/Jableh_District "Jableh District") countryside.[[25]](#cite_note-25)

### March 4

A missile heading towards Turkey was intercepted by NATO nearby [Kurdish-controlled](/wiki/Syrian_Democratic_Forces "Syrian Democratic Forces") [Qamishli International Airport](/wiki/Qamishli_International_Airport "Qamishli International Airport"),[[26]](#cite_note-26)[[27]](#cite_note-27)[[28]](#cite_note-28) leading to the missile to land in an empty field where curious locals gathered around the missile,[[29]](#cite_note-29) the missile failed to explode.[[30]](#cite_note-30)
Israel carried out an incursion into southern [Syria](/wiki/Syria "Syria"), shelling the area between [Jamla](/wiki/Jamla "Jamla") and Saisoun in [Daraa Governorate](/wiki/Daraa_Governorate "Daraa Governorate"), and arrested four civilians. Israel accused Syria of deploying combat units to the Syrian [Golan Heights](/wiki/Golan_Heights "Golan Heights"), stating it will not allow the Hezbollah–Israel strikes to be used as a pretext to "[harm the Druze](/wiki/Druze_insurgency_in_Southern_Syria "Druze insurgency in Southern Syria")" in southern Syria and demanded the Syrian government prevent [Iraqi militias](/wiki/Private_militias_in_Iraq "Private militias in Iraq") from crossing Syrian territory towards the Golan Heights.[[31]](#cite_note-31)[[32]](#cite_note-32)

### March 8

A Syrian pro-Iranian group called [Kataib Jund al-Karrar](/wiki/Kataib_Jund_al-Karrar "Kataib Jund al-Karrar"), claimed attacks in [Palmyra](/wiki/Palmyra "Palmyra"), attacks against Israeli targets in the [Golan Heights](/wiki/Golan_Heights "Golan Heights"),[[33]](#cite_note-33) and an attack in the Tower 22, a military base in [Jordan](/wiki/Jordan "Jordan").[[34]](#cite_note-34)

### March 10

Following the Syrian presidency's support for the disarmament of [Hezbollah](/wiki/Hezbollah "Hezbollah") and a campaign of mobilization by the Syrian army on the Lebanese border,[[35]](#cite_note-35)[[36]](#cite_note-36) shells from Lebanon landed in the town of [Serghaya](/wiki/Serghaya "Serghaya"), 20 km (12 mi) from Damascus where the [Syrian Armed Forces](/wiki/Syrian_Armed_Forces "Syrian Armed Forces") forces presided.[[37]](#cite_note-37)[[38]](#cite_note-38) The Syrian government accused Hezbollah of targeting its positions there,[[39]](#cite_note-39) amid what they said was a buildup of Hezbollah forces along the border.[[40]](#cite_note-40)

### March 14

The [Syrian forces](/wiki/Syrian_Army "Syrian Army") removed mines along the border with [Lebanon](/wiki/Lebanon "Lebanon") in the Wadi Khaled area, in anticipation of a possible clashes with [Hezbollah](/wiki/Hezbollah "Hezbollah").[[41]](#cite_note-41)

### March 19

Rockets were launched from Syria towards Israel and [IDF positions in the Syrian Golan Heights](/wiki/Israeli_invasion_of_Syria_(2024%E2%80%93present) "Israeli invasion of Syria (2024–present)"); the missiles landed in open areas, without resulting in damage.[[42]](#cite_note-42)

### March 20

Israel bombed southern Syria, saying the strikes were in response to "[reports of attacks against Druze civilians](/wiki/Druze_insurgency_in_Southern_Syria "Druze insurgency in Southern Syria")" in [Suwayda Governorate](/wiki/Suwayda_Governorate "Suwayda Governorate").[[43]](#cite_note-43) The airstrikes also hit several Syrian government positions in [Izraa](/wiki/Izraa "Izraa") in Daraa Governorate, including command and control centres, supply depots, and facilities held by the 40th Battalion of the Syrian Army, in retaliation to the escalation of hostilities.[[44]](#cite_note-44)[[45]](#cite_note-45)

### March 23

The [Islamic Resistance in Iraq](/wiki/Islamic_Resistance_in_Iraq "Islamic Resistance in Iraq") claimed responsibility for an attack on the [Kharab al-Jir base](/wiki/Kharab_al-Jir_base "Kharab al-Jir base") in [Hasakah](/wiki/Hasakah "Hasakah"); according to local sources, the drones were launched from Iraqi territory and belonged to [pro-Iranian militias](/wiki/Private_militias_in_Iraq "Private militias in Iraq"),[[46]](#cite_note-46) at least six drones attacked the base, and the extent of the damage is unknown,[[47]](#cite_note-47)[[48]](#cite_note-48) the missiles were launched from [Tal Al-Hawa](/wiki/Tell_al-Hawa "Tell al-Hawa"), and Syrian forces are coordinating with Iraqi police to arrest those involved,[[49]](#cite_note-49)[[50]](#cite_note-50) according to Wael Alwan, it was in response to the arrest of a [Popular Mobilization Forces](/wiki/Popular_Mobilization_Forces "Popular Mobilization Forces") cell by Syrian security forces in [Deir ez-Zor](/wiki/Deir_ez-Zor "Deir ez-Zor").[[51]](#cite_note-51) During the same day 30 Israeli soldiers carried out incursions in [Samdaniyah al-Sharqiyah](/wiki/Samadanieh_al-Sharqiyeh "Samadanieh al-Sharqiyeh") and advanced toward the [Ruwaihina Dam](/w/index.php?title=Ruwaihina_Dam&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ruwaihina Dam (page does not exist)") in the northern countryside of Quneitra province.[[52]](#cite_note-52)[[53]](#cite_note-53)

### March 28

Missiles originating from Iraq were intercepted over the [Al-Tanf base](/wiki/Al-Tanf_base "Al-Tanf base"), according to the Syrian Army's Directorate of Operations.[[54]](#cite_note-54)[[55]](#cite_note-55) Kataib Jund al-Karrar claimed an attack towards the [Israeli-occupied Golan Heights](/wiki/Israeli_occupation_of_the_Golan_Heights "Israeli occupation of the Golan Heights").[[56]](#cite_note-56)

### March 29

Four missiles launched from Iraq, fell at the US base of [Qasrok](/wiki/Qasrok,_Syria "Qasrok, Syria"), the vice president of the [Syrian Ministry of Defense](/wiki/Ministry_of_Defense_(Syria) "Ministry of Defense (Syria)"), [Sipan Hamo](/wiki/Sipan_Hamo "Sipan Hamo"), stated: "We urge Iraq to prevent a recurrence of these attacks, which threaten regional stability".[[57]](#cite_note-57) Syrian forces were the ones that intercepted the missiles.[[58]](#cite_note-58) The Israeli army led an incursion into Lebanon from the Syrian side of [Jabal al-Sheikh](/wiki/Mount_Hermon "Mount Hermon"), towards the Mount Dov area, in southern Lebanon.[[59]](#cite_note-59)

### March 30

Kataib Jund al-Karrar claimed an attack on [Palmyra Airport](/wiki/Tiyas_Air_Base "Tiyas Air Base"), with a barrage of missiles that achieved a direct hit, against the [US presence in Syria](/wiki/United_States_intervention_in_Syria "United States intervention in Syria"),[[60]](#cite_note-60) also claimed responsibility for a drone attack on the [Muwaffaq Salti Air Base](/wiki/Muwaffaq_Salti_Air_Base "Muwaffaq Salti Air Base") in Jordan.[[61]](#cite_note-61)

### April 2

Protests took place in the [Quneitra Governorate](/wiki/Quneitra_Governorate "Quneitra Governorate"), where dozens of young people marched in cars and motorcycles to protest against Israel and to protest against Israeli practices against Palestinian prisoners,[[62]](#cite_note-62) the protesters called for the bombing of [Tel Aviv](/wiki/Tel_Aviv "Tel Aviv") and [Haifa](/wiki/Haifa "Haifa"), also chanted the slogan, “[Khaibar, Khaibar, oh Jews, the army of Muhammad will return!](/wiki/Khaybar_Khaybar_ya_yahud "Khaybar Khaybar ya yahud")",[[63]](#cite_note-63) protests also took place in the [Daraa Governorate](/wiki/Daraa_Governorate "Daraa Governorate"), in Sanamayn, al-Hirak, Nasib, Nawa, Jasim, Inkhil, al-Harra, Sheikh Miskin, Tafas, Jillin, Qarfa y Namer,[[64]](#cite_note-64) the Bedouin tribes in Daraa announced a general mobilization against Israel, arriving the southern Syria, near the border with Israel.[[65]](#cite_note-65)

Reactions
---------

[Saudi Arabia](/wiki/Saudi_Arabia "Saudi Arabia"), [Qatar](/wiki/Qatar "Qatar"), [Egypt](/wiki/Egypt "Egypt"), and [Turkey](/wiki/Turkey "Turkey") encouraged [Syria](/wiki/Syria "Syria") to maintain its neutral stance. These countries also intervened with the [United States](/wiki/United_States "United States") to ease pressure on Syrian president [Ahmed al-Sharaa](/wiki/Ahmed_al-Sharaa "Ahmed al-Sharaa"), who has made it clear that he does not wish to become involved in Lebanon.[[66]](#cite_note-66)

Syrian assistant defense minister Sîpan Hemo condemned at attack on the US base in [Qasrok](/wiki/Qasrok,_Syria "Qasrok, Syria").[[67]](#cite_note-67)

Impact
------

### Gas shortage

On February 28, [Israel](/wiki/Israel "Israel") suspended natural gas exports to [Egypt](/wiki/Egypt "Egypt"), prompting Egypt to reduce its exports to Syria via [Jordan](/wiki/Jordan "Jordan").[[68]](#cite_note-68) According to Safwan Ahmad, the large-scale smuggling of gas cylinders from Syria into Lebanon was one of the factors contributing to the shortage.[[69]](#cite_note-69)

### Syrian remigration

According to the UN refugee agency the total population of Syrian refugees in the entirety of Lebanon was 1.4 million people in March 2026.[[70]](#cite_note-70)

Due to the [2026 Lebanon war](/wiki/2026_Lebanon_war "2026 Lebanon war") thousands of individuals of Syrian heritage remigrated back in Syria, especially by using the Joussieh border crossing.[[71]](#cite_note-71) A report dated 13 March 2026 by [Intersos](/wiki/Intersos "Intersos") reported that other local agencies had recorded back on 10 March 2026 that 85,000–90,000 people had crossed the main legal border crossings from Lebanon to Syria. The demographic of these refugees was composed by 93% of Syrian returnees and 7% by Lebanese citizens.[[72]](#cite_note-72) By 18 March 2026, only counting the Syrians, 119,000 people had crossed the border from Lebanon into Syria according to UN agency's Displacement Tracking Matrix.[[73]](#cite_note-73) The people that had crossed the border in total by then were 125,000 people.[[74]](#cite_note-74)

Threats to civilian and administrative sites
--------------------------------------------

On 27 March 2026, Iran threatened to strike three sites which allegedly "hosted" or "supported" U.S. units in the region, such as the [Four Seasons Hotel](/wiki/Four_Seasons_Hotel_Damascus "Four Seasons Hotel Damascus"), the Sheraton Damascus Hotel and the [People's Palace](/wiki/People%27s_Palace_(Syria) "People's Palace (Syria)").[[75]](#cite_note-75)

U.S. push for entry in the war
------------------------------

Syria was pressured to militarily intervene in Lebanon by the United States by sending its troops and striking positions in Lebanon alongside the IDF in order to further weaken Hezbollah,[[76]](#cite_note-76) however the Syrian government refused the offer on the same day Israel Katz declared a ground invasion of southern Lebanon.[[77]](#cite_note-77) [Saudi Arabia](/wiki/Saudi_Arabia "Saudi Arabia"), [Qatar](/wiki/Qatar "Qatar"), [Egypt](/wiki/Egypt "Egypt"), and [Turkey](/wiki/Turkey "Turkey") discouraged a confrontation between Syria and Hezbollah.[[78]](#cite_note-78) Syrian [Foreign Affairs Minister](/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_and_Expatriates_(Syria) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Expatriates (Syria)") [Asaad al-Shaibani](/wiki/Asaad_al-Shaibani "Asaad al-Shaibani") stated that Syria had no intentions to intervene directly in Lebanon.[[79]](#cite_note-79)
