# Iran judiciary chief calls to fast-track cases tied to ‘enemy collaboration’

7 April 2026 07:20 BST

![](/sites/default/files/styles/max_2600x2600/public/2026-04/000_A6XH2EW.jpg.jpg?itok=az6vTcg6)

Iran’s judiciary chief has called for swift prosecutions against those accused of aiding the country’s adversaries, as arrests continue following ongoing US-Israeli strikes.

Speaking on Tuesday, Gholamhossein Mohseni Ejei said cases involving individuals accused of assisting hostile actors should be handled more rapidly, according to Mizan news. He urged authorities to accelerate verdicts, including executions or the confiscation of assets.

Since the launch of US-Israeli attacks last month, security forces have detained hundreds of people on accusations of collaboration.
