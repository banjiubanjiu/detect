# Macron rallies 15 nations to reopen Strait of Hormuz

April 8, 2026, 10:51 AM

![French President Emmanuel Macron (Photo: REUTERS/Yves Herman)](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/271/109/original/bc3cc32c95841ddf8d7067be17c8e275.png?q=85&stamp=20260408135219&w=900&f=webp)

French President Emmanuel Macron (Photo: REUTERS/Yves Herman)

Around 15 countries are preparing to help restore safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz following a ceasefire between the United States and Iran, French President Emmanuel Macron announced on April 8, according to [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/iran-war-live-trump-announces-two-week-ceasefire-iran-says-talks-begin-soon-2026-04-08/).

Speaking at a defense and security council meeting, Macron said France would lead a defensive mission to reopen the vital shipping corridor.

"About 15 countries are currently mobilized and are participating in the planning, under France's leadership, to enable the implementation of this strictly defensive mission in coordination with Iran to facilitate the resumption of traffic," he said.
