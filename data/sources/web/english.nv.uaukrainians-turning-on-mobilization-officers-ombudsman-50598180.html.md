# Civilians turning on mobilization officers, ombudsman warns

April 6, 2026, 10:19 PM

![National Police](https://static.nv.ua/shared/system/Article/posters/003/270/036/original/de1b0c2f496ffe5e650235ab9eda3186.png?q=85&stamp=20260407005653&w=900&f=webp)

National Police

Attacks on military officers carrying out [mobilization](https://english.nv.ua/nation/budanov-coercive-mobilization-necessary-in-wartime-50597549.html) and draft across Ukraine have become systemic, military ombudsman Olha Reshetylova told NV Radio on April 6.

Reshetylova said her office does not keep statistics on civilian attacks against the Territorial Recruitment Centers, but the trend has become apparent from media reports alone.

“We can see a trend from media reports and from the communications we have with servicemembers — these attacks have become regular,” Reshetylova said.

“From our contacts with servicemembers at the territorial centers, we understand that aggression from people avoiding military service is growing toward those servicemembers. This is becoming a major problem.”

On **April 6**, the Kharkiv regional recruitment center said that a civilian attacked and stabbed officers inspecting military registration of eligible citizens.

**On April 4**, the Vinnytsia center said two servicemembers were stabbed during mobilization activities. According to the center, servicemembers stopped a man, identified themselves and asked to check his documents when he suddenly pulled a knife and stabbed several times.

**On April 2**, a 52-year-old recruitment center employee was fatally stabbed in the neck in Lviv.
