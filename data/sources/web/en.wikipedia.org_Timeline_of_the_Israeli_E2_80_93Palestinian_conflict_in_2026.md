The following is a list of events during the [Israeli–Palestinian conflict](/wiki/Israeli%E2%80%93Palestinian_conflict "Israeli–Palestinian conflict") in 2026, including the events of the [Gaza war](/wiki/Gaza_war "Gaza war").

January
-------

### 1 January

* Israel orders the banning effective 1 March of 37 humanitarian agencies from operating in the [Gaza Strip](/wiki/Gaza_Strip "Gaza Strip") for failing to comply with [its revised regulations](/wiki/Guidelines_for_the_Registration_of_Organizations "Guidelines for the Registration of Organizations") on the disclosure of detailed information on their Palestinian staff.[[1]](#cite_note-1)
* Israeli forces arrested 50 Palestinians and demolished homes following operations involving alleged abuse and property destruction in the [West Bank](/wiki/West_Bank "West Bank").[[2]](#cite_note-2)[[3]](#cite_note-3)

### 4 January

* Israeli forces killed three Palestinians in [Khan Younis](/wiki/Khan_Younis "Khan Younis"), Gaza.[[4]](#cite_note-4)

### 5 January

* The [Palestinian embassy in the United Kingdom](/wiki/Palestinian_Mission,_London "Palestinian Mission, London") officially opens in London, following the United Kingdom's diplomatic recognition of Palestine in September 2025.[[5]](#cite_note-5)

### 6 January

* An Israeli assault on [Birzeit University](/wiki/Birzeit_University "Birzeit University") in the West Bank injures dozens of people.[[6]](#cite_note-6)
* In the West Bank, where [communications control](/wiki/Communications_in_Palestine "Communications in Palestine") is still in effect, Israel approves the expansion of [4G](/wiki/4G "4G") mobile services for Palestinians.[[7]](#cite_note-7)[[8]](#cite_note-8)

### 7 January

* Israeli forces killed two Palestinians in Gaza.[[9]](#cite_note-9)[[10]](#cite_note-10)
* Israeli forces killed two people in Southern Lebanon.[[11]](#cite_note-11)

### 8 January

* Israeli forces killed an eleven-year-old Palestinian girl in [Jabalia refugee camp](/wiki/Jabalia_refugee_camp "Jabalia refugee camp") in Gaza.[[12]](#cite_note-12)[[13]](#cite_note-13)
* Spanish Prime Minister [Pedro Sanchez](/wiki/Pedro_Sanchez "Pedro Sanchez") said he is open to sending peacekeeping troops to Palestine.[[14]](#cite_note-14)
* Somalia accuses Israel of planning a relocation of Palestinians to [Somaliland](/wiki/Somaliland "Somaliland").[[15]](#cite_note-15)[[16]](#cite_note-16)
* Israeli strikes killed at least thirteen people, including at least four children, in Gaza.[[17]](#cite_note-17)[[18]](#cite_note-18)[[19]](#cite_note-19)[[20]](#cite_note-20)
* One person was killed in an Israeli drone strike in southern Lebanon.[[21]](#cite_note-21)[[22]](#cite_note-22)

### 9 January

* Israeli forces killed at least three people in an overnight strike in Gaza.[[23]](#cite_note-23)[[24]](#cite_note-24)[[25]](#cite_note-25)

### 10 January

* Israeli settlers beat a 67-year-old deaf Palestinian man in [Deir Sharaf](/wiki/Deir_Sharaf "Deir Sharaf"),[[26]](#cite_note-26)[[27]](#cite_note-27) as settler violence forces entire Palestinian communities to flee from their homes.[[28]](#cite_note-28)[[29]](#cite_note-29)

### 11 January

* Israeli forces killed a Palestinian in [Hebron](/wiki/Hebron "Hebron") during raids in the West Bank.[[30]](#cite_note-30)[[31]](#cite_note-31)
* A Palestinian baby dies of hypothermia in Gaza.[[32]](#cite_note-32)
* Israeli forces kill one person in southern Lebanon.[[33]](#cite_note-33)

### 12 January

* Three Palestinians were killed by an Israeli quadcopter drone in Khan Younis.[[34]](#cite_note-34)
* [CSF](/wiki/Counter-Terrorism_Strike_Force "Counter-Terrorism Strike Force") leader Hussam Al-Astal claims responsibility for killing a senior Hamas police officer in Khan Younis, Gaza.[[35]](#cite_note-35)

### 13 January

* At least five people die in Gaza from collapsed buildings and extreme cold.[[36]](#cite_note-36)[[37]](#cite_note-37)

### 14 January

* The United States announces the second phase of the Gaza ceasefire plan.[[38]](#cite_note-38)[[39]](#cite_note-39)[[40]](#cite_note-40)

### 15 January

* Israeli strikes[[41]](#cite_note-41) kill ten people in Gaza, including a minor, along with Hamas and [Palestinian Islamic Jihad](/wiki/Palestinian_Islamic_Jihad "Palestinian Islamic Jihad") commanders.[[42]](#cite_note-42)[[43]](#cite_note-43)[[44]](#cite_note-44)
* It is announced that Palestinian President [Mahmoud Abbas](/wiki/Mahmoud_Abbas "Mahmoud Abbas") will meet Russian President [Vladimir Putin](/wiki/Vladimir_Putin "Vladimir Putin") during a visit to Moscow on 21 January.[[45]](#cite_note-45)

### 16 January

* The Palestinian committee that will govern Gaza under U.S. supervision holds its initial meeting in Cairo.[[46]](#cite_note-46)[[47]](#cite_note-47)[[48]](#cite_note-48)
* The IDF kills a Palestinian child in [Al-Mughayyir, Ramallah](/wiki/Al-Mughayyir,_Ramallah "Al-Mughayyir, Ramallah"), West Bank.[[49]](#cite_note-49)

### 19 January

* Three Palestinians were killed in Gaza, and others were injured.[[50]](#cite_note-50)

### 20 January

* A 3-month-old baby dies of hypothermia in [Daraj Quarter](/wiki/Daraj_Quarter "Daraj Quarter"), in Gaza City.[[51]](#cite_note-51)[[52]](#cite_note-52)
* Israel bulldozes [UNRWA](/wiki/UNRWA "UNRWA") headquarters in [East Jerusalem](/wiki/East_Jerusalem "East Jerusalem"),[[53]](#cite_note-53)[[54]](#cite_note-54) despite the United Nations' condemnation.[[55]](#cite_note-55)[[56]](#cite_note-56)

### 21 January

* Israeli strikes in Gaza killed at least 11 people, including two children, three journalists and a woman.[[57]](#cite_note-57)[[58]](#cite_note-58) The IDF claimed one of the strikes targeted suspects who were operating a drone that posed a threat.[[59]](#cite_note-59)
* The IDF carried out airstrikes on the Syria–Lebanon border, which Israel said were targeted at sites used to smuggle weapons to Hezbollah, killing two people.[[60]](#cite_note-60)
* The [Minister of National Security](/wiki/Ministry_of_National_Security_(Israel) "Ministry of National Security (Israel)") [Itamar Ben-Gvir](/wiki/Itamar_Ben-Gvir "Itamar Ben-Gvir") approved gun licences for 18 illegal settlements in the West Bank in order to "enhance self-defence and increase personal security".[[61]](#cite_note-61)

### 22 January

* US President [Donald Trump](/wiki/Donald_Trump "Donald Trump") launches the [Board of Peace](/wiki/Board_of_Peace "Board of Peace") in [Davos](/wiki/Davos "Davos"), Switzerland.[[62]](#cite_note-62)
* It is announce that the [Rafah Border Crossing](/wiki/Rafah_Border_Crossing "Rafah Border Crossing") will fully resume operations next week, according to the [National Committee for the Administration of Gaza](/wiki/National_Committee_for_the_Administration_of_Gaza "National Committee for the Administration of Gaza").[[63]](#cite_note-63)
* Russian President [Vladimir Putin](/wiki/Vladimir_Putin "Vladimir Putin") holds talks with American envoys and Palestinian President [Mahmoud Abbas](/wiki/Mahmoud_Abbas "Mahmoud Abbas") in Moscow.[[64]](#cite_note-64)
* The IDF announces that a soldier died from wounds sustained during fighting in southern Gaza in October 2025.[[65]](#cite_note-65)

### 26 January

* The IDF announced that the remains of the final Israeli hostage, Ran Gvili, was recovered in Gaza.[[66]](#cite_note-66)

### 28 January

* [Israeli settlers](/wiki/Israeli_settlement "Israeli settlement") set fire to Palestinian property in Khallat al-Sidra, a [Bedouin](/wiki/Bedouin "Bedouin") community northeast of [East Jerusalem](/wiki/East_Jerusalem "East Jerusalem"), with Israeli forces stormed the area shortly after the attack.[[67]](#cite_note-67)

### 31 January

* Israeli forces launched several airstrikes into the Gaza Strip, killing at least 32 people, including seven children and injuring at least 30 others.[[68]](#cite_note-68)[[69]](#cite_note-69) One of the strikes targeted a police station in [Sheikh Radwan](/wiki/Sheikh_Radwan "Sheikh Radwan"), where seven people were killed, including four female police officers.[[70]](#cite_note-70) The IDF claimed that the strikes targeted several commanders of [Hamas](/wiki/Hamas "Hamas") and the [Palestinian Islamic Jihad](/wiki/Palestinian_Islamic_Jihad "Palestinian Islamic Jihad"), as well as a weapons depot, an arms manufacturing site and two rocket launching positions, which was considered as a "violation of the ceasefire agreement".[[71]](#cite_note-71)

February
--------

### 3 February

* A 24-year-old Palestinian man named Saeed Nael Saeed al-Sheikh was killed by Israeli forces and injuring three others in [Jericho](/wiki/Jericho "Jericho").[[72]](#cite_note-72) According to the IDF, the incident was a response after several Palestinians threw rocks at the soldiers during a raid in the city.[[73]](#cite_note-73)

### 10 February

* Two Palestinians were killed by an Israeli drone strike while cycling in eastern [Deir al-Balah](/wiki/Deir_al-Balah "Deir al-Balah") near the ceasefire line, according to the  [Al-Aqsa Martyrs Hospital](/wiki/Shuhada_al-Aqsa_Hospital "Shuhada al-Aqsa Hospital").[[74]](#cite_note-74)

### 12 February

* The [Commission of Detainees and Ex-Detainees Affairs and the Palestinian Prisoners' Society](/wiki/Ministry_of_Detainees_and_Ex-Detainees_Affairs "Ministry of Detainees and Ex-Detainees Affairs") announced that a Palestinian paramedic, Hatem Ismail Rayyan, who was arrested by Israeli forces at Kamal Adwan Hospital in Gaza on 27 December 2024, was pronounced dead under Israeli custody.[[75]](#cite_note-75)[[76]](#cite_note-76)

### 13 February

* Several Palestinians were injured after Israeli settlers attacked farmers near [Talfit](/wiki/Talfit "Talfit") before Israeli forces fired tear gas and live ammunition at residents who tried to stop the settler attack.[[77]](#cite_note-injure-77) Israeli settlers also destroyed about 300 Palestinian olive trees near [Turmus Ayya](/wiki/Turmus_Ayya "Turmus Ayya").[[77]](#cite_note-injure-77)

### 18 February

* Palestinian-American teenager Nasrallah Abu Siyam was shot dead by Israeli settlers in the West Bank village of [Mukhmas](/wiki/Mukhmas "Mukhmas"), making him the second Palestinian-American to have been killed by settlers in less than a year. The settlers also attacked a farmer and injured several other Palestinians. A funeral for Abu Siyam was held the following day.[[78]](#cite_note-78)[[79]](#cite_note-79)

### 23 February

* The Ministry of Religious Affairs of the Palestinian Authority reported that the Abu Bakr Al-Siddiq Mosque in [Tell, Nablus](/wiki/Tell,_Nablus "Tell, Nablus") was vandalized by Israeli settlers, spray-painted with offensive phrases insulting the prophet [Muhammad](/wiki/Muhammad "Muhammad"), words "revenge" and "[price tag](/wiki/Price_tag_attack_policy "Price tag attack policy")" as well as setting the mosque on fire.[[80]](#cite_note-80)

### 27 February

* Israeli drones targeted a police checkpoint at the al-Maslakh intersection in [Al-Mawasi](/wiki/Al-Mawasi,_Rafah "Al-Mawasi, Rafah"), killing four people and injuring several others.[[81]](#cite_note-sites-81) That same day, an Israeli drone targeted another police post near the entrance of the [Bureij refugee camp](/wiki/Bureij "Bureij"), killing two people.[[81]](#cite_note-sites-81)

March
-----

### 8 March

* Two Palestinians were killed in an attack by Israeli settlers in [Khirbet Abu Falah](/wiki/Khirbet_Abu_Falah "Khirbet Abu Falah") and another resident later died from fumes of a tear gas canister fired by Israeli soldiers who accompanied the settlers to disperse the residents.[[82]](#cite_note-82) All three of them were later buried in a joint funeral.[[83]](#cite_note-83)

### 12 March

* A mosque in the village of [Duma](/wiki/Duma,_Nablus "Duma, Nablus") was vandalised and attempted to set on fire by Israeli settlers.[[84]](#cite_note-84)

### 14 March

* A 28-year-old man named Amir Moatasem Odeh was killed while two others were injured by Israeli settlers in the Palestinian village of [Qusra](/wiki/Qusra "Qusra").[[85]](#cite_note-85)
* *[Wafa](/wiki/Wafa "Wafa")* reported that five Palestinians were injured after Israeli settlers attacked them in Rashayda, near the village of [Kisan](/wiki/Kisan,_Palestine "Kisan, Palestine") after "firing live ammunition" as well as stole 100 sheep.[[86]](#cite_note-86)
* Israeli police opened fire on a Palestinian family after a Ramadan shopping trip through [Tammun](/wiki/Tammun "Tammun"), killing the parents and two of their children.[[87]](#cite_note-87) Two of their other children were also injured.[[88]](#cite_note-88) Israeli police later stated that "a vehicle accelerated toward the forces, which felt endangered and opened fire."[[89]](#cite_note-89)

### 15 March

* The IDF announced that the military targeted several Hamas operatives, including two who were involved in the [October 7 attacks](/wiki/October_7_attacks "October 7 attacks"), stating that the gunmen were preparing to carry out an attack on Israeli forces in Gaza.[[90]](#cite_note-90)
* A senior police official and eight others were killed by an Israeli airstrike in [Zawayda](/wiki/Az-Zawayda "Az-Zawayda") and 14 others were injured.[[91]](#cite_note-reuters-91) That same day, an Israeli airstrike targeted western [Nuseirat](/wiki/Nuseirat_refugee_camp "Nuseirat refugee camp"), killing a man, his pregnant wife and his son, according to health officials.[[91]](#cite_note-reuters-91) A 15-year-old child in the refugee camp also later died after taken to [Al-Awda Hospital](/wiki/Al-Awda_Hospital "Al-Awda Hospital").[[92]](#cite_note-92)

### 28 March

* Israeli forces launched several airstrikes into the Gaza Strip, killing one in Khan Yunis and two more in [Shuja'iyya](/wiki/Shuja%27iyya "Shuja'iyya"), according to health officials.[[93]](#cite_note-93)
