Russian Force Generation Update: The Russian military is experiencing manpower challenges and is unable to recruit enough contract soldiers to replace its frontline losses.

The Russian MoD is attempting to generate additional manpower through other sources, regardless of the financial and societal costs.

Russian losses may be even higher than the Ukrainian General Staff data indicates.

Russia is facing increasing recruitment challenges as the pool of Russians willing to sign contracts declines, despite the Kremlin's efforts to increase signing bonuses and tap additional sources of manpower.

ISW previously forecasted in February 2025 that Russia would likely face a number of manpower issues in 12 to 18 months if Ukrainian forces continued to inflict casualties on Russia at the then-prevailing rate, which they have done.

Russian President Vladimir Putin likely continues to desire to offset the significant backlash that any future limited involuntary reserve callup would cause and is likely reluctant to increase financial incentives for new recruits dramatically out of concern for the federal budget, any benefits that the Kremlin is gaining from increased oil prices due to the ongoing war in the Middle East and the United States’ partial lifting of sanctions on Russia notwithstanding.
