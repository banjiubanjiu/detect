🇺🇸 🇨🇳 U.S. STATE DEPARTMENT CALLS OUT CHINA'S TAIWAN DRILLS: RESTRAINT AND DIALOGUE NEEDED TO COOL TENSIONS

The US State Department kicked off 2026 with a sharp statement slamming China's latest military muscle-flexing around Taiwan, saying the drills and tough talk "increase tensions unnecessarily." 

Principal Deputy Spokesperson Thomas "Tommy" Pigott urged Beijing to back off the military pressure, stop the coercion, and sit down for real dialogue instead.

This comes right after China's massive "Justice Mission 2025" exercises, dozens of warships, jets, coast guard vessels encircling Taiwan, missile launches, live-fire drills simulating a blockade. 

Beijing tied it to a fresh $11 billion U.S. arms package for Taipei and warnings against "separatist" moves.

Taiwan's President Lai Ching-te fired back in his New Year's speech, vowing to defend sovereignty no matter what. 

The US stuck to its line: peace and stability across the Strait, no unilateral changes by force. 

Tensions are sky-high heading into the new year, with everyone watching if words turn into de-escalation or more fireworks.
