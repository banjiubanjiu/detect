Instability in Syria — Monthly Overview (January 2026):
The ceasefire with the Kurdish forces does not herald stability, but rather signals a profound shift in the country’s power dynamics. The United States has chosen to gamble on the regime in Damascus as a partner, even at the cost of an almost complete erosion of the Kurds’ standing. At the same time, the Syrian arena continues to be marked by multi-layered instability: ongoing internal unrest, violent clashes with the Druze, terrorist activity, and a lack of governmental transparency. The absence of security reform, continued harm to civilians, and reliance on military arrangements instead of political ones undermine the prospects for long-term stability.
