🚨 New threats are emerging against the United States and its allies from Yemen, as the Houthi group announced it is considering closing the Bab al-Mandeb Strait, a vital waterway connecting the Red Sea to the Gulf of Aden. This move is seen as a form of direct support for Iran, which is currently in conflict with the U.S. and Israel.

Houthi Political Bureau member Mohammed al-Bukhaiti made this statement on March 20, 2026, emphasizing the need to explore all scenarios to strengthen Iran’s position amid the ongoing conflict. Houthi leader Abdul-Malik al-Houthi also stressed their readiness to act and coordinate directly with Iran.

Details of the threat and current situation:

Target scope: The Houthis clarified that, if enforced, the blockade would affect only ships belonging to countries participating in the “aggression” against Iran, Lebanon, Palestine, and Iraq.

Historical context: The group has issued similar threats since 2015 but never carried them out. During the Gaza conflict in October 2023, they limited themselves to drone and missile attacks on vessels linked to Israel and the U.S.

Military response: Houthi attacks have previously triggered hundreds of airstrikes from the U.S. and U.K., as well as repeated Israeli strikes targeting their missile sites and ports.

Current escalation: Since the war erupted on February 28, 2026, other Iranian-backed groups like Hezbollah and Iraqi militias have already launched attacks, while the Houthis remain in the threat and preparation phase, signaling that escalation could occur at any moment.

Potential economic impact of closing the strait:
If the blockade is implemented, global economic consequences would be severe. Oil tankers and commercial ships would be forced to reroute around Africa, significantly increasing travel time and shipping costs. This would lead to higher fuel prices and rising global inflation, compounding the energy crisis triggered by Iran’s previous closure of the Strait of Hormuz.
