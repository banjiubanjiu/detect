Ukraine said it hit Russia’s key Baltic port of Ust-Luga, the latest in a spate of attacks on the country’s oil-export infrastructure as the war between the two nations shows no signs of easing.

The overnight attack on Ust-Luga Oil’s terminal — which loads petroleum products — hit three reservoir tanks belonging to Transneft-Baltika, Ukraine’s General Staff said in a Telegram statement. The scale of damage is being clarified, it said. The attack happened just as Ust-Luga resumed crude loadings over weekend after days of disruptions amid attacks in the region.
